Academic Dilemma in the Digital Age

Academic Dilemma in the Digital Age: Alienation, Gap, and Integrity Crisis

In the rapid wave of digital technology development, the academic world is facing unprecedented challenges and crises. Once regarded as an "ivory tower," the sanctum of knowledge is now overshadowed by the digital wave, plunging into a predicament like never before.

Academic Alienation: From Pursuing Truth to Chasing Numbers

The essence of academic research is exploring truth and seeking knowledge, but in today's digital age, this sacred mission is being distorted by cold, hard digital metrics. Scholars are no longer focusing on deep thinking and innovation but are forced to dissect academic research into fragmented journal publications, with chasing the number of papers becoming their primary task.

Digital Gap: A New Barrier to Academic Equity

As the digital wave sweeps across the globe, a subtle and dangerous fracture is quietly expanding in the academic world—this is the digital gap. This gap not only exists in geographical space but is also deeply embedded in disciplines and academic communities, becoming a significant obstacle to the progress of academic equity.

Underground Economy: Shadows Eroding Academic Integrity

Behind the brilliance of the academic world, an unseen "shadow network" is quietly weaving. This is an underground economic system outside the formal academic system, encompassing various academic misconduct such as ghostwriting, data fabrication, and experiment outsourcing.

Reconstructing the Academic Ecosystem: Challenges and Opportunities

To break the academic dilemma in the digital age, systemic reforms are essential. First, we must break the digital shackles of the academic evaluation system and establish a diversified academic evaluation system, eliminating the single standard of "thesis-centered" evaluation.
