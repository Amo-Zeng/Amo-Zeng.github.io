Psychological Time Lag: The Art of Perception and Design

Definition
Psychological Time Lag refers to the phenomenon where the perceived waiting time differs from the actual time. This perception discrepancy can be an acceleration or deceleration, primarily influenced by emotional states and environmental factors.

Psychological Theories
Studies have shown that emotions like anxiety and anticipation significantly affect time perception. Anxiety can make time feel slower (time dilation), while anticipation or pleasure can make time feel faster (time compression). The allocation of attention also plays a crucial role in time perception.
