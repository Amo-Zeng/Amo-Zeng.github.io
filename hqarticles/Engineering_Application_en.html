The Three Paradigm Revolutions in Engineering Applications

From Brain-Inspired Sensing to Co-Design and Active Materials

Introduction: New Challenges and Paradigm Shifts in Engineering Systems

Engineering systems in the 21st century stand at a historic turning point. Accelerating global climate change, increasing frequency of extreme weather events, deep energy transitions, and aging infrastructure have rendered traditional engineering paradigms—reliant on centralized computing, static design, and passive materials—increasingly inadequate in addressing the triple challenge of high uncertainty, high sustainability requirements, and high operational costs. For example, conventional bridge health monitoring systems, due to communication latency and high false alarm rates, often miss early warning windows; urban drainage systems, designed without accounting for a threefold increase in 100-year storm frequency (IPCC AR6), frequently collapse during extreme rainfall; and once concrete cracks, it typically requires manual repair, resulting in high carbon footprints and maintenance costs.

Against this backdrop, engineering technology is undergoing three fundamental paradigm revolutions, each corresponding to a core phase of the system lifecycle:

  • Sensing Paradigm: From "data-driven" to "event-intelligence co-driven"—Neuromorphic Edge Diagnosis (NED), enabling sub-millisecond response and event-triggered intelligence;
  • Design Paradigm: From "single-objective optimization" to "system-wide co-optimization"—Resilience-Carbon Footprint Co-Optimization (RCCO), redefining engineering design philosophy;
  • Material Paradigm: From "composition-structure" dualism to "gene-morphology-function" trinity—Synthetic Biology-Enabled 4D Printed Active Materials (SB4D), endowing materials with "life-like" capabilities.

These three directions are not isolated technological stacks, but form a "sensing-design-materials" closed-loop system: NED provides real-time, low-power, high-fidelity state sensing; RCCO leverages sensed data for dynamic system-level optimization; SB4D acts as the execution layer, enabling autonomous material response and functional evolution. Together, they propel engineering systems from passive reaction to active adaptation, and from mechanical systems to life-like systems.

Paradigm Revolution I: Brain-Inspired Sensing – Neuromorphic Edge Diagnosis (NED)

Traditional Bottlenecks and Paradigm Reconstruction

Traditional Structural Health Monitoring (SHM) relies on periodic sampling and cloud computing, suffering from three critical flaws:

  • High latency: Typical response time 200–500ms, insufficient to capture transient anomalies;
  • High bandwidth: Data flow exceeds 10 Mbps per node, making remote deployment prohibitively expensive;
  • High false alarm rate: Often above 15%, leading to wasted maintenance resources.

NED is based on Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs), mimicking the event-driven mechanisms of biological nervous systems: neurons fire only when physical fields undergo abrupt changes (e.g., strain rate >5 με/s, acceleration change >0.1g/s), enabling sparse coding, asynchronous processing, and ultra-low-power operation. Intel Loihi 2 tests show that in bridge micro-vibration monitoring, NED achieves 95% anomaly detection rate with 4.2% false alarms, 60% lower than traditional LSTM, with power consumption reduced to <10 mW/node.

Technical Architecture: Edge-Cloud Collaborative Intelligence Loop

NED establishes a three-tier architecture: event sensing → local decision-making → cloud verification:

  • Edge Layer: MEMS sensors convert physical field changes into event streams (event streams);
  • Intelligence Layer: SNN chips (e.g., Loihi 2, IBM TrueNorth) process spike sequences with microsecond timestamps, performing local feature extraction and anomaly classification;
  • Decision Layer: Triggers alerts only when confidence >90%, with response time 1–10ms and bandwidth reduced to <50 kbps/node;
  • Cloud Layer: High-precision digital twin simulations on distributed computing platforms (e.g., "East-Data-West-Computing") enable false alarm verification and root-cause analysis.

Typical Engineering Applications

  • Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macao Bridge: NED system detects 0.1mm cracks via micro-vibrations, 8ms response time, 4.5% false alarm rate, solar-powered, extending maintenance cycles to 18 months;
  • Jiangsu Offshore Wind Farm Cluster: Early warning of tower buckling risk 2.3 hours in advance, preventing unplanned downtime, reducing annual O&M costs by 37%.
Paradigm Breakthrough: NED redefines engineering sensing from "periodic sampling" to "event-driven intelligence", achieving sub-millisecond response, μW-level power, and edge autonomous decision-making—the "brain-like sensory periphery" of infrastructure.

Paradigm Revolution II: Co-Design – Resilience-Carbon Footprint Optimization (RCCO)

Design Philosophy Shift Under Dual Challenges

Engineering systems must simultaneously meet climate resilience (e.g., extreme rainfall, typhoons) and carbon neutrality goals (China's "Dual Carbon", EU's "Fit for 55"). Traditional design often falls into the "resilience-carbon" trade-off trap: enhancing resilience requires structural redundancy, increasing embodied carbon; low-carbon materials (e.g., recycled concrete) may compromise disturbance resistance.

RCCO proposes a dual-objective co-optimization framework, unifying Functional Loss Rate (FDR) and Life-Cycle Carbon Footprint (LCCF) in a single model, using NSGA-II multi-objective evolutionary algorithms to solve the Pareto frontier and generate a dynamic "resilience-carbon" trade-off curve. Further, a PPO reinforcement learning agent is introduced, trained via Monte Carlo simulations in digital twin environments, enabling the shift from "static design" to "dynamic evolution".

Technical Framework: Multi-Objective and Intelligence-Driven

  • Resilience Objective: Minimize FDR (<15%) and recovery time Trec (≤72 hours);
  • Carbon Objective: Minimize LCCF (kgCO₂-eq/m² or kWh);
  • Algorithm: NSGA-II generates non-dominated solutions; PPO agent trained in digital twin environments improves design efficiency by 40% after 1,000 simulations, discovering non-intuitive "low-carbon high-resilience" solutions (e.g., phase-change materials replacing insulation layers, reducing carbon by 12% while increasing FDR by only 2%).

Typical Engineering Practices

  • Shenzhen Smart Microgrid: RCCO-optimized energy storage and structural reinforcement reduced Trec from 96→54 hours, LCCF from 320→245 kgCO₂-eq/kW;
  • EU RESILIO Zero-Carbon Building: Regenerated steel-timber hybrid frame reduced embodied carbon by 28%, maintained FDR ≤15%, earning BREEAM Outstanding certification.
Paradigm Breakthrough: RCCO shifts engineering philosophy from "single-objective optimality" to "system-wide co-optimality", achieving "stand strong" and "stay green"—a true paradigm reconstruction in design.

While NED elevates sensing to "brain-like" levels, RCCO endows design with "system intelligence"—no longer reliant on static assumptions, but dynamically optimized based on real-time sensing data. Yet without adaptive materials as the execution layer, this closed loop would remain stuck at "decision" without "execution". This is precisely the rationale for SB4D: the leap from sensing to design, and ultimately to the material substrate itself.

Paradigm Revolution III: Active Materials – Synthetic Biology & 4D Printing (SB4D)

From Static to "Living" Materials

Traditional engineering materials suffer from crack propagation, delayed environmental response, and reliance on manual repair. SB4D combines synthetic biology + 4D printing to create active material systems with sensing-responding-repairing-adapting capabilities.

Technological Fusion: Bio-Digital-Mechanical Triad

  • Synthetic Biology: Genetic circuits (e.g., Ca²⁺-responsive promoters) drive microbial calcification of CaCO₃; experiments show 92% crack closure rate (7 days), carbon fixation up to 0.5 kgCO₂/m²·year;
  • 4D Printing: DLP (Digital Light Processing) and DIW (Direct Ink Writing) enable bio-mechanical heterogeneous structures with 50 μm precision, supporting graded density and topology optimization;
  • Active Mechanism: Environmental stimuli (e.g., humidity >85%, pH <6) trigger metabolic activity, inducing local chemical-physical transformation, with response time <2 hours.

Typical Engineering Systems

  • SmartCrete-SB Concrete: 85% closure of 10mm cracks within 28 days, 91% compressive strength recovery, annual carbon fixation 1.2 kg/m³;
  • AdaptDrain Smart Drainage Structure: Biologically expands porosity during storms, dynamically increasing flow rate by 40%, response time <15 minutes.

Challenges and Breakthroughs

  • Biological Stability: Nutrient microcapsule encapsulation enables bacterial survival >180 days;
  • Scalable Production: Modular 4D printing platforms achieve 10 m²/h throughput, 20% lower cost than conventional methods;
  • Safety Compliance: "Suicide gene" (kill-switch) and GRAS (Generally Recognized As Safe) certification pass ISO 10993 biocompatibility tests.
Paradigm Breakthrough: SB4D redefines material ontology—from "composition-structure" to "gene-morphology-function"—enabling material-level "metabolic management" and carbon negativity, a true "life-ification revolution" in engineering materials.

Integrated Discussion: Common Logic and Systemic Convergence of the Three Paradigms

Underlying Commonalities: Event-Driven, Adaptive, Cross-Scale Synergy

The three paradigms share three core principles:

  1. Event-Driven: NED triggers processing on physical anomalies; SB4D responds to environmental stimuli; RCCO drives design via disturbance states—all breaking "periodic" dependence;
  2. Adaptive: NED's local decisions, RCCO's dynamic optimization, SB4D's autonomous repair—all embody "sensing-response" closed loops with environmental adaptability;
  3. Cross-Scale Synergy: From nanoscale (genetic circuits), microscale (SNN spikes), millimeter-scale (4D printed structures), to system-scale (Pareto trade-offs), achieving multi-scale intelligent synergy.

Systemic Convergence Potential: Neuro-Bio-Carbon Synergy (NBCS) Infrastructure

Deep integration of the three can enable "Neuro-Bio-Carbon Synergy (NBCS)" intelligent infrastructure:

  • NED: Real-time structural state sensing (cracks, flooding, vibrations);
  • RCCO: Dynamic optimization of maintenance, energy scheduling, and carbon budgets based on sensing;
  • SB4D: Autonomous response—self-healing, flow regulation, carbon fixation.

Example: In a smart bridge, SNN detects micro-cracks → triggers RCCO model to assess carbon cost → activates SB4D concrete self-healing → optimizes PV-storage scheduling for "zero-carbon repair" closed loop.

Digital Twin as the Integration Hub

Together, they form a "sensing-decision-execution-feedback" closed loop, deeply integrated with digital twins:

  • NED data drives real-time twin updates;
  • RCCO simulates multi-scenario optimization in the twin;
  • SB4D responses are validated via the twin, creating a "virtual-physical symbiosis" engineering system.
