Legal Boundaries and Challenges of Digital Art

The rise of digital art presents unprecedented challenges to art law. NFTs, as representatives of digital art, have given digital art uniqueness and verifiability through blockchain technology, but have also brought challenges in determining copyright ownership and combating piracy. The proliferation of virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) technologies provides new platforms for art creation and display, but also introduces legal issues in virtual spaces, such as the special protection of art display and the copyright ownership of virtual objects. AI-generated art challenges traditional concepts of artistic creation, raising questions about copyright ownership and ethical issues, such as whether AI can be considered an "author" and whether AI art creation deprives human artists of their creative rights.

Legal Boundaries of Art Criticism and Freedom of Speech

Art criticism, as an essential part of the art ecosystem, carries the core value of freedom of speech, but it also faces challenges such as the blurring of boundaries between malicious attacks and legitimate criticism, the anonymity and influence in the online environment, and the tension between art censorship and freedom of expression. The law must balance the protection of freedom of speech and the rights of artists' reputation, clarify the boundaries between "malicious attacks" and "legitimate criticism" in art criticism, regulate behavior in the online environment, and seek a balance between art censorship and freedom of expression, ensuring that artistic freedom aligns with social moral norms.

Building an Art Legal Framework for the Digital Age

The rise of digital art and the protection of intangible cultural heritage present unprecedented challenges and opportunities for art law. In building an art legal framework for the digital age, we need to consider multiple dimensions of technology, law, and ethics. The law must provide clear rights definitions and protection mechanisms for digital art and intangible cultural heritage while also allowing sufficient flexibility for new technologies. Additionally, the law must be aligned with ethical and social values to ensure the prosperity of digital art and the protection of intangible cultural heritage without compromising artists' rights and social equity.
