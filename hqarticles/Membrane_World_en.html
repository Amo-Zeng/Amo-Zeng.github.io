1. Sociological Experiments in the Membrane World: Moral Dilemmas and Human Nature Exploration

In the film "Membrane World," the director reveals profound issues of moral dilemmas and human nature through meticulously designed sociological experiments. This experimental setting is in a highly closed, resource-scarce, and strictly regulated social system, where individual behavior patterns and moral choices under extreme conditions become the core of observation.

Experimental Design and Moral Dilemmas

The experimental design includes resource scarcity, strict rules, anonymity, and surveillance, aiming to simulate social order under extreme conditions. Individuals in the Membrane World face conflicts between survival and morality, dilemmas between cooperation and competition, and the desire for power and control, triggering profound reflections on selfishness, survival instincts, cooperative desires, and power and control in human nature.

Experimental Results and Reflections

The results show that the social structure in the Membrane World exhibits high instability and conflict initially, but over time, it gradually differentiates into several sub-groups, each forming specific moral norms and behavioral guidelines. This phenomenon indicates that even under extreme conditions, human society still tends to form a certain order and structure. However, the multi-dimensionality and relativity of morality are also revealed in the experiment, reminding us of the need to pay attention to the multi-dimensionality and relativity of moral norms and ethical standards in real society.

Implications for Real Society

The sociological experiments in the film "Membrane World" are not just fictional simulations but profound reflections on real society. They remind us that morality is not a single-dimensional concept but a complex phenomenon influenced by multiple factors; the formation and evolution of power structures have a profound impact on social dynamics; and the complex interaction between individuals and groups requires attention. By understanding the social dynamics in the Membrane World, we can better understand ourselves, reflect on social structures, and seek more reasonable moral and ethical norms.
