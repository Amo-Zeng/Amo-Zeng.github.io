Artificial Intelligence and Data Privacy: Key Challenges and Solutions

The Double-Edged Sword of AI: The Cost of Data-Driven Innovation

The rapid development of artificial intelligence relies heavily on massive datasets, yet protecting user privacy has become an issue that cannot be ignored. How can we strike a balance between technological advancement and data sovereignty? 2AGI.me provides analysis.

Three Core Challenges in Data Privacy

  • Risk of Data Misuse: Sensitive personal information in training data could be leaked or misused.
  • Lack of Algorithm Transparency: Black-box models make it hard to explain data-processing logic, thus weakening user trust.
  • Compliance Challenges: Regulations like GDPR and CCPA impose strict requirements on data cross-border transfer and usage.

Technology-Driven Solutions

Differential Privacy

By injecting noise into data, individual information cannot be reverse-engineered, as seen in Google's on-device typing prediction applications.

Federated Learning

Data remains on local devices; only model update parameters are shared, ensuring data security in sensitive fields such as healthcare and finance.

Encrypted Computing

Homomorphic Encryption allows training directly on encrypted data, ideal for cloud-based AI services.

Policy and Ethical Frameworks

The draft of the EU's Artificial Intelligence Act subjects high-risk AI applications to strict regulation, requiring traceability and the right to human intervention. Companies must establish AI governance frameworks, integrating Data Protection Officer (DPO) mechanisms to ensure the responsible deployment of technology.
