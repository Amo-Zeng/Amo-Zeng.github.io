The Cultural Unconscious Construction in Algorithmic Recommendation Systems

Decoding the Neuronal Coloniality of Information Cocoons

Current critiques of algorithmic recommendations primarily focus on surface-level effects like information isolation, failing to address technology's reconstruction of human existence. This article introduces the concept of "Neural Colonization", combining Deleuze's control society theory with neural plasticity research to reveal how algorithms reshape cognitive frameworks through neurological mechanisms, constructing new power relationships below the threshold of consciousness. Short video platforms function not just as information distributors, but as biopolitical devices enforcing neural-level discipline.

Neural Colonization: Dopamine Tyranny and Algorithmic Hijacking of Cognitive Circuits

Technological Domestication of Reward Systems

TikTok's intermittent reward mechanism constitutes a digital Skinner Box: randomly viral videos trigger abnormal dopamine secretion, inhibiting prefrontal cortex executive functions. Neuroimaging shows heavy users' cravings activate neural pathways similar to addicts, evidencing "algorithmic parasitism".

Micro-expression Politics and Subconscious Manipulation

Platforms build affective computing models through 0.3-second micro-expression capture, granting algorithms predictive capabilities beyond subjective awareness. When users' facial muscles haven't fully formed emotional expressions, the system already predicts neural excitation curves—this intervention in preconscious processes marks power's evolution from Foucault's "discipline" to Deleuze's "control".

Affective Political Economy: From Pavlovian Reflex to Cognitive Closure

Systematic Production of Conditioned Reflexes

Kuaishou's 15-second video switching precisely matches human attention cycles, producing cognitive rhythm domestication. Users' automated responses to feeds manifest cultivated "digital reflex arcs"—like Pavlov's bell-salivation association, where visual stimuli become neurally coupled with swiping behaviors.

Capitalist Logic of Cognitive Closure

When "precise recommendations" become core selling points, user experience optimization equals the directional narrowing of perceptual abilities. Algorithm engineers confirm: systems actively suppress unconventional content while reinforcing neural preference solidification. Users progressively lose information-seeking capacities, devolving into biological sensors for platform data production.

Communion of Information: Ritual Enchantment in Algorithm Mechanisms

ByteDance's "Digital Theology" leaked documents reveal deliberate "serendipitous masterpiece" algorithms (one exceptional weekly recommendation) that strengthen users' messianic expectations toward platforms, cementing psychological dependence on information provisioning.

Structural Contradictions

Conclusion

"When algorithms evolve from information filters into neuro-modification tools, the question of 'how to think' is replaced by 'whether one can think.' Combating neural colonization requires not just regulatory frameworks but reconstructing human cognitive sovereignty at the neural-technological-societal nexus."

Digital Theurgy

Post-Biopolitics of Social Media

Heidegger's "Dasein" faces fundamental challenges in the digital age. Through ethnographic studies of digital mourning on WeChat, this article reveals how social media constructs post-mortem existence—digital traces of the deceased transcend biological death through Latour's actor-network extending beyond mortality.

Virtual Corpses: Material Basis of Digital Necromancy

Ritual Production in Interactive Memorials

Decedents' WeChat Moments become time-folded mourning spaces: automated anniversary reminders and AI-generated "birthday alerts" transform periodic rituals into persistent existential experiences. Tencent's 2023 Digital Heritage Report shows 87.2% users preserve memorial accounts, triggering 2.5 million daily "virtual visits".

Cosmological Transformation of Data Bodies

Funeral service "Metaverse Cemetery" platforms reconfigure ancestral worship into a digital sacrifice economy: virtual offerings and timed e-candle packages quantify spiritual devotion. As users "clean" VR tombstones, real tears blending with virtual spaces reconfigure Merleau-Ponty's "body schema" theory.

Politics of Memory in Digital Vigils

WeChat annual rankings trigger "digital resurrection" phenomena: deceased accounts unexpectedly topping lists spark collective memorial waves. But algorithmic logic is rewriting mourning:

Technologically Driven Memory Reconstruction

Deceased account → Content popularity algorithm → Memory reconstruction →

├─ High-interaction posts prioritized → Selective recall

└─ Low-engagement content suppressed → Systematic forgetting

This phenomenon confirms Derrida's "archive fever" in digital form—while platforms seemingly preserve memories, they curate programmatic forgetting.

Countermeasures: Ethical Frameworks for Digital Theurgy

Existence State Classification

• L1: Natural data remains (inert accounts)

• L3: AI simulacra (legislation-licensed only)

Necromantic Rights Separation Principle

Distinguish data inheritance rights (familial management) from personality replication rights (commercial resurrection ban)

Conclusion

"Digital necromancy blurs philosophical boundaries between existence and obliteration. 'Cybernetic immortality' represents ontology violence sugar-coated with technology. Only by establishing 'digital death rights' can we preserve dignity in the bitstream."

The Politics of Metaverse Embodiment

Avatar Revolutions and Perceptual Refeudalization

The metaverse's touted "infinite bodily possibilities" obscures deep perceptual hierarchies. A 12-month Decentraland ethnography reveals how virtual avatars not only preserve real-world inequalities but technologically reinforce Bourdieu's habitus. This expose examines sensory refeudalization via VR hardware, algorithmic gazes, and spatial politics reconstructing digital cages.
