Cross-Generational Dialogue and Reverse Shaping: Cultural Cognition Reconstruction through Metaphor Decoding

Cultural cognition, like a ceaseless river, traverses the boundaries of time and space, continuously evolving and reshaping itself amidst the tides of globalization and technological innovation. Whether it is the cultural dialogue between generations, the reverse shaping of culture in a global context, or the deep encoding of cultural cognition through metaphors, they collectively reveal the complexity and diversity of cultural evolution. This article will explore the dynamic reconstruction of cultural cognition and its future development direction from three key perspectives: "cross-generational dialogue," "reverse shaping," and "metaphor decoding."

Cross-Generational Dialogue: The Two-Way Interaction of Cultural Breakdown and Fusion

The generational differences in culture are one of the core propositions of cultural evolution. The older generation, rooted in tradition, maintains cultural continuity and stability through oral narratives and rituals. The younger generation, however, shaped by the internet and globalization, has developed a fragmented and diversified approach to cultural cognition. This difference is particularly evident in daily life. For example, in the celebration of traditional festivals, the older generation adheres to the ritualistic traditions, while the younger generation imbues the festivals with new meanings through social media. However, this generational breakdown is not a cultural interruption but another form of cultural continuity.

Take Hanfu culture as an example. The younger generation has transformed Hanfu from a historical relic into a global fashion symbol through modern design and digital dissemination, attracting widespread attention. This innovation not only revitalizes traditional culture but also prompts the older generation to re-examine and accept modern expressions of culture. In this two-way interaction, cultural cognition becomes a dynamic evolving system, retaining the spiritual core of tradition while injecting modern innovative vitality. Cross-generational dialogue not only bridges the cultural gap but also provides new opportunities for cultural inheritance and innovation.

Reverse Shaping: The Global Flow and Reconstruction of Cultural Cognition

In the context of globalization, cultural cognition is no longer a one-way propagation process but a tension-filled interactive field. Cultural products not only carry the expression of specific cultures but also reverse shape the forms of culture in global flows. For instance, Hollywood films, by absorbing aesthetic elements from Chinese martial arts films, reshape global audiences' perception of Eastern culture. Meanwhile, Japanese writer Haruki Murakami's works have sparked a re-interpretation of Japanese culture in the West, yet his创作 is deeply influenced by Western modernism.

This reverse shaping power reveals the agency of cultural cognition. Culture is no longer a passive object but a dynamic generative process through consumption, dissemination, and recreation. In the global context, the boundaries of culture gradually blur, and regionality and globality achieve dialectical unity. The reverse shaping of cultural cognition not only enriches the diversity of culture but also provides new possibilities for cultural innovation.

Metaphor Decoding: The Deep Grammar of Cultural Cognition

Metaphors, as the deep grammar of cultural cognition, carry the spiritual essence of different civilizations. Western culture metaphorically views time as money, emphasizing efficiency and planning; Eastern culture, with its core metaphor of "harmony between heaven and man," pursues harmony and cycles. This metaphorical thinking paradigm profoundly influences societal values and behaviors. For example, Western business culture values rapid iteration and competition, while Eastern culture emphasizes sustainable development and collective consciousness.

In the global context, the fluidity and reconstructive nature of metaphors are further manifested. Emerging metaphors like "digital existence" blend technicism and holistic wisdom, serving as a bridge for cross-cultural understanding. By decoding metaphors, we gain insight into the spiritual essence of different civilizations and achieve deeper understanding and dialogue in the collision and fusion of cultures.
