How Does Gut Microbiota "Remote Control" Stroke Rehabilitation?

Gut-Brain Axis Ushers in New Era of Stroke Treatment

Stroke remains one of the leading causes of disability and mortality worldwide. Recent studies reveal that gut microbiota may subtly orchestrate post-stroke rehabilitation through the gut-brain axis - a discovery revolutionizing the field of neural repair.

I. Scientific Background: The "Cross-Organ Network" Between Gut and Brain

The human gut harbors approximately 40 trillion microbes, containing 150 times more genes than human DNA. Dynamic interactions occur through neurotransmitters, immune factors and metabolites:

  • Stroke triggers microbiota disruption: 40% reduction in Faecalibacterium within 24 hours of onset
  • Remote microbial impact: Germ-free mice show 40% smaller infarct areas with accelerated recovery

II. Mechanisms Revealed: Triple Action of Microbial Metabolites

1. Short-Chain Fatty Acids (SCFAs)

Every 1μmol/g increase in butyrate correlates with 15% improvement in motor recovery (Cell Reports, 2022)

2. Tryptophan Metabolites

Stroke patients show 3× higher serum kynurenine levels than healthy controls

3. Leaky Gut Effects

LPS endotoxin triggers vicious cycle of "brain-gut inflammation axis"
