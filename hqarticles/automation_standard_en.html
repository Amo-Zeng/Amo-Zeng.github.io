Automation Standards: The Three Pillars Shaping the Future

Automation standards, a seemingly obscure technical topic, is quietly becoming the three core pillars shaping future society: driving the revolution in metaverse digital infrastructure, serving as an innovative engine for combating climate change, and building the foundation for trustworthy artificial intelligence. These three pillars, though seemingly independent, are closely intertwined, together painting a picture of a more open, green, and intelligent future.

1. The Metaverse: Revolutionizing Digital Infrastructure

The metaverse, a digital world that integrates cutting-edge technologies such as virtual reality, augmented reality, and blockchain, is reshaping human online life at an unprecedented speed. However, to achieve this grand vision, a key challenge lies ahead: how to build a complex infrastructure capable of supporting seamless interaction, content sharing, and transactions for millions of users?

Automation standards are the key to solving this challenge.

The power of standardization, like the "digital highway" of the metaverse, provides a unified language and rules for different platforms, applications, and devices. They define the formats and protocols for key elements such as user profiles, digital assets, identity verification, and payment systems, ensuring smooth interaction and collaboration between different systems.

Interoperability, security, and development efficiency are the three core areas where automation standards empower the metaverse. They break down barriers between platforms, enabling the free flow of data, assets, and services; provide a unified framework for identity verification, data transmission, and transaction security; and simplify the development process, allowing developers to focus on innovation and user experience without starting from scratch.

Some pioneering companies have already recognized the importance of automation standards and are actively involved in their development and promotion. For example, the Khronos Group's OpenXR standard provides a unified API for virtual and augmented reality applications; Meta's Metaverse Standards Forum brings together industry leaders and technical experts to drive the development and adoption of metaverse standards.

2. Climate Change: A Source of Innovation and Power

Climate change, a major global threat, is driving profound transformations in energy, transportation, construction, and other sectors of human society. In this transition, automation technology, with its powerful data processing and intelligent decision-making capabilities, shows tremendous potential. However, technology alone cannot address the challenge; the development and promotion of automation standards are the key to realizing its potential.

Automation standards are the cornerstone of addressing climate change.

They provide a unified framework for energy efficiency by ensuring seamless coordination between different devices and systems; lay the foundation for the large-scale application of renewable energy, supporting the integration and optimization of distributed energy resources; and provide clear guidelines for technology developers, clearing the path for rapid market adoption.

Smart grids, electric vehicles, and building automation are specific areas where automation standards address climate change. They provide strong support for global energy transition and sustainable development through standardized technology applications, improved system efficiency, and accelerated innovation.

Climate change is a global issue that requires coordinated efforts worldwide. The development and promotion of automation standards provide a common technical language and cooperation framework for countries to address climate change. International standardization and coordination, the role of public-private partnerships, and enhanced policy implementation are all important aspects of how automation standards drive climate action.

3. Artificial Intelligence: A Foundation of Trust

Artificial intelligence (AI) is rapidly permeating every corner of society, from self-driving cars to intelligent medical diagnosis, with its influence growing by the day. However, alongside this technological revolution comes a deep concern about the safety and reliability of AI systems. Faced with this challenge, automation standards become the key to unlocking a trustworthy AI future.

Safety foundation, reliability assurance, and a bridge of trust are the three pillars that automation standards build for trustworthy artificial intelligence. They provide a solid safety foundation for AI systems, systematic methods for testing and verifying AI models, and set ethical boundaries for the design and use of AI systems.

Developing automation standards is not a one-time effort; it requires the collective efforts of governments, businesses, academia, and the public. Governments should actively promote the development and implementation of relevant standards, businesses should embrace standards and integrate them into product development processes, academia should continuously research and improve the standard system, and the public should actively participate in discussions and oversight of standards.

Conclusion

Automation standards are the three pillars shaping future society. They are the digital infrastructure revolution of the metaverse era, the source of innovation and power in combating climate change, and the foundation of trustworthy artificial intelligence. On the path to the future, we need to lead with automation standards to build a more open, green, and intelligent society, creating a better future for humanity.
