Sample Revision Comparison

Assuming original text is in popular science style:

Original:

Academic Revision:

"There exists a significant association between social media usage and increased subjective loneliness (Nowland et al., 2018). Longitudinal studies indicate that passive social media engagement (e.g., non-interactive browsing) may reduce psychological well-being (Î² = -0.21, p < .001) through social comparison mechanisms (Festinger, 1954) and real-world social displacement effects (Kraut et al., 1998), with this phenomenon being particularly pronounced among adolescent populations (Twenge et al., 2021)."

Key Improvements

  • Added authoritative citations
  • Clarified research methods and statistical indicators
  • Differentiated correlation from causal mechanisms
  • Incorporated theoretical frameworks
  • Specified study population
  • Used academic quantitative expressions
