Article 2: Dual-Layer Pricing Model for Climate Beta Premium

Optimization Highlights

Title Highlighting Differential Contributions: "Bidirectional Transmission of Climate Risk: A Stratified Asset Pricing Model Based on Physical Risk Beta and Transition Risk Beta"

Abstract Clarifying Stratification Mechanism:

**Abstract**  
Addressing the shortcomings of homogeneous climate risk modeling, this paper proposes a dual-layer climate beta pricing theory:  
- **Physical Risk Beta (β_P)**: Spatial epidemic model + geographic coordinate weighting of fixed assets  
- **Transition Risk Beta (β_T)**: Markov regime-switching model for policy sensitivity  
Key innovations include:  
(1) Designing the **Climate Policy Sensitivity Coefficient (CPS)** to dynamically capture carbon policy shocks;  
(2) Constructing the **Climate Vulnerability Adjustment Factor (CVA)** to quantify regional heterogeneity;  
(3) Discovering a 43% post-Paris Agreement β_T premium surge in high-carbon firms (t=5.21).  
The model reduces pricing errors for climate-sensitive assets from 38.7% (traditional CAPM) to 9.8%, providing actionable tools for TCFD disclosures.
