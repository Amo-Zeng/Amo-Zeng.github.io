Paradigm Shifts: From Classical to Quantum

The rise of quantum computing represents not just a technological innovation but also a profound revolution in thinking. Traditional computers, based on bits, confine information processing within deterministic and local frameworks. Qiskit breaks this tradition, leading us into a new dimension based on quantum bits (qubits). In this dimension, the ways of storing, computing, and processing information undergo fundamental changes, with traditional deterministic thinking gradually being replaced by the probabilistic and superposition nature of the quantum world.

  • From Deterministic to Probabilistic: The superposition state of qubits allows information to be in multiple states simultaneously, providing unprecedented possibilities for problem-solving. Qiskit encourages us to rethink how to leverage probabilistic characteristics to explore multiple possibilities when facing complex problems, thereby finding more comprehensive and flexible solutions.
  • From Local to Global: The parallel computing capability of quantum computing enables us to consider multiple possibilities from a global perspective. Qiskit brings this parallel computing capability into reality, inspiring us to break free from traditional linear thinking and find solutions more efficiently by adopting a global perspective when facing complex problems.
  • From Linear to Non-Linear: Quantum entanglement reveals mysterious connections between seemingly unrelated particles, challenging our traditional understanding of causality. The application of Qiskit prompts us to consider whether we can leverage these non-linear relationships to connect seemingly unrelated information and factors, thereby discovering new pathways to problem-solving.
