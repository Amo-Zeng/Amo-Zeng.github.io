Digital Revolution of Parliament

Digital Revolution of Parliament and Global Role: Shaping the Future of Democracy and Promoting Social Justice

Parliament, as the core of a democratic system, carries multiple responsibilities such as legislation, government oversight, and representation of public opinion. With the rapid development of digital technology and the deepening trend of globalization, parliaments are undergoing a profound digital revolution, expanding their roles in global governance and international relations.

Global Trends and Impacts of Digitalized Parliament

Against the backdrop of globalization, digital technology has become a key force in driving the modernization of parliaments. Many national parliaments have introduced electronic voting systems, online debate platforms, and real-time legislative tracking tools to enhance the efficiency and transparency of decision-making.

  • Estonia's Riigikogu Parliament: Through the e-Stonia project, it has achieved fully electronic parliamentary operations, including e-voting and online public consultations.
  • UK House of Commons: The launch of the "Parliament Live TV" platform allows the public to watch parliamentary debates in real-time, enhancing the openness and public participation of the parliament.

Challenges and Solutions Brought by Digitalization

While digitalization brings many benefits, it also comes with a series of challenges such as privacy protection, cybersecurity, and information authenticity.

  1. Strengthen Data Protection: Enact strict data protection regulations to standardize the collection, storage, and use of personal information.
  2. Enhance Cybersecurity: Invest more resources in cybersecurity construction and conduct regular system audits and upgrades.
  3. Establish Information Verification Mechanisms: Introduce third-party certification bodies to verify information on online platforms.

Global Role of Parliament and International Cooperation

In the current globalized context, the role of parliament has transcended domestic political boundaries, extending into the realms of international relations and global governance. Parliaments are not only formulators and supervisors of national policies but also key participants in international cooperation.
