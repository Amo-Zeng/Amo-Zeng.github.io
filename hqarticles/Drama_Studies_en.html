The Technological Triad in Theater

Posthuman Theater Revolution: From Data Gaze to Material Insurrection

Abstract

This paper proposes the "Technological Triad in Theater" model, revealing three paradigm shifts in contemporary drama studies: the alienation of digital labor through databodied performers in algorithmic theater, the reconstruction of ecological agency in cross-species performances, and the negative dialectics of technological rationality through glitch aesthetics. These dynamic contradictions form a political arena driving theater's transformation into an "interface of posthuman cognition."

Introduction: Technological Theater as Cognitive Revolution Battleground
