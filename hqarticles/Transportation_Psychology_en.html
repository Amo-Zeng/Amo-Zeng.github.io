Enhancing Road Safety and Driver Mental Health

Emotional Contagion: The Invisible Ripples in Urban Traffic

In the bustling urban traffic network, drivers' emotional fluctuations ripple not only affecting their own behavior but also subtly spreading to other road users through visual, auditory contact, and shared spaces. This emotional contagion can inadvertently significantly alter driving decisions and behaviors, increasing the likelihood of risky driving and thereby elevating accident risks. To curb these potential negative effects, developing intelligent in-vehicle systems, humanized roadside facilities, and promoting mental health education are considered practical and effective responses.

Virtual Reality: A New Dimension in Driver Psychological Training

Virtual reality technology, akin to opening a door to a new world, has brought unprecedented changes to driver training. In the simulated environment created by VR, drivers can repeatedly practice strategies for dealing with extreme and complex traffic scenarios under zero risk. This training not only enhances drivers' risk perception capabilities and emergency response speeds but also necessitates vigilance against potential side effects such as "simulation sickness." Therefore, balancing virtual training with actual driving and regularly assessing driver adaptability becomes paramount for future development.

Social Media: The Invisible Shaper of Traffic Behavior and Psychology

Social media platforms, like an invisible net, profoundly influence public traffic behavior and mental states. Popular discussions on dangerous driving videos, traffic policy debates, and other content on these platforms subtly shape individual and collective behavior patterns.
