Multidimensional Interpretation of Penis Envy

Penis envy, a concept proposed by Sigmund Freud, refers to the female desire or jealousy for the male genitalia. Although this theory has been widely criticized and questioned in modern gender studies, its core idea—the complex relationship between physical characteristics and gender identity—still holds significant value in the context of gender diversity. Penis envy has transcended mere physical comparison, evolving into a multidimensional phenomenon involving gender identity, social power, cultural symbols, and mental health. 1. The Complexity of Gender Identity and Penis Envy

In traditional views, biological sex and gender identity are often considered closely linked. However, with the development of gender theory, people have gradually come to understand gender identity as a more complex psychological and social construct. For some, their physical characteristics do not align with their deep-seated gender identity, which can lead to intense internal conflict.

Transgender men (assigned female at birth but identify as male) may experience a strong desire for a penis, as it is traditionally seen as a symbol of masculinity. This desire is not merely a physical need but also a confirmation and expression of gender identity. Similarly, non-binary individuals (who do not fit into the traditional male-female gender binary) may feel confused between physical characteristics and self-expression, and penis envy could become part of their exploration of self-identity.

1.2 Balancing Social Expectations and Self-Expression

In today's gender-diverse world, societal expectations of gender roles still exist, although they are gradually changing. Traditional gender norms associate the penis with masculinity, power, and dominance, while the absence of a penis is linked to femininity, submission, and dependency. These societal expectations have a profound impact on individual self-expression. For those who do not conform to traditional gender norms, penis envy may become a way to break these norms and pursue self-identification.
