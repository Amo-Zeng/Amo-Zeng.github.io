Film Town: From Cultural Output to Metaverse Experiment, a New Engine for Urban Development

Film towns, once merely considered as locations for filming movies and TV shows, have evolved into multifunctional platforms that integrate cultural output, technological innovation, and urban development. They are not only birthplaces of film and TV works but also spreaders of cultural values, lifestyles, and aesthetic tastes; they are not only experimental fields where virtual and real worlds intersect but also important exploration platforms for the future metaverse; they are not only a significant part of the cultural industry but also a new engine driving urban economic development.

Film Town: An Important Platform for Cultural Output

Traditionally, film towns were seen as temporary locations for filming movies and TV shows, where crews were busy and sets were built. However, with the deepening of global cultural exchanges and the booming development of the film and television industry, the role of film towns has transcended simple filming functions, becoming a diversified platform for cultural output. They are not only birthplaces of film and TV works but also spreaders of cultural values, lifestyles, and aesthetic tastes. Through various forms such as film and TV works, tourism projects, and derivative products, film towns transmit specific cultural symbols and content to domestic and international audiences, playing an important role in shaping the national cultural image and promoting cultural output.

Take Hengdian Film Town as an example. It is not only one of the largest film and television production bases in China but also an important window for Chinese cultural output. Hengdian has successfully established the brand image of "Hollywood in China," integrating traditional Chinese culture, historical stories, and modern elements to create a series of internationally influential film and TV works. For instance, the ancient costume dramas such as "Empresses in the Palace" and "Nirvana in Fire" shot in Hengdian not only sparked a收视热潮 domestically but also gained widespread popularity in overseas markets, attracting a large number of international audiences to pay attention to Chinese history and culture. At the same time, Hengdian Film Town combines film shooting experiences with cultural exhibitions through tourism projects. Visitors can personally experience ancient palace life and participate in the film shooting process, further deepening their understanding of Chinese culture.

Although film towns have shown great potential in cultural output, their development also faces numerous challenges. First, how to meet the aesthetic needs of international audiences while preserving cultural connotations is a question that film towns need to deeply reflect on. Cultural output is not simply about translating works or replicating scenes but requires finding共鸣 points with the international market while maintaining cultural uniqueness. Film towns can enhance the international competitiveness of their works by collaborating with international production teams and adopting advanced technology and narrative techniques.

Secondly, film towns need to find a balance between cultural output and commercial interests. Cultural output is a long-term endeavor, while commercial interests are often short-term pursuits. Film towns need to focus on long-term cultural value accumulation while pursuing economic benefits. For example, they can reasonably plan tourism projects to avoid excessive commercialization leading to distorted cultural experiences; they can also support the inheritance and innovation of local culture through cultural funds and public welfare projects.

Film Town: The Convergence of Virtual and Real Worlds, the "Test Field" of the Metaverse

Under the impetus of technological waves, cutting-edge technologies such as virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) are reshaping our lifestyles at an unprecedented speed. Film towns, the "dream factories" of the film industry, are quietly becoming the "test field" of the convergence of virtual and real worlds. They are not only birthplaces of film and TV works but also important exploration platforms for the future metaverse.

Traditional film towns are famous for their real-scene setups, serving as windows for audiences to understand behind-the-scenes film production. However, with the popularization of digital technology, film towns are breaking the limitations of physical space, becoming the "new battlefield" of the convergence of virtual and real worlds. Through VR/AR technology, audiences can transcend temporal and spatial boundaries to experience more immersive viewing journeys. For example, by immersing themselves in a virtual set, audiences can "personally" feel the behind-the-scenes stories of film production, even interacting with virtual characters. This experience revolutionizes traditional viewing modes, transforming audiences from passive information recipients to active content participants.

Film towns are not limited to providing immersive experiences for audiences; they will also become the "test field" for metaverse business models. In the metaverse, the boundaries between virtual and real worlds will become increasingly blurred. Film towns can leverage this characteristic to create new entertainment experiences. For instance, audiences can purchase virtual land in a virtual film town, build their own "virtual sets," and even participate in the creative process of film and TV works, becoming "virtual directors" or "virtual actors." In addition, virtual film towns can be combined with offline real-world experiences to form an "online + offline" business model, further expanding the commercial value of film towns.

In the future, as metaverse technology continues to mature, how will film towns leverage this trend to create new entertainment experiences and business models? Perhaps virtual film towns will become a new traffic entry point, attracting global audiences; perhaps virtual characters will become new IPs, spawning rich peripheral products; or perhaps virtual film towns will break national boundaries, becoming new platforms for cultural exchange. Regardless of how the future develops, the convergence of virtual and real worlds will be an inevitable trend in the development of film towns.

Film Town: A New Engine for Urban Development, a Paragon of Cultural and Tourism Integration

In recent years, as a product of the deep integration of cultural and tourism industries, film towns are rapidly changing the trajectory of urban development. They are not just filming bases for movies and TV shows but also new engines driving urban economic development, injecting new vitality into regional economies, and becoming a paragon of cultural and tourism integration.

As an important part of the cultural industry, the role of film towns in driving urban economy cannot be overlooked. First, the construction of film towns itself drives a large amount of investment and employment opportunities, from infrastructure construction to post-operation management, involving multiple industries and fields. Second, film towns attract a large number of crews and tourists, directly driving the development of related industries such as tourism, catering, and accommodation in surrounding areas. This "film + tourism" model not only extends the industry chain but also creates more employment opportunities and economic benefits.

Take the Western Theater Town in Zhenbei, Yinchuan, Ningxia, for example. Originally just a desolate ancient fortress, it has become a famous tourist attraction through film shooting and tourism development. Classic films such as "A Chinese Odyssey" and "Red Sorghum" were shot there, making the Western Theater Town in Zhenbei renowned and attracting a large number of tourists. This not only提升了 the local reputation but also stimulated the development of surrounding catering, accommodation, and other service industries, promoting rapid economic growth in the region.

However, as the number of film towns continues to increase, the issue of homogenization competition has become increasingly prominent. Many film towns lack in-depth excavation of local cultural characteristics during construction, resulting in similar content and insufficient attraction. Therefore, how to avoid homogenization competition and achieve sustainable development has become an important issue facing the development of film towns.

To stand out in fierce market competition, film towns must deeply integrate into local culture to create unique cultural tourism brands. First, during planning and construction, film towns should fully consider the local natural environment and historical culture, combining the design of film towns with local cultural elements to form a unique cultural atmosphere. For example, the Songcheng Film Town in Hangzhou successfully attracts a large number of tourists by restoring the urban landscape and cultural life of the Song Dynasty, becoming a cultural tourism名片 for Hangzhou.

Second, film towns should focus on interaction with local culture, enhancing tourists' cultural experiences through various cultural activities and festivals. For instance, Hengdian Film Town attracts a large number of tourists and boosts the reputation and influence of the film town by holding events such as the "Film Carnival," combining film shooting with local culture.

Conclusion

Film towns, once merely single-purpose filming locations, have now evolved into multifunctional platforms integrating cultural output, technological innovation, and urban development. They are not only important windows for cultural output but also test fields for the convergence of virtual and real worlds and new engines driving urban economic development. By deeply integrating into local culture and creating unique cultural tourism brands, film towns can inject new vitality into urban economies and promote the transformation and upgrading of regional economies. However, to achieve sustainable development, film towns must focus on cultural innovation and ecological protection during planning and operation, avoiding homogenization competition, thereby providing continuous impetus for long-term urban development. In the future, with the further maturity of metaverse technology, film towns will open a new chapter in development through the convergence of virtual and real worlds, bringing global audiences richer and more diverse cultural experiences.
