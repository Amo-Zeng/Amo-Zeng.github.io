Urban Underground Space Development, Greening, and Smart Management: Building a Resilient and Sustainable Future for Cities

As urbanization rapidly progresses, cities face multiple challenges such as land scarcity, environmental degradation, and frequent natural disasters. How to achieve efficient and sustainable development within limited space has become a core issue in urban planning. This article delves into the utilization of urban underground space, the interaction between urban greening and geological environment, and the integration of smart cities with Geographic Information Systems (GIS), proposing a comprehensive solution aimed at enhancing urban resilience and sustainable development.

I. Diversified Use and Geological Safety of Urban Underground Space

1. Diversified Use:

  • Underground transportation systems: Such as subways and tunnels, reducing surface traffic congestion.
  • Underground shopping streets: Providing a climate-controlled shopping environment, saving surface space.
  • Underground parking: Addressing parking issues while preserving urban landscapes.
  • Underground energy storage facilities: Including underground reservoirs and thermal storage, enhancing energy utilization efficiency.

2. Geological Safety Considerations:

  • Groundwater level changes: Underground construction might lead to a drop in groundwater levels, causing subsidence.
  • Risk of land subsidence: Improper underground engineering construction can damage the surface.
  • Seismic activity: Large-scale excavation might alter geological stresses, increasing seismic risks.

3. Technological Means:

  • Geological exploration: Using ground penetrating radar, electromagnetic detection, and seismic wave detection techniques.
  • Engineering reinforcement: Techniques like reinforced concrete and anchoring.
  • Monitoring and warning: Utilizing real-time monitoring systems and AI technology to prevent geological disasters.

II. Interaction between Urban Greening and Geological Environment

1. Geological Stability and Greening Choices:

Based on different geological conditions, select appropriate deep-rooted or shallow-rooted plants to enhance soil shear strength or prevent surface erosion.

2. Ecological Restoration and Plant Communities:

Simulate natural ecosystems by creating multi-layered plant communities to promote soil health and biodiversity.

3. Soil and Water Conservation and Plant Layout:

Use slope vegetation, vegetation buffer zones, and rain gardens to reduce soil erosion and protect water quality.

4. Microclimate Improvement and Heat Island Effect:

Utilize plant transpiration and shading effects to mitigate urban heat island effects and enhance ventilation.

III. Integration of Smart Cities and GIS

1. Role of GIS in Urban Management:

  • Integrate and analyze geological data to support urban planning and real-time monitoring.
  • Predict natural disasters and formulate disaster prevention strategies.
  • Conserve and utilize geological heritage to enhance urban cultural value.

2. Prediction and Prevention of Natural Disasters:

  • Establish earthquake warning systems, real-time monitoring for landslides and flood risks.
  • Manage groundwater resources to prevent subsidence.

3. Technological Integration and Future Outlook:

  • Big data and AI technology to extract useful information, improving prediction accuracy.
  • IoT sensor networks providing richer data, promoting GIS applications.
  • Realize sustainable use of resources, reducing environmental impact.

Conclusion

Through the rational development of underground spaces, scientific urban greening layout, and implementation of smart city management systems, cities can achieve efficient resource use, environmental improvement, and disaster prevention. The development of underground spaces is not just about solving land resource issues but is an innovative path for sustainable urban development. Urban greening beautifies the environment and serves as the foundation of the ecosystem, helping cities live in harmony with nature. The integration of smart cities with GIS provides a dynamic, multi-dimensional perspective for urban management, making cities more flexible and resilient in facing challenges.

In the future, urban planners and engineers need to integrate these elements into the blueprint for city development, ensuring that construction meets current needs while also laying the foundation for future sustainable development. Through technological innovation and increased ecological awareness, cities will become exemplars of human-nature symbiosis, driving society towards a greener, safer, and more sustainable direction.
