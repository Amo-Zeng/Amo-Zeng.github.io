From "Knowledge Embodiment" to "Service Manufacturing": The Paradigm Shift in Smart Manufacturing Reshaping Productivity and Value Creation

Smart Manufacturing: The Paradigm Shift in Reshaping Productivity and Value Creation

Smart manufacturing, as the core engine of the Fourth Industrial Revolution, is reshaping the global manufacturing landscape at an unprecedented speed and depth. This transformation not only disrupts traditional production methods but fundamentally redefines the roles of knowledge, humans, and value creation in economic activities. From "knowledge embodiment" to "knowledge-enabled equipment," from "machine replacing humans" to "human-machine collaboration," and from "product manufacturing" to "service manufacturing," smart manufacturing is ushering in a new revolution in productivity and value creation.

1. The Leap in Knowledge Productivity: The Essence of Smart Manufacturing

The essence of smart manufacturing is a profound revolution in knowledge productivity. In traditional manufacturing models, knowledge primarily served as an auxiliary factor, responsible for optimizing production processes and improving efficiency. However, in the new paradigm of smart manufacturing, knowledge has become the core driving force behind production, driving the transition from factor-driven to innovation-driven production methods. This transformation is divided into two key stages:

  1. Knowledge Embodiment: The Initial Phase of Smart Manufacturing

    In the early stages of smart manufacturing, knowledge was embedded into equipment, enhancing production efficiency through automated devices and digital means. Key characteristics of this phase include: converting the knowledge and experience of process experts into equipment control programs, achieving mechanized operations; optimizing fixed production parameters through data analysis to enhance production efficiency; and equipment capable of completing tasks according to preset programs but lacking autonomous decision-making abilities. Although this model significantly improved production efficiency, it was essentially an extension of traditional production methods, failing to fully leverage the value of knowledge.

  2. Knowledge-Enabled Equipment: The Fundamental Transition of Smart Manufacturing

    With the maturity of artificial intelligence and big data technologies, smart manufacturing has entered a new phase of knowledge-enabled equipment. Key characteristics of this phase include: equipment with self-learning capabilities, able to autonomously optimize operating parameters through machine learning algorithms; equipment capable of self-diagnosing faults and predicting maintenance needs, improving equipment utilization; production systems dynamically adjusting production strategies based on real-time data to achieve personalized customization; and knowledge-driven production systems rapidly responding to market demands, enabling intelligent flexible production. Knowledge-enabled equipment allows manufacturing systems to possess human-like thinking abilities, capable of proactive learning, autonomous decision-making, and dynamic optimization, marking the fundamental leap from mechanization to intelligence in smart manufacturing.

2. Human-Machine Collaboration: Redefining Human Value

In smart manufacturing, the value of humans is redefined, transitioning from "manual laborers" to "knowledge workers" and "innovators." The traditional "machine replacing humans" mindset overlooks the unique value of humans in knowledge accumulation, creativity, and complex decision-making, while the "human-machine collaboration" paradigm views machines as extensions and assistants to humans, freeing humans from heavy physical labor and repetitive tasks to focus on more creative and strategic work.

  • Collaborative Robots (Cobots)

    Collaborative robots are flexible and safe, enabling them to work alongside humans in the same workspace, accomplishing tasks such as precision assembly and quality inspection, significantly enhancing production efficiency and safety.

  • Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR) Technologies

    VR technology provides operators with an immersive training environment, allowing them to simulate real production scenarios in a virtual space, enhancing operational skills; AR technology overlays virtual information onto the real world, assisting engineers in product design and production process optimization.

  • Digital Twin Technology

    Digital twin technology maps the real-world factory into a virtual space, allowing engineers to monitor, analyze, and optimize the production process in real-time, improving efficiency and quality while reducing production costs. For example, Siemens' Amberg factory has achieved comprehensive digitization and visualization of the production process through digital twin technology.

3. Service Manufacturing: The Transformation of Value Creation Models

Smart manufacturing not only changes production methods but critically reshapes the value creation models of the manufacturing industry. The transition from "product manufacturing" to "service manufacturing" signifies that the manufacturing industry is evolving from a single product provider to a comprehensive service provider.

  • The "Product as a Service" Model

    In the era of service manufacturing, business models such as "Product as a Service" (PaaS) and "Solution as a Service" (SaaS) have emerged. Manufacturing companies no longer simply sell products but provide products as a service to users, who pay for the value they receive on demand. For example, industrial equipment manufacturers can offer "Equipment as a Service" (EaaS), where users pay based on usage, and the manufacturer is responsible for maintenance, upgrades, and other services.

  • Full Lifecycle Management

    Under the service manufacturing model, manufacturing companies focus more on full lifecycle management of products, providing services at every stage from design, production, and delivery to use, maintenance, and recycling, thereby creating greater value for users. For example, by analyzing user habits through big data, manufacturers can offer personalized product optimization solutions; by remotely monitoring equipment status, manufacturers can predict potential faults in advance and provide timely maintenance services.

  • Case Study

    Siemens is a prime example of service manufacturing. Through its MindSphere Industrial IoT platform, Siemens provides customers with digital services such as remote equipment monitoring, predictive maintenance, and energy efficiency management, helping customers improve production efficiency and reduce operational costs. For instance, Siemens' predictive maintenance service for a car manufacturer analyzes equipment operational data to predict failures, reducing equipment downtime by 30% and significantly boosting production efficiency.

4. The New Era of Smart Manufacturing: The Reconstruction of Knowledge, Humans, and Value

Smart manufacturing is ushering in a new era where knowledge, humans, and value creation are redefined and reconstructed:

  • Knowledge: Transformed from an auxiliary production factor to a core driving force behind production, driving the transition from factor-driven to innovation-driven production methods.
  • Humans: From manual laborers to knowledge workers and innovators, unleashing greater potential in "human-machine collaboration."
  • Value Creation: From single product manufacturing to comprehensive service provision, the value creation model of manufacturing transitions from "product-oriented" to "service-oriented."

This revolution not only changes manufacturing production methods but critically reconfigures the roles of knowledge, humans, and value in economic activities, driving the transformation and upgrading of the entire industry. Knowledge is no longer a passive production factor but an active innovation force, leading the manufacturing industry towards a new era of intelligence, service, and knowledge. The depth and breadth of this revolution will undoubtedly have a profound impact on the entire economic system, ushering in a new era of human production methods.
