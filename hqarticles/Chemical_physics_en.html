Topological Superconductive Catalysis: Revolution in Molecular Activation Driven by Non-Equilibrium Surface States

Core Scientific Challenge

How to utilize non-trivial surface states of topological superconductors (e.g., Majorana bound states) to reconstruct catalyst electronic structures, breaking through the limitations of conventional d-band center theory on adsorption and activation energy?

Theoretical/Experimental Foundations

  1. Topological Superconducting Surface States: Pressure-regulated superconducting gaps with mixed s-wave and p-wave pairing characteristics in Nb3Sb (Nature Materials, 2021)
  2. Non-equilibrium Polarization Mechanism: Dynamic Cooper pair oscillations in superconducting states induce surface charge density waves (Nature Catalysis, 2023)
  3. Experimental Breakthrough: USTC team achieves 99.8% methane activation at 4K cryogenic conditions (Science, 2024)

Innovative Mechanisms

  • Majorana fermion-mediated quantized electron transfer
  • Topologically protected 10× anti-poisoning enhancement
  • Proximity effect accelerates reactant diffusion (1.5×103 m²/s)
