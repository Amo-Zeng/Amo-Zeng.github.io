Modular Forms in String Theory

String theory, an attempt to unify gravity and quantum mechanics, predicts the existence of higher-dimensional spaces. In string theory, Calabi-Yau manifolds are key objects describing extra dimensions. The shapes and sizes of these manifolds can be precisely described using modular forms.

The parameters of modular forms, such as the modulus and weight, correspond to different geometric features of Calabi-Yau manifolds. By studying the variations of these parameters, scientists can explore the evolution of extra dimensions and the formation of cosmic structures. For example, certain special values of modular forms can correspond to specific physical phenomena in the universe, such as the formation and evolution of black holes.
