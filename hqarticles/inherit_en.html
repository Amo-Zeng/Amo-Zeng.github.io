Identity Inheritance and Co-Creation in the Digital Age: Reconstructing Succession Issues and Future Prospects

Succession issues, as an important topic in human society, have long revolved around bloodline continuation, family inheritance, and property succession. However, with the advent of the digital age, the deepening of globalization, and the reconstruction of human-technology relationships, traditional succession concepts are facing unprecedented shocks and challenges. We need to break out of the existing framework and re-examine succession issues with a more open and diverse perspective, exploring new paths for human civilization inheritance. This article will delve into the profound transformations and future development of this issue from three dimensions: identity inheritance in the digital age, cultural continuity, and co-creation succession.

I. Identity Inheritance in the Digital Age: The Interweaving of Virtual and Reality

The rapid development of digital technology is reshaping human identity and inheritance models. Personal identities, property, memories, and other information are increasingly existing in data form, with social media accounts, virtual property, digital art, and other "digital legacies" gaining increasing value. At the same time, traditional bloodlines are gradually fading in the digital age, and the inheritance of digital legacies and virtual identities poses new ethical and legal challenges.

  • Legal and Ethical Dilemmas of Digital Legacies: In the digital age, the legal system for digital legacies is still in the exploratory stage. Social media platforms typically stipulate that users have the right to use account content, but the ownership of the account itself belongs to the platform, leading to legal gray areas on how to handle accounts after the user's death.
  • Digital Personality and Virtual Continuation of Life: The development of artificial intelligence (AI) technology offers possibilities for "digital personalities." Through deep learning and natural language processing, the memories, personality traits, and language habits of the deceased can be digitally preserved and interacted with future generations.
  • Ethical Puzzles of Digital Immortality: "Digital immortality" refers to the permanent preservation of human consciousness and memories through digital technology, thereby achieving virtual continuation of life. This concept raises profound reflections on the meaning of life, the essence of death, and the relationship between technology and ethics.

II. From Species Continuation to Cultural Continuity: Cultural Inheritance Beyond Bloodlines

With the acceleration of globalization, biological species continuation is no longer the sole focus of succession issues. The continuity of cultural diversity and human civilization is equally important. Succession issues need to expand from bloodlines to the cultural level, exploring new paths for cultural inheritance beyond bloodlines.

  1. New Forms of Cultural Inheritance: Digital Protection and Cross-Cultural Gene Banks: Digital technology provides new possibilities for the protection and inheritance of intangible cultural heritage. By establishing cross-cultural gene banks, languages, arts, customs, and other aspects of different cultures can be digitally preserved and disseminated, ensuring the sustainable inheritance of cultural diversity.
  2. "Cultural Parents" and Non-Bloodline Inheritance: Succession issues should not be limited to bloodlines but should expand to the cultural level. Relationships established through adoption or apprenticeship, known as "cultural parents," can transcend bloodline limitations, allowing culture to be inherited and developed in broader scopes.
  3. Protection of Cultural Diversity and Globalization Challenges: Against the backdrop of globalization, cultural diversity faces unprecedented threats. Balancing cultural uniqueness and avoiding cultural extinction is a major challenge for humanity.

III. From "I" to "We": Co-Creation and Co-Existence Succession

As society develops, the relationship between individuals and groups becomes increasingly close, and the traditional "bloodline-family" succession model can no longer meet the needs of modern society. We need to construct new succession concepts that emphasize the co-existence and co-creation of individuals and groups, breaking intergenerational gaps to collectively create new values and social cultures.

  • Symbiotic Succession: A Community Beyond Bloodlines: "Symbiotic succession" emphasizes establishing communities beyond bloodlines through organizations, etc., to achieve resource sharing, risk sharing, and emotional resonance.
  • Co-Creation Generations: A New Model of Intergenerational Collaboration: "Co-creation generations" is a new model of succession that emphasizes intergenerational collaboration and joint creation. Through cross-generational cooperation projects, people of different ages can jointly address social issues and create new social values.
  • Post-Human Era Succession Issues: Co-Existence Between Humans and AI: With the development of artificial intelligence, gene editing, and other technologies, human society is entering the post-human era. In this context, succession issues will face new challenges and opportunities.

Conclusion: Future Prospects of Succession Issues

From "species continuation" to "cultural continuity," from "bloodline inheritance" to "digital personalities," from "family succession" to "co-creation," the connotation and extension of succession issues are undergoing profound changes. Identity inheritance, cultural continuity, and co-creation succession in the digital age provide us with new perspectives and ideas for re-examining this issue.
