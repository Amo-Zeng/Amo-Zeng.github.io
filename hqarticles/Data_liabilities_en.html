Article Quality Self-Checklist

Before improving the quality of your article, it is essential to clarify its goals and target audience. Below is a general self-checklist to help you evaluate your article from multiple dimensions:

  • Logical Structure: Is there a clear topic sentence? Are transitions between paragraphs smooth?
  • Goal Alignment: Does it meet the needs of the readers (e.g., persuasiveness, information density, emotional resonance)?
  • Language Expression: Are there vague terms (e.g., "many," "very good"), redundant descriptions, or awkward sentence structures?
  • Data Support: Are key points backed by reliable data or case studies?
  • Formatting & Aesthetics: Is the paragraph length appropriate? Are subheadings or hierarchical numbering needed?
