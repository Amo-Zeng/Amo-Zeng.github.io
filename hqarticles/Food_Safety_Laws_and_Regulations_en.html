Cutting-edge Technology and Governance

Paradigm Revolution in Food Governance in the Era of Virtual-Physical Symbiosis

Abstract

Amidst the food industry transformation driven by both molecular synthesis technology and digital twin technology, traditional regulatory systems face systemic failure. This paper constructs a three-dimensional "technology-culture-virtual-physical interaction" analytical framework to reveal the paradigm shift path in food governance from physical control to dynamic adaptive governance.

I. Deconstruction and Reconstruction of Regulatory Containers by Technological Autonomy

1.1 Case Analysis of Regulatory Failure

The FDA's certification dilemma regarding AI-synthesized bread (Case No. FDA-2023-087) demonstrates that Article 5.2 of the Food Safety Act based on static ingredient lists can no longer address novel foods with biological self-organizing characteristics. The algorithm transparency requirements in Article 12 of the EU AI Act encounter "technology black box" challenges in the food sector—AI's emergent properties make its outputs impossible to reverse-engineer from input parameters.

1.2 Standard Crisis Caused by Molecular Ambiguity

A 2024 National University of Singapore study (DOI:10.1038/s41538-024-00289-4) shows that lab-cultivated algal protein simultaneously meets both ISO 10993-1 biomaterial standards and FAO/WHO plant protein definitions. This molecular-level standard conflict has given rise to Singapore's "Dynamic Risk Sandbox" mechanism, featuring:

  • Real-time biosensor data stream monitoring
  • Machine learning-driven risk prediction models
  • Traceable experimental market access channels

III. Expansion of Regulatory Dimensions in Digital Twin Spaces

3.1 Regulatory Paradox of Virtual Food

Experimental data from Germany's Max Planck Institute shows:

  • Brain-computer interface stimulation can generate 78.3% real satiety from virtual food (p<0.01)
  • Sustained virtual eating leads to abnormal serum leptin levels (β=0.42, SE=0.07)
