Federalism and Digital Identity Management: Balancing Unified Standards and Flexible Applications

Digital identity management is the cornerstone of digital economy development. It not only concerns personal privacy and data security but is also crucial for the smooth conduct of cross-border digital transactions. However, significant differences in the definition, standards, and regulation of digital identities among different countries and regions pose significant challenges to the global interconnectivity of the digital economy. Federalist countries, such as the European Union and the United States, exhibit unique advantages in addressing these challenges due to their distinctive political structures.
