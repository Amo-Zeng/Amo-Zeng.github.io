Governance Systems of the Han Dynasties

Technological Frameworks and Cognitive Mechanisms in Imperial Governance

A Composite Governance Framework: From Population Algorithms to Logistics Networks

The Han Empire constructed one of the most sophisticated control systems in the ancient world. Through interdisciplinary analysis of bamboo manuscripts, spatial archaeology, and iconographic records, we reveal a governance system integrating technological devices, cognitive frameworks, and spatial strategies.

Prologue: The Trinity Model of Han Governance

The household registry transformed populations into computable data, the courier network redefined imperial spacetime perception, and disaster interpretation converted natural phenomena into political capital. These systems integrated chaotic territories into an ordered empire through spatial conquest, temporal domestication, and semantic manipulation.

Foucault's "governmentality" finds historical grounding here: Han institutional innovations converted governance rationality into technical systems. The coupling of three subsystems (data flow, material flow, meaning flow) created closed-loop governance resembling modern information systems.
