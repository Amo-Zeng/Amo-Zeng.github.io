Specific Case Studies of Adaptive Environmental Sensing Systems in Accident Prevention Design

Here are several specific case studies of adaptive environmental sensing systems applied in various industrial environments.

Case Study One: Monitoring Harmful Gases in Chemical Plants

Background

The production process in chemical plants often generates harmful gases like ammonia, chlorine, etc. Leakage of these gases not only threatens the safety of operators but can also lead to severe accidents like explosions or fires. Therefore, real-time monitoring of harmful gas concentrations is crucial for safety management in chemical plants.

Technical Implementation

  1. Sensor Deployment
    • Gas Sensor Selection: Choose gas sensors with high sensitivity and low false alarm rates, typically including electrochemical sensors, catalytic combustion sensors, and infrared absorption sensors.
    • Sensor Layout: Layout sensors based on potential leak paths to ensure coverage of all possible leak points and high-risk areas.
    • Network Connectivity: Use industrial Ethernet, ZigBee, or other wireless communication protocols to ensure stable and real-time data transmission.
  2. Data Processing and Analysis
    • Data Acquisition: Real-time transmission of sensor data to the central control room via a data acquisition system (DAQ).
    • AI Algorithms:
      • Anomaly Detection: Use statistical methods (like Z-score, IQR) or machine learning algorithms (like Isolation Forest, One-class SVM) to identify anomalies.
      • Trend Prediction: Utilize time series models like ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average) or LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) to predict future concentration changes.
      • Pattern Recognition: Identify characteristic patterns of gas leaks through Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) or Support Vector Machines (SVM), such as sudden increases in concentration or changes in specific gas ratios.
  3. Automated Response Measures
    • Ventilation System Activation: Control ventilation equipment via PLC (Programmable Logic Controller) or DCS (Distributed Control System) to rapidly reduce gas concentrations.
    • Area Isolation: Automatically close valves using a valve control system based on sensor data to isolate the leak area.
    • Evacuation Instructions: Send evacuation instructions through broadcast systems or instant messaging on mobile devices to ensure safe evacuation of personnel.
