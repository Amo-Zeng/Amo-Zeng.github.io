Reimagining the Relationship Between Humans and Nature: From Climate Therapy to the Sustainable Path of Circular Economy

In the rapid development of modern society, humanity is facing unprecedented environmental challenges. Issues such as climate change, resource depletion, and ecological imbalance not only threaten the health of the Earth but also have a profound impact on human physical and mental health. To address these challenges, we need to fundamentally rethink the relationship between humans and nature and adopt comprehensive solutions.

Climate Therapy: The Healing Power of Natural Environment

Climate therapy, as an emerging treatment method, emphasizes improving human physical and mental health by enhancing environmental factors. Research shows that the aesthetic value of natural environments and the improvement of air quality have a significant impact on mental health.

  • Aesthetic Environment and Mental Health: Exposure to natural environments can significantly reduce stress levels and improve emotional states. People living near green spaces typically have better mental health. A study conducted by University College London found that green spaces in urban areas can significantly reduce symptoms of anxiety and depression in residents. Additionally, participating in natural activities such as gardening and forest bathing has been proven to effectively relieve stress and enhance overall well-being.
  • Air Quality and Mental Health: The improvement of air quality also has a profound impact on mental health. Air pollution not only affects physical health but also has a negative impact on mental health. Research has shown that long-term exposure to polluted environments significantly increases the risk of anxiety and depression. Conversely, improving air quality can enhance people's mental health. A study in Los Angeles found that after measures such as reducing vehicle emissions to improve air quality, residents' mental health conditions improved significantly.
  • "Green Prescription" Programs: Practice of Natural Therapy: The "Green Prescription" program is a typical case of climate therapy, aiming to improve physical and mental health by allowing participants to connect with nature. The program has been implemented in many countries and regions, achieving significant results. For example, the UK's "Green Prescription" program encourages doctors to prescribe "green prescriptions" for patients, recommending them to participate in natural activities such as walking and gardening. Studies have shown that patients participating in the program have significant improvements in mental health, with reduced stress and depression symptoms. Similar programs in other countries have also achieved good results, further demonstrating the positive impact of natural environments on mental health.

Environmental Education: Shifting from "Protection" to "Symbiosis"

Facing the increasingly severe global environmental challenges, the limitations of the traditional "protection" concept are gradually emerging. It separates humans from nature, emphasizing human's unilateral responsibility and ignoring the inseparable symbiotic relationship between humans and nature.   • Emergence of the "Symbiosis" Concept: "Symbiosis" is not merely about "harmonious coexistence" but emphasizes a dynamic balance of mutual dependence and mutual promotion between humans and nature. Under the guidance of the "symbiosis" concept, environmental education is no longer a one-sided "protection" lecture but instead guides students to rethink the relationship between humans and nature, igniting their passion and creativity for sustainable development.
  • Technology-Empowered Environmental Education: Technology-empowerment provides new possibilities for environmental education. For example, using virtual reality technology to simulate ecosystems such as tropical rainforests and polar glaciers allows students to experience the beauty of nature and feel the vulnerability and complexity of ecosystems, thus more intuitively understanding the practical significance of the "symbiosis" concept. Another advantage of technology-empowered environmental education is that it provides more opportunities for students to participate in practice. For example, using online platforms for environmental data analysis, conducting virtual ecological experiments, and participating in environmental protection projects allow students to learn knowledge, develop skills, and cultivate their sense of responsibility and action in practice.

Circular Economy: The Path to Sustainable Development in the Post-Petroleum Era

The traditional "pollute first, treat later" development model is no longer sustainable. Facing the increasingly pressing issues of resource depletion and environmental pollution, building a sustainable circular economy system has become an inevitable path for human society in the post-petroleum era.

  • Concept and Practice of Circular Economy: The circular economy is not simply about recycling waste but focuses on the efficient use and recycling of resources, adhering to the principles of "reduce, reuse, recycle," and ecologically designing economic activities, fundamentally disrupting the traditional linear economic model.
  • Resource Utilization and Waste Management: The circular economy emphasizes the regeneration of resources, treating waste as "urban minerals." In the future, cities will become "super recycling factories," where waste electrical appliances, construction waste, and household waste will be efficiently sorted, dismantled, and extracted into new resources such as metal, plastic, and paper, returning to the production chain and realizing the cyclic regeneration of resources. Waste will no longer be a burden on the environment but a valuable resource. Under the circular economy model, waste management will achieve "zero landfill," with kitchen waste used for composting, plastic waste for producing new materials, and hazardous waste such as batteries treated and recycled harmlessly.
  • Industrial Structure and Technological Innovation: The circular economy will drive the transformation and upgrading of traditional industries, constructing closed-loop production models from "farm to factory." Deep integration of agriculture and industry will turn by-products such as crop straw and livestock manure into raw materials for industrial products such as biofuels and organic fertilizers, realizing cross-industry resource recycling. Technological innovation is the engine for the development of the circular economy. In the future, smart homes and green transportation will become the norm. Smart home systems will achieve intelligent resource management to reduce waste, while new energy vehicles will reduce dependence on fossil fuels and promote green transportation development.

Conclusion: The Path to Symbiosis Between Humans and Nature
