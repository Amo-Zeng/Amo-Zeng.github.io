Just as the prophecies of Cassandra in ancient Greece, if people could early on perceive potential crises and take action, many tragedies might be avoided. The preventive nature of safety awareness reminds us: Murphy's Law is no mere saying; any potential risk, no matter how small, can lead to disaster.

At the same time, safety awareness is also the cornerstone of psychological resilience. In an era of information explosion and frequent risks, cultivating safety awareness helps us maintain calm and reason when faced with sudden threats. III. The Vulnerability of Civilization and the Responsibility of Safety Awareness

The complexity of modern societal systems determines their vulnerability. The tight interweaving of systems like electricity, transportation, the internet, and healthcare means that a small safety flaw can trigger a chain reaction, leading to unimaginable disasters. As seen in the 2003 North American blackout, a single fallen tree could plunge millions into darkness. This vulnerability reveals a profound reality: safety awareness is not just a technical issue but a necessary question of social governance.

In this context, the responsibility of safety awareness extends from the individual to the entire society. Each person is a node in the social system, and only when everyone possesses sufficient safety awareness can the security of the entire system be ensured. This requires education to embed safety awareness into everyone's way of thinking; it requires businesses to prioritize safety as a prerequisite for innovation; it requires governments to establish stricter safety standards and emergency response systems.
