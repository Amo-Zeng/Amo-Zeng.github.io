Gravitational Waves: An Rhythm Detector of the Universe and a Bridge for Multi-Messenger Astronomy

Gravitational Waves: An Rhythm Detector of the Universe

Gravitational waves, ripples in the fabric of spacetime, have become a new tool for humanity to explore the mysteries of the universe since their first direct detection in 2015. They not only verified Einstein's predictions in general relativity but also, in their unique way, connected micro and macro physics, ushering in a new era of multi-messenger astronomy.

Gravitational Waves: The "Heartbeat" and "Breath" of the Universe

Gravitational waves can capture the subtle vibrations of the universe's most mysterious objects. The "heartbeat" and "breath" signals of black holes and neutron stars reveal the internal dynamical processes and evolutionary clues of these objects.

Gravitational Waves: A Bridge Connecting Micro and Macro

Gravitational waves not only reveal the macroscopic rhythms of the universe but also provide crucial clues to exploring micro physics. Major scientific issues such as quantum gravity, the origin of the universe, and the black hole information paradox may be answered through gravitational wave research.
