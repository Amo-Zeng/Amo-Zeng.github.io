Introduction: Transcending Passive Observation

Traditional sky surveys face three fundamental limitations: The Square Kilometre Array (SKA) generates 20 PB of annual data (exceeding conventional processing thresholds), gravitational wave detection has a decade-long sensitivity gap in the 0.1-10 Hz range, and multi-messenger event response times exceed 30 seconds. Quantum Metasurfaces (QMS), Cold Atom Interferometers (CAI), and the AutoMapper system form a technological triad addressing signal acquisition, frequency coverage, and real-time decision-making. This analysis explores the paradigm shift through hardware reconfiguration, spectral expansion, and intelligent systems.
