Background Radiation: The Ultimate Archive of the Universe and the Gateway to Parallel Universes

Cosmic background radiation is hailed as the "embers of the Big Bang," permeating every corner of the universe and recording the earliest distribution of matter and structure formation. It is not just an archive of the past but also a prophecy for the future. High-precision observation instruments reveal subtle temperature fluctuations and polarization patterns in the background radiation, providing a glimpse of early universe's matter distribution. However, the potential of this information remains largely untapped.

Within the small fluctuations of background radiation, more astonishing secrets might be hidden: traces of parallel universes. According to the multiverse theory, our universe is just one of countless parallel universes, and these universes might leave unique quantum imprints from collisions. These imprints might exist as specific frequency patterns or polarization directions in background radiation, waiting for us to interpret. By analyzing these imprints, we might be able to detect the existence of other parallel universes and even build a "multiverse navigation system" to explore the multiverse.

The Singularity and Parallel Universes: The Ultimate Prophecy in Background Radiation
