Multidimensional Perspectives of Ethnicity: The Interweaving of Globalization, Historical Memory, and Economic Models

Under the impact of globalization, ethnic cultures, historical memories, and economic models are facing unprecedented challenges and opportunities. This article will explore the multidimensional development of ethnicity in modern society from three perspectives: ethnic identity and cultural identity under the context of globalization, ethnic historical memory and modern social construction, and ethnic economic models and sustainable development. Through the intertwining of these three perspectives, we can gain a more comprehensive understanding of the complexity and diversity of ethnicity in modern society.

Ethnic Identity and Cultural Identity under the Context of Globalization

In the context of globalization, ethnic cultures face both the risk of cultural homogenization and the opportunity of cultural dissemination and exchange. To maintain its uniqueness, ethnic culture must adhere to its cultural core while making appropriate innovations and developments. For example, many ethnic music and dances, while retaining traditional elements, incorporate modern music and dance elements, making them more contemporary and appealing.

Cultural exchange and integration are inevitable trends. Ethnic cultures should actively engage in exchanges with other cultures, absorbing the excellent elements of other cultures to enrich their own cultural connotations. For instance, Chinese New Year, India's Diwali, and other ethnic festivals have become significant cultural events worldwide, attracting people from all over the world to participate and experience.

However, cultural exchange and integration do not mean the disappearance of ethnic cultures. On the contrary, through exchanges and integration with other cultures, ethnic cultures can gain new vitality and development momentum. For example, Japanese anime culture is widely popular globally, not weakening its ethnic characteristics but further expanding its cultural influence.

Ethnic Historical Memory and Modern Social Construction

In modern society, ethnic historical memory is not only an essential component of ethnic identity but also a key factor in social construction. However, with the passage of time and social changes, ethnic historical memory is often influenced and reshaped by political, economic, cultural, and other factors. This reshaping not only alters people's perception of history but also profoundly affects ethnic identity and social stability.

Political factors play a significant role in the reshaping of ethnic historical memory. Governments and political groups often shape and disseminate specific historical memories through education, media, and public commemorative activities. For example, some countries emphasize the glorious history and heroic figures of their own ethnicity through textbooks and history courses to enhance national pride and cohesion. However, this politicized historical memory can also lead to exclusion and hostility towards other ethnicities, causing social conflicts and ethnic tensions.

Economic factors also have a profound impact on the reshaping of ethnic historical memory. Driven by globalization and market economy, many ethnic traditional lifestyles and economic models have undergone significant changes. This change not only alters people's lifestyles but also affects their understanding and memory of history. For example, some ethnic traditional handicrafts and agricultural techniques gradually lose competitiveness in the modern economy, leading to the marginalization and forgetting of these ethnic historical memories.

Cultural factors also play an important role in the reshaping of ethnic historical memory. With the exchange and integration of global cultures, many ethnic cultural traditions and customs are influenced by foreign cultures. This cultural impact not only changes ethnic cultural identity but also affects their memory and understanding of history. For example, some ethnic traditional festivals and rituals are gradually being diluted in modern society, replaced by global cultural activities and consumerist holidays.
