I. Basic Principles of Tuina

Tuina, also known as massage, is one of the external treatment methods in traditional Chinese medicine. It involves manual techniques applied to specific parts of the body to achieve the purpose of treating diseases and strengthening the body. The theoretical foundation of Tuina mainly comes from the meridian theory and organ theory in traditional Chinese medicine, which believe that the health of the human body is closely related to the smoothness of meridians and the harmony of qi and blood. Through Tuina, qi and blood can be promoted, yin and yang can be balanced, thus achieving the effect of treating diseases.

II. Mechanisms of Action of Tuina

1. **Promoting Circulation**: Tuina, through techniques applied to the skin, muscles, and meridians, promotes blood circulation, accelerates metabolism, and helps the body eliminate toxins.

2. **Relieving Muscle Tension**: Tuina can relax muscles, relieving fatigue and tension caused by long hours of work or exercise.

3. **Regulating the Nervous System**: Tuina stimulates specific acupoints to regulate the function of the autonomic nervous system, reducing stress and anxiety.

4. **Enhancing Immunity**: Tuina promotes the flow of lymph fluid, strengthening the immune system and increasing the body's resistance.

III. How to Unlock the Body's Self-Healing Potential Through Tuina

1. **Regular Tuina**: Regular Tuina treatments can maintain the balance of the body, preventing diseases.

2. **Self-Tuina**: Learning simple Tuina techniques allows self-regulation at home, relieving daily fatigue and discomfort.

3. **Combining with Other Therapies**: Tuina can be used in conjunction with other therapies such as acupuncture and herbal medicine to enhance treatment effects.
