The Quantum Nature of Spacetime: From Continuity to Discreteness

These spacetime quanta are considered the fundamental units of the universe, with their geometric structure and interaction rules determining the topological properties and the mechanism of gravity.

A central idea of quantum gravity is the discreteness of spacetime. At the Planck scale, the geometric properties of spacetime no longer adhere to classical geometric rules but are governed by quantum fluctuations and uncertainty principles. The quantum gravity field exhibits complex entanglement structures at this microscopic scale, which not only determine the topological properties of spacetime but may also be closely related to information theory. For example, research in quantum information theory suggests that the quantum information capacity of spacetime might determine the overall information-carrying capacity of the universe, providing new directions for resolving the black hole information paradox.

Further research reveals that interactions between spacetime quanta might be realized through quantum entanglement networks, with the network's topological structure determining both local and global spacetime properties. The quantum gravity field plays a central role within this network, akin to a global quantum field, regulating the evolution of spacetime's quantum states. By understanding the morphology, interactions, and quantum information characteristics of spacetime quanta, we can unlock the mysteries of the quantum nature of spacetime and lay a solid foundation for the development of quantum gravity theory.
