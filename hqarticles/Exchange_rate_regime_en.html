Monetary Undercurrents in Geopolitical Fragmentation

Introduction: Paradigm Shift in Monetary Order

The accelerating fragmentation of global geopolitics is shaking the Bretton Woods monetary legacy in unprecedented ways. Against the backdrop of expanding cracks in U.S. dollar hegemony, underground payment networks controlled by non-state actors accounted for 15.3% of global cross-border capital flows in 2023 (IMF estimates). The resulting "shadow exchange rate system" transmits volatility to sovereign financial markets through complex network effects.
