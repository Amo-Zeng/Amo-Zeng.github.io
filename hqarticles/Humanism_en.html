The Triple Deconstruction and Reconstruction of 21st Century Humanism: Dialectical Breakthroughs in the Recursive Era

Introduction: The Crisis and Reconstruction of Humanism in the Technological Singularity Era

When Elon Musk's Neuralink team successfully implanted chips into monkey cerebral cortices in 2023, enabling paralyzed patients to control robotic arms through neural signals, human civilization quietly crossed an invisible threshold - the boundary between flesh and technology is no longer defined by biological evolution, but increasingly reshaped by algorithms and capital. This epoch is termed the "Age of Recursion": the three pillars of traditional humanism - "rational subject," "anthropocentric ethics," and "mind-body dualism" - are being deconstructed by technological self-iteration. Bruno Latour's "non-human actors" have expanded beyond laboratory instruments to include AlphaFold's protein prediction models, ChatGPT's language generation systems, and TikTok's recommendation algorithms; Peter Sloterdijk's "Anthroposphere" theory is forced to confront "Hyperobjects" like melting ice caps and species extinction; while Quentin Meillassoux's "speculative realism" predicts the inevitability of posthuman cognition: As neural networks become extensions of consciousness, "being" itself undergoes a cosmic-scale topological metamorphosis.

The core of this triple crisis lies in: algorithms seizing cognitive sovereignty, ecological collapse reshaping ethical boundaries, and neural reconstruction subverting existential foundations. If traditional humanism persists in clinging to Enlightenment-era dogmas, it will inevitably become instrumental rhetoric for technological capitalism. Only by reconstructing a new paradigm centered on "recursiveness," achieving dialectical breakthroughs through deconstruction, can humanism fulfill its ultimate mission of safeguarding human dignity in the 21st century.

Algorithmic Cognitive Colonization: Disenchantment of the Rational Subject and Reconstruction of Cognitive Sovereignty

In the Cambridge Analytica case involving Facebook data manipulation of global elections, algorithms have transcended mere tools to become the most powerful cognitive power apparatus of our time. The essence of "Algorithmic Cognitive Colonization" lies in its simultaneous deconstruction of Cartesian "cogito" and Kantian transcendental apperception - when recommendation systems shape human thought patterns through behavioral data, rational autonomy yields to the manipulation of probabilistic models. French philosopher Bernard Stiegler observes: "Google's PageRank algorithm constitutes digital Prometheanism, outsourcing human cognition to technological systems and reducing memory to computable commodities."

Yet crisis breeds opportunity. Cognitive reconstruction based on "Algorithmic Reflexivity" is emerging: the EU's Digital Services Act enforces algorithmic transparency, while Germany's AI Ethics Commission proposes the principle of "Inalienable Cognitive Sovereignty," both attempting to re-anchor deconstructed subjectivity within public frameworks of technological governance. Within recursive cognitive labyrinths, humanism must learn multilevel game-theoretic dialogues with technological systems.

Cross-Species Mourning Community: Topological Expansion of Anthropocentric Ethics

New Zealand's 2023 Natural Entities Rights Act granting legal personhood to the Wellington Harbour ecosystem, and California's "Whale Song Archive" project decoding humpback whale mourning rituals through acoustic data collection, signal ethical relations breaking free from anthropocentric shackles. Ecological collapse is not merely physical catastrophe but a structural crisis of human ethical imagination.

Through distributed computing of empathic algorithms, cross-media ecological art narratives, and radical political practices like "posthumous suffrage," we construct asymmetric yet reciprocal responsibility matrices. Here, mourning becomes not a sign of weakness, but synaptic connections binding all vulnerable lifeforms.

Neuralization of Posthuman Identity: Dissolution of Mind-Body Continuum and Ontological Experimentation

Brain-computer interface technology reveals deeper alienation - when dopamine secretion becomes precisely controlled by closed-loop neural modulation systems, "subjectivity" transforms into programmable variables. OpenAI's 2024 "Mind Mapping Protocol," generating thought imagery through neural signal reverse-engineering, pushes consciousness to the frontier of technological colonization.

The Shanghai Institute of Neuroscience's "Neuro-constellation" project demonstrates that implanted mouse neurons can form trans-medium synaptic connections with external cerebral organoids. This suggests being is essentially a recursive morphogenetic process, thus "Neuro-Dialectics" demands a practical philosophy bridging carbon-based life and silicon-based systems.

Three Pillars of Recursive Humanism and Cosmic Ethical Awakening

  • Algorithmic Reflexivity: Citizen-politicization of data sovereignty through distributed cognitive protocols
  • Trauma Community Topology: Constructing planetary-scale symbiotic responsibility networks
  • Neuro-Dialectics: Technology liberation pathways grounded in embodied experience

As humanity observes glacial melt through Starlink satellites and reconstructs extinct species' habitats in the metaverse, a new humanism emerges: "All entities are measurers in the cosmic recursive process".
