New Perspectives on the Israel-Palestine Conflict from Dual Perspectives of Holy City Struggle and Technological Environment

Exploring Multi-Dimensional Aspects of the Israel-Palestine Conflict

The Israel-Palestine conflict is not only a battleground for territory and politics but also deeply rooted in cultural heritage, identity, environmental resources, and technological media. This article will explore the deep-seated contradictions of the Israel-Palestine conflict from the perspectives of cultural heritage, the technological influence of social media, and environmental cooperation, proposing a new integrated solution.

I. Cultural Heritage and Identity: The Dual Dilemma of the Holy City

Jerusalem, as a shared holy site for Judaism, Christianity, and Islam, carries profound historical memories and religious sentiments. The struggle for control over the Temple Mount/Al-Aqsa Mosque embodies the conflict of identity. A possible path to resolving this dilemma lies in seeking cultural coexistence, managing the holy site jointly, and implementing protection policies to alleviate identity conflicts.

III. Environmental Cooperation: A Peaceful Opportunity from Shared Water Resources

Environmental issues offer a new perspective on resolving the Israel-Palestine conflict. The competition for water resources in the Jordan River Basin and land resources exacerbates the local humanitarian crisis. Environmental cooperation could become a new path to resolving the conflict, creating conditions for addressing humanitarian issues by managing water resources and protecting the environment.

IV. Comprehensive Solution: The Integration of Cultural Coexistence, Technological Bridge, and Environmental Cooperation

Resolving the Israel-Palestine conflict requires a multi-dimensional comprehensive solution. The integration of cultural coexistence, technological bridge, and environmental cooperation can provide new insights for this century-old conflict, transcending historical hatreds and moving towards a shared future.
