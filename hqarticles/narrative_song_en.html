The Revival of Narrative Poetry: A Narrative Revolution from Literature to Virtual Reality

Introduction

Narrative Poetry, a literary form that integrates poetic rhythm with narrative content, has occupied a unique position in literary history since ancient times. However, with the diversification of modern literary forms, narrative poetry has gradually been supplanted by novels and other literary genres in the literary realm. Despite this, the unique narrative style and emotional expression of narrative poetry have not disappeared; instead, they have found new life in video games, AI-generated content, and the reflection of social issues. This article explores the revival and application of narrative poetry in modern and future narrative arts from these three perspectives, as well as its potential as a tool for social critique.

The Revival of Narrative Poetry in Video Games

In video game narratives, elements of narrative poetry have been rediscovered and applied, especially in some narrative-driven games. "What Remains of Edith Finch" and "Edith Finch's Memory" are prime examples of this trend. These games draw on the narrative rhythm and emotional expression of narrative poetry, transforming static narrative texts into dynamic immersive experiences through player interaction and decision-making.

  • Player Agency and Environmental Narrative Integration: In traditional narrative poetry, readers can only passively accept the story. In video games, however, players can influence the direction of the story through interaction and decision-making. This enhanced player agency makes the narrative rhythm and emotional expression of narrative poetry more personalized.
  • Prospects of Virtual Reality Technology: With the continuous development of virtual reality (VR) technology, the application of narrative poetry in video game narratives has an even broader future. VR technology provides players with a more immersive experience, making the narrative rhythm and emotional expression of narrative poetry more realistic and personalized.

AI-Generated Narrative Poetry: The Intersection of Technology and Art

With the rapid development of artificial intelligence technology, AI-generated narrative poetry has become a notable new field. Through algorithms and machine learning, AI can create stories with complex plots and rich characters, bringing new possibilities to traditional storytelling methods.

  1. Technical Analysis: The core technologies of AI-generated narrative poetry include Natural Language Processing (NLP) and machine learning. NLP enables computers to understand, parse, and generate human language, which is the foundation of AI-generated stories.
  2. Pros and Cons Analysis: Although AI-generated narrative poetry has advantages such as high generation efficiency, personalized customization, and innovation, it still faces challenges in emotional depth, creative limitations, and ethical issues.
  3. Future Outlook: In the future, the potential of AI-generated narrative poetry is enormous. AI can serve as an auxiliary tool for human writers, helping them generate story outlines, character settings, and plot developments, thereby improving the efficiency and quality of creation.

The Reflection of Narrative Poetry on Social Issues

As an ancient literary form, narrative poetry has always reflected and explored social injustices, prejudices, and contradictions. From Chaucer's "The Canterbury Tales" to Thackeray's "Vanity Fair," narrative poetry weaves social issues into stories through metaphors, symbols, and other rhetorical devices, transmitting critique and reflection subtly and gradually.

  • Case Analysis: Classic narrative poetry works such as Kafka's "The Metamorphosis," George Orwell's "1984," and Margaret Atwood's "The Handmaid's Tale" reveal issues of alienation, totalitarianism, and gender inequality in modern society through surreal plots and complex symbolic techniques.
  • Realistic Significance and Future Prospects: In contemporary society, narrative poetry continues to play its social critique and reflective value. Future narrative poetry will enrich its narrative layers by combining literature, film, music, digital art, and other media through cross-media narrative methods, enhancing its emotional impact and ideological resonance with the audience.

Conclusion

As a narrative art form, narrative poetry has found new vitality in video games, AI-generated content, and the reflection of social issues. Through the application of player agency, environmental narrative, and VR technology, the revival of narrative poetry in video game narratives provides players with more immersive and personalized experiences. AI-generated narrative poetry brings new possibilities to story creation, although it still faces challenges such as emotional depth and creative limitations, its future development potential is enormous. Additionally, the potential of narrative poetry as a tool for social critique cannot be ignored. Through complex symbols and multi-layered storytelling, narrative poetry continues to reflect and explore the complex issues of contemporary society. Future narrative poetry will further enrich its forms through cross-media narrative methods and VR technology, becoming a powerful tool for promoting social equality and justice.
