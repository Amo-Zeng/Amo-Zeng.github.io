Theatrical Production of Perceptual Thresholds

This design simulates the neural competition mechanism in the parietal cortex during spatial localization processing. When superior temporal sulcus and premotor cortex receive conflicting signals, the perceptual system enters a "desynchronized dormant state". This neural-level violence mapped onto theatrical space materializes Wölfflin's "planar depth paradox" as a navigable topological field.
