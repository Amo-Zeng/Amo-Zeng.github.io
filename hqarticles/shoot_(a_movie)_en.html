Exploring Multidimensional Vision: Photography Art from Underwater to Mirrors to Architectural Frames

Photography art is a visual exploration and creation, opening up a new world beyond daily perception through various techniques and perspectives. In this article, we will delve into three unique photographic techniques—underwater perspective, mirror reflection, and architectural framing—revealing how they collectively build a dreamy yet profound visual experience.

Underwater Perspective: Creating a Dream World

Underwater photography brings a unique dream-like effect to photography. Shooting upwards from beneath the water, people and objects appear to float on the water surface, creating a surreal atmosphere. This technique requires:

  • Waterproof Equipment: Use a waterproof camera or waterproof housing, paired with a wide-angle lens to capture expansive underwater landscapes.
  • Light Control: Considering the attenuation of light underwater, use underwater strobes or LED lights to adjust the lighting.
  • Perspective Effects: Enhance the floating sensation using water surface ripples, suspended particles, and light refraction.

Through this approach, photographers not only showcase technical finesse but also express the blurred lines between dreams and reality, conveying feelings of tranquility, peace, or even solitude.

Mirror Reflection: The Charm of Multiple Visions

Mirrors play a unique role in photography, capturing reality through reflection and creating multiple visual effects, providing an infinitely extending visual world.

  • Angle and Position: The placement angle and position of the mirror determine how the reflection is portrayed, creating a sense of depth and space.
  • Light Control: The intensity and angle of light are crucial in mirror reflections, capable of producing different light and shadow effects.
  • Multiplicity of Reflections: Multiple reflections can present a complex visual structure in a single frame, enhancing the artistic appeal of the image.

This method is not only technically challenging but also symbolizes multiple cognitions of self and the world, expressing complex emotions and thoughts.

Inside and Outside Architecture: Viewing the World Through Frames

Architecture serves as the stage for human activities, with its internal structure providing a unique framed perspective looking outward.

  • Framing Effect: Use elements like doors and windows of architecture as visual guides, combining the external landscape with the internal space to create a dialogue between time and space.
  • Depth and Storytelling: This shooting method captures visual depth, showcasing the relationship between human life and the environment.
  • Technical and Artistic: Choose the right time and lens, utilizing natural light and depth of field to enhance the narrative and visual impact of the photo.

Viewing the external world through the architectural frame is not just a technical display but also an expression of emotion and thought, revealing the interaction between humans and their environment.

Comprehensive Artistic Expression

Combining these three photographic techniques, we can see how they together construct a multidimensional visual space:

  • Merging Fantasy with Reality: Underwater perspective and mirror reflection create a dream-like world, while architectural framing provides an inside-out perspective, blending reality with fantasy.
  • Exploring Multiple Visions: Through the multiple visual effects of mirror reflections and the visual guidance of architectural framing, we see the multifaceted nature and infinite extension of the world in photographs.
  • Unifying Technique and Art: Each technique requires precise light control, angle selection, and post-processing, but ultimately presents a unique understanding of beauty and a reinterpretation of the world.

By merging these three photographic techniques, photographers not only push technical boundaries but also explore the limits of human perception and understanding. Each shot tells a story, and each frame is a world, inviting viewers to immerse themselves in this visual feast, exploring and feeling the multidimensional nature and aesthetic pursuit of life.
