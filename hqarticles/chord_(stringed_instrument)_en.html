Quantum Entanglement: The Ghost of the Quantum World

Quantum entanglement is one of the most mysterious phenomena in quantum mechanics, describing the spatiotemporal correlations that exist between two or more particles. Regardless of the distance, measuring one particle instantly affects the state of another, this "spooky action at a distance" challenges our understanding of the nature of reality. Quantum entanglement is not only the foundation of quantum information processing and quantum computing but also provides important clues for exploring the early state of the universe and the internal structure of black holes.
