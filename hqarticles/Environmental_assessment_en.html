Theoretical Reconstruction: Dissolving Species Boundaries in Cognition

Donna Haraway's Cyborg Manifesto deconstructed the binary narrative of organisms and machines, while Bruno Latour's Actor-Network Theory (ANT) reveals how humans, salmon, hydropower plants, and sedimentary rocks form an interconnected web of actants. New Understandings of Material Expression

When Arctic glaciers undergo structural collapse due to surpassing temperature thresholds, their "interests" manifest materially through hydrological shifts and species migration. French philosopher Jean-Luc Marion's concept of "givenness" illustrates how trout express their existential will through reduced spawning rates when migration routes are blocked.

Challenges and Tensions: Measuring the Immeasurable

Germany's "Secondary Perception Metrics" attempt to model ecological suffering, while China's panda reserve wind farm project exposes dilemmas in adjudicating heterogeneous rights. Yellowstone National Park cases warn against ecological populism. (Theoretical basis: Morton's Nature as Other, 2019)

Conclusion: Reframing Assessment's Ultimate Question

When the core issue shifts from "is development sustainable" to "which species has the right to define sustainability," this transformation becomes humanity's ultimate reflection on its own existential paradigm.
