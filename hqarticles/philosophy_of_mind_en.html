The Architecture of Consciousness

A Multidimensional Integration Theory of Self, Emotion, and Reality Perception

Introduction: A Paradigm Shift in Consciousness Research

In the revolutionary progress of cognitive science, the traditional Cartesian mind-body dualism is giving way to more dynamic theories of consciousness construction. This paper proposes an integrative framework that views self-cognition (selfhood), emotional intelligence, and reality modeling as the three pillars of the consciousness system. Through cross-validation of neuroscientific empirical research and phenomenological analysis, we reveal that consciousness is essentially a high-order predictive system evolved by the brain to adapt to complex environments. Its three subsystems are relatively independent yet operate synergistically, collectively constructing the unique subjective experience of humans.

I. Neurophilosophical Analysis of Self-Construction: From Entity Illusion to Dynamic Narrative

1. Revolutionary Discoveries in Neuroscience

  • The prefrontal-parietal network constitutes the "default mode network" (DMN), continuously generating self-narratives
  • The somatic marker hypothesis (Damasio, 1999) reveals the foundational role of bodily sensations in self-awareness
  • Experimental evidence: the rubber hand illusion demonstrates the plasticity of self-boundaries

2. Deconstruction and Reconstruction of Philosophical Traditions

  • Comparative analysis: Descartes' entity self vs. Buddhism's "anatta" (no-self) doctrine
  • Process philosophy perspective: Whiteheadian "relational self"
  • Narrative identity theory (Ricoeur): the self as a continuously revised life story

3. Insights from Clinical Psychology

  • Self-fragmentation in patients with dissociative disorders
  • Loss of autobiographical memory and identity deconstruction in Alzheimer's patients
  • Practical value: new paradigms for psychological intervention based on self-plasticity

    The integrative framework proposed in this paper transcends traditional disciplinary boundaries, advancing consciousness research to a new stage of "4E cognition" (embodied, embedded, enacted, extended). Future research should focus on:
