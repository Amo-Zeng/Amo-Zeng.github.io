From "Sense of Security" to "Security Power": Shaping a Multidimensional Security Landscape for the Future

From "Sense of Security" to "Security Power": The Evolution of Security Concepts

In today's rapidly changing world, security is no longer merely a passive defense against external threats, but an active, multidimensional, and collaborative capability. From individuals to nations, from physical spaces to digital twins, human society is undergoing a profound transformation in security concepts. This transformation is not only about responding to known threats but also about identifying, predicting, and mitigating potential risks, turning challenges into opportunities, and building a robust barrier capable of addressing future uncertainties.

From "Sense of Security" to "Security Power": The Evolution of Security Concepts

Traditional security concepts are often confined to the realm of "Sense of Security," viewing security as a passive state. This approach emphasizes building defenses and strengthening protection to ward off external threats. While this may have been effective in relatively stable environments, global and information-driven advancements today have made security threats increasingly complex and ever-changing, making traditional passive defense models inadequate.

"Security Power" represents a proactive security approach, transcending the passive defense of "Sense of Security" and emphasizing the ability to actively shape the security environment. "Security Power" focuses not only on responding to known threats but also on identifying, predicting, and mitigating potential risks, transforming security challenges into development opportunities, thereby achieving higher-quality and sustainable security.

The Three Pillars of "Security Power": Technology, Institutions, and Education

Building "Security Power" requires the support of three pillars: technology, institutions, and education:

  • Technology Empowerment: Building an Intelligent Security System

    Technology is a crucial support for "Security Power." Emerging technologies such as artificial intelligence, big data, and cloud computing have brought revolutionary changes to the security field. For example, in the field of cybersecurity, "offensive and defensive drills" are no longer simple simulations but are conducted using AI technology to build intelligent offensive and defensive platforms, which can autonomously discover and fix security vulnerabilities, enhancing the proactiveness and precision of network defense. In urban safety, "smart governance" relies on technologies like the Internet of Things and cloud computing to construct a three-dimensional, intelligent urban safety management system, enabling real-time monitoring and risk warning of city operations and eliminating potential hazards at the early stage.

  • Institutional Guarantee: Building a Comprehensive Security Governance System

    The enhancement of "Security Power" requires robust institutional support. It is necessary to establish and improve relevant laws and regulations, clarify security responsibilities, and regulate security behaviors, providing legal support for the construction of "Security Power." Additionally, mechanisms for risk assessment, emergency management, and information sharing need to be established and improved, building a top-down, coordinated, and efficient security governance system to enhance the ability to respond to complex security challenges.

  • Education Guidance: Building Public Security Awareness

    The enhancement of "Security Power" depends on the improvement of public security awareness. Security education should be strengthened, integrating security awareness into all stages of national education, guiding people to establish correct security concepts, acquire necessary security knowledge and skills, and enhance self-protection abilities. Additionally, professional talent development is crucial to provide a talent base for the construction of "Security Power."

From "Single Security" to "Comprehensive Security": Building a Modern Security System with Multidimensional Collaboration

Human society is experiencing unprecedented changes, and the security landscape is also exhibiting new characteristics. The traditional single-security perspective can no longer address increasingly complex security challenges. Building a "Comprehensive Security" framework and achieving cross-domain collaboration have become the inevitable choice for maintaining national security and social stability in the new era.

  • The Limitations of "Single Security" are Increasingly Evident

    For a long time, security issues have been simply categorized into different fields, such as military security, political security, and economic security. This "single security" mindset, with its narrow focus, is unable to address the complexity and interconnections of security issues in a globalized context.

    • Fragmented Response: Each security field operates independently, lacking overall coordination and integration, making it difficult to form a cohesive force and often leading to imbalances. For instance, during the COVID-19 pandemic, the conflict between public health security and economic security became evident, and balancing the two posed a significant challenge.
    • Lagging Response: "Single security" is often limited to known threats, lacking foresight and response to new security risks, making it difficult to prevent potential issues in advance. For example, cybersecurity threats have become increasingly severe, and traditional security defense methods are no longer effective.
    • Single-Interest Orientation: "Single security" can lead to parochialism, overlooking the overall and interconnected nature of security issues, making true security difficult to achieve. For example, the conflict between energy security and environmental protection requires a more macro perspective for coordination.
  • The "Comprehensive Security" Framework: Addressing Complex Challenges with Systems Thinking

    The "Comprehensive Security" framework emphasizes combining traditional and non-traditional security, breaking down disciplinary barriers, and achieving cross-domain collaboration to address increasingly complex security challenges.

    • Holistic Perspective: The "Comprehensive Security" framework places security issues within a broader temporal and spatial dimension, incorporating politics, economics, culture, society, ecology, and other fields into the security consideration, building a comprehensive, multi-layered, and broad-spectrum security system.
    • Collaborative Coordination: The "Comprehensive Security" framework highlights the interconnections and interdependencies among various security fields, achieving joint prevention and control of security risks through information sharing, resource integration, and mechanism coordination.
    • Dynamic Balance: The "Comprehensive Security" framework focuses on seeking a dynamic balance among different security fields, avoiding imbalances and achieving the maximization of security benefits. For example, during the COVID-19 pandemic, it was necessary to ensure public health security while also maintaining economic security and social stability.

From "Physical Space" to "Digital Twin": Reshaping the Urban Security Landscape

Cities, as the crystallization of human civilization, are full of vitality and opportunities. However, with the acceleration of urbanization, the increasing population density and resource constraints have made various security challenges increasingly severe. Natural disasters, traffic accidents, terrorist attacks... These threats hang like the Sword of Damocles over cities, constantly reminding us of the vulnerability of urban security.

Faced with these challenges, traditional security management models are becoming increasingly inadequate. Relying on experience and human patrols is no longer sufficient to address the increasingly complex and ever-changing security landscape. The rapid development of digital technologies, particularly the rise of digital twin technology, provides us with a key to unlocking the future of urban security.

Digital twin, as the name suggests, is the creation of a highly consistent virtual model of the real world in the digital realm. It is like a "mirror" of the real world, reflecting every detail and change in the physical space into the digital space, creating a "living" and dynamic urban model.

Using digital twin technology, we can extend the perspective of urban safety management from the physical space to the digital space, opening up a new paradigm for safety governance:

  • Predicting Risks, Preventing Emergencies: By integrating data from various urban domains, digital twins can simulate scenarios such as natural disasters and traffic accidents, predicting potential dangers and formulating emergency plans in advance. For example, using historical meteorological data and geographical information, digital twins can predict flood paths, providing scientific decision-making support for flood prevention and disaster relief.
  • Real-Time Monitoring, Rapid Response: Digital twins can monitor urban operating status in real time, identifying abnormal situations promptly. For instance, in the transportation field, digital twins can integrate traffic flow, weather conditions, and road conditions, monitor traffic operations in real time, identify congestion and accidents, and automatically trigger emergency response mechanisms.
  • Optimizing Resources, Enhancing Efficiency: Digital twins can help optimize resource allocation and improve emergency response efficiency. For example, in emergency rescue, digital twins can quickly formulate rescue plans based on the type, scale, and location of the disaster, simulate the rescue process, optimize resource allocation, and improve rescue efficiency.

Of course, building a digital twin for urban safety is no easy task, and it poses numerous challenges:

  • Data Acquisition and Integration: It requires integrating massive data from different departments and systems, including urban infrastructure data, environmental data, population data, etc.
  • Model Building and Optimization: High-precision and real-time digital twin models need to be constructed and continuously optimized based on real-world conditions.
  • Technology Integration and Application: Technologies such as artificial intelligence, big data, and blockchain need to be deeply integrated with digital twin technology to achieve intelligent perception, prediction, and control of urban safety.

Despite the challenges, the application prospects of digital twin technology in urban safety are vast. It will provide strong technical support for building safer, smarter, and more sustainable cities, enabling them to face future security challenges with greater confidence.

Conclusion: Embracing "Security Power" to Shape the Future

From "Sense of Security" to "Security Power," from "Single Security" to "Comprehensive Security," and from "Physical Space" to "Digital Twin," we are undergoing a profound transformation in security concepts. This transformation is not only about responding to known threats but also about identifying, predicting, and mitigating potential risks, turning challenges into opportunities, and building a robust barrier capable of addressing future uncertainties.

Facing increasingly complex security challenges, only by continuously enhancing "Security Power" can we firmly grasp the initiative for development in the ever-changing tides of our times and build a safer and more beautiful future.
