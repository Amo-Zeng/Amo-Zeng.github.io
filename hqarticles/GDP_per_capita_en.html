International Comparison of Per Capita GDP: Does It Really Make Sense?

In today's globalized world, rankings and comparisons of countries' per capita GDP frequently appear in media and academic discussions, serving as a key indicator of national economic development levels. However, does this comparison really make sense? When we delve deeper into this issue, we find that a simple numerical comparison cannot fully reflect a country's economic development status and living standards. Factors such as exchange rate fluctuations, differences in purchasing power, varying stages of development, and unequal distribution of wealth all make the international comparison of per capita GDP complex and multidimensional.
