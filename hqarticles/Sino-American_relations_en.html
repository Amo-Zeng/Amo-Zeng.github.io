From "Thucydides' Trap" to "Digital Symbiosis": Exploring New Paradigms of Sino-US Relations

Introduction: The Global Significance of Sino-US Relations

In an era where globalization and digitalization intertwine, Sino-US relations stand as the most influential bilateral relationship globally. The trajectory of this relationship not only profoundly impacts the two nations themselves but also shapes the future of the world. Traditional viewpoints often frame Sino-US relations as a zero-sum game, where the rise of one inevitably leads to the decline of the other. This perspective frequently invokes "Thucydides' Trap" to predict that conflict is inevitable between an emerging and an established power. However, this static view of power and adversarial thinking no longer suffices to explain the complex and ever-changing nature of contemporary Sino-US relations. We need to move beyond the traditional zero-sum framework, adopting a broader perspective and a more constructive approach to explore new paradigms for Sino-US relations.

1. "The World's Twin Engines": A New Paradigm of Sino-US Synergy and Competition

As the world's two largest economies, China and the United States serve as the "twin engines" driving global economic growth, given their economic scale and developmental potential. Traditional views often portray Sino-US relations as a zero-sum game, where one's rise necessarily results in the other's decline. However, this perspective overlooks the profound interdependence of the two nations within the global economy.

1.1 Synergy: The Twin Engines of Global Economic Growth

China and the United States not only dominate in economic scale but also complement each other structurally. China, as the global manufacturing hub and consumer market, drives global supply chains with its robust production capabilities and infrastructure; the United States, with its innovation prowess, financial systems, and deep consumer markets, serves as another major driver of the global economy. Cooperation in trade, investment, and technology between the two nations not only fosters their own economic development but also provides stability and growth for the global economy.

By enhancing economic collaboration, China and the United States can maximize the synergy of their "twin engines," jointly promoting sustainable global economic development. For instance, in the realm of climate change, the two countries can cooperate to develop and apply clean energy technologies, achieving their own green transitions while offering solutions for global climate governance.

1.2 Balancing Competition and Synergy

Of course, competition between China and the United States is inevitable, especially in areas such as technology, economy, and geopolitics. However, competition does not necessarily lead to conflict. Instead, it can stimulate innovation and drive progress in technology and the economy. The key lies in establishing effective communication mechanisms to prevent恶性 competition and seek synergy amidst competition, achieving mutual benefit.

For example, China and the United States can collaborate in emerging fields like artificial intelligence and 5G through joint research and development, and standard-setting cooperation, sharing the benefits of technological advancements. Simultaneously, in addressing global challenges such as public health crises and climate change, cooperation between the two nations can not only effectively tackle these challenges but also contribute to the improvement of the global governance system.

2. From "Thucydides' Trap" to "Co-evolution": Theoretical Advancements in Sino-US Relations

"Thucydides' Trap" refers to the inevitable conflict that arises from the shift in power between an emerging and an established great power. This theory has long dominated international relations. However, in the context of globalization's deepening, accelerated technological innovation, and increasingly severe global challenges, the limitations of this theory become more apparent.

2.1 Limitations of "Thucydides' Trap"

Firstly, "Thucydides' Trap" assumes a zero-sum structure in the international power system, failing to adequately consider the mutual interdependence of economies in the globalized era. Secondly, this theory overlooks the role of international institutions and rules in mitigating great power conflicts. Lastly, it does not sufficiently account for cultural differences, cognitive biases, and domestic societal sentiments' impact on bilateral relations.

In today's Sino-US relations, the simplistic logic of power politics is insufficient to explain the complex interactions between the two nations. We need a more inclusive and dynamic theoretical framework to understand the future of Sino-US relations.

2.2 "Co-evolution": A New Theoretical Framework

"Co-evolution" originates from biology, referring to the mutual adaptation of two or more species interacting, achieving a dynamic equilibrium over the long term. This concept offers new insights into understanding Sino-US relations:

  • Mutual Interdependence and Dynamic Balance: China and the United States have formed deep mutual interdependence in economics, technology, environment, and security. The interests of both are intertwined, and any changes in one will have profound effects on the other.
  • Balancing Competition and Cooperation: Competition between China and the United States is inevitable, but it does not necessarily lead to conflict. On the contrary, competition can stimulate innovation and drive progress in technology and the economy. Cooperation is also crucial, especially in addressing global challenges.
  • Long-term Adaptability: The development of Sino-US relations is not static; it requires both nations to adapt to changing internal and external environments continuously.

By constructing a framework for a new type of major power relationship, strengthening social foundations and cultural exchanges, and collaborating to address global challenges, China and the United States can fully realize co-evolution through competition and cooperation, contributing to world peace and development.

3. "Digital Symbiosis" in the Digital Age: New Opportunities and Challenges for Sino-US Relations

The advent of the digital age has not only reshaped global economic operation models but also profoundly transformed the interaction of international relations. As the world's two largest economies, China and the United States are both leaders and competitors in the digital wave. The rise of emerging fields such as artificial intelligence, 5G technology, and cybersecurity presents unprecedented opportunities and challenges for Sino-US relations.

3.1 Opportunities and Challenges in the Digital Age

The digital age offers new dimensions of cooperation and competition for Sino-US relations. In applications of cutting-edge technologies such as artificial intelligence, blockchain, and quantum computing, cooperation between China and the United States can drive the digital transformation of global governance. However, digital age competition is also intensifying. The global dominance race in artificial intelligence and 5G technology directly impacts the technological hegemony of both nations; the cybersecurity arena's博弈may lead to national security risks; and the race for digital trade rules has become a new focal point in the Sino-US economic relationship.

3.2 Risks and Prevention of "Digital Cold War"

The "Digital Cold War" refers to the all-around confrontation between China and the United States in the digital technology field, including technology blockades, talent competition, and market exclusion. Such confrontation not only harms the technological development and economic interests of both nations but may also lead to global technological fragmentation and market segmentation, hindering the healthy development of the global digital economy.

To avoid a "Digital Cold War," China and the United States need to establish effective risk prevention mechanisms. Both should clarify the boundaries of competition through dialogue mechanisms, avoiding confrontation due to misunderstandings or misjudgments; jointly develop global digital governance rules to ensure the inclusiveness and security of technological development; strengthen cybersecurity cooperation, building trust measures to prevent security risks from spilling over.

3.3 Feasible Paths to "Digital Symbiosis"

"Digital Symbiosis" refers to the positive interaction and complementary advantages of China and the United States in the digital age, jointly promoting the development of the global digital economy. Specific paths include:

  • Jointly Developing Global Digital Governance Rules: China and the United States should promote the establishment of global digital governance rules, covering data privacy protection, cybersecurity, and digital trade, on multilateral platforms such as the United Nations and G20.
  • Strengthening Technological Cooperation: In fields like artificial intelligence and 5G, the two countries can share the benefits of technological advancements through joint research, development, and standard-setting cooperation.
  • Promoting the Digital Transformation of Cultural Exchanges: Digital technology provides new channels for Sino-US cultural exchange, allowing people from both countries to enhance mutual understanding through virtual reality and online education.

4. Conclusion: From Confrontation to Cooperation – The Future Paradigm of Sino-US Relations

The future of Sino-US relations should not be a repeat of "Thucydides' Trap" nor a "Digital Cold War" of confrontation but a model of "Co-evolution" and "Digital Symbiosis." By exploring the synergy of "the world's twin engines," building a framework for a new type of major power relationship, strengthening social foundations and cultural exchanges, and collaborating to address global challenges, China and the United States can fully achieve mutual benefit through competition and cooperation.

This is not only in the common interest of the two nations but also a necessary requirement for sustainable global economic development and human societal progress. Through joint efforts, China and the United States can inject new vitality into the construction of the global governance system, contributing to world peace and prosperity.
