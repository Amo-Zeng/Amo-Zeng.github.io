Introduction: Civilizational Leap from "Remote Control" to "Autonomy"

When NASA's Perseverance rover autonomously selects drilling sites in Jezero Crater, its commands from Earth take 11 minutes to arrive—this latency represents not just a technical challenge but a critical threshold for our ethical frameworks. While mission control traditionally serves as the moral decision-making core, deep space exploration faces fundamental physical constraints (3-22 minutes Mars-Earth delay, 5 hours for Pluto), rendering "real-time control" obsolete.

This compels AI systems to make autonomous decisions under uncertainty, raising a fundamental question: When humans cannot be "present," who bears moral responsibility for interstellar actions?
