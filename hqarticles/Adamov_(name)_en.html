Absurdist Drama and Modern Psychology: Dreams, Subconscious, and Identity Interplay

Adamov's dramatic works explore the deep psychological states of modern individuals, particularly dreams, the subconscious, and crises of identity, through absurd plots, distorted characters, and irrational dialogues. Using the theoretical frameworks of Freud and Jung, we can better understand the psychological elements in Adamov's works and explore their potential applications in contemporary psychotherapy.

Dream and Subconscious Symbolism

Freud, in his seminal work "The Interpretation of Dreams," posits that dreams are the expression and fulfillment of subconscious desires. Adamov's plays are filled with dream-like scenes that often blur the lines between reality and fantasy, revealing the innermost fears and desires of the characters.

Crisis of Identity

Identity is a crucial topic in modern psychology, especially in postmodern society where individuals face conflicts and choices among multiple identities. Adamov's plays often explore this theme through absurd character portrayals.

Deconstruction and Reconstruction of Language: Revealing Truth and Deception

Adamov's dramatic works, through unique use of language, subvert traditional dialogue structures, revealing the contradictions in conveying truth and deception. This deconstruction and reconstruction of language not only bring new dimensions to dramatic art but also offer profound insights into linguistics and cognitive science.

Absurdist Drama and Political Satire: Resonance from the Cold War to Modern Politics

Adamov's plays are not only famous for their absurd forms but also contain profound political satire within their plots and characters. Particularly in the context of the Cold War era, Adamov used absurdist drama to reveal authoritarianism, bureaucracy, and the loss of the individual within the collective.

Conclusion

Adamov's absurdist plays are not just innovations in theatrical art but also profound explorations of human psychology, language, and political realities in modern society. Through absurd plots, distorted characters, and irrational dialogues, Adamov reveals the dilemmas of modern man, showcasing the fictitious nature of language and the deep meanings of political satire.
