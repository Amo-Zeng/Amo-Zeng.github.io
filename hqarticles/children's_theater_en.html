The Forge of Creativity: Cultivating Future Citizens from Theater to Games

Stage and Games: Arenas for Emotion and Thought

Throughout the course of human civilization, the stage and games have always been arenas for emotion and thought, key forces in shaping individuals and society. From ancient Greek theater to modern digital games, these seemingly different forms collectively construct a complex "brain-building" process. Through emotional resonance, social cognition, and creativity, they shape future citizens.

1. Brain-Building on Stage: The Intersection of Emotion and Cognition

The stage is a laboratory for emotion and thought. Actors transform abstract emotions and complex thoughts into concrete actions through performance. This transformation is driven by highly coordinated and creative brain activity.

  • Identity Reconstruction: When actors step into a role, the prefrontal cortex processes self-awareness, while the hippocampus helps retrieve background information about the character.
  • Emotional Resonance and Mirror Neurons: Through the activation of mirror neurons, actors experience the emotional fluctuations of the character and convey them to the audience through emotional resonance.
  • Improvisation and Creativity: Improvisation in theater is the neural basis of creativity. Actors develop their creative skills through improvisational training.

2. Social Experimentation: Shaping the Values of Future Citizens

Children's theater is not merely entertainment; it is a profound social experimentation arena. By fostering multicultural integration, cultivating civic awareness, and practicing cooperation and communication, it shapes the values of future citizens.

  • Multicultural Integration: Children's theater provides a window for kids to encounter diverse cultures, nurturing their open-world perspective.
  • Civic Awareness and Social Responsibility: Through exploring social issues, theater sparks children's interest and critical thinking about societal problems.
  • Practicing Cooperation and Communication: Theater performance is a collective art. Children learn to listen, understand, and coordinate with others during performances.

3. Game Upgrades: The Awesome Laboratory of Creativity and Imagination

In the digital age, games have evolved into an infinitely possible "awesome laboratory." Through rule restructuring, instant feedback, and the magic of virtual reality, games spark human creativity and imagination.

  • Freedom in Rule and Boundary Reconstruction: Games provide players with spaces for free exploration, sparking creativity.
  • Magic of Virtual and Augmented Reality: VR and AR technologies extend imagination into the real world, offering new experiences of "virtual-real fusion."
  • Future Prospects of Gamification: With the rise of the metaverse, games will become a basic form of life, unleashing human creative potential.

4. Fusion and Co-Creation: The Future of Theater and Games

Although theater and games differ in form, they collectively construct a forge for cultivating future citizens. Through emotional resonance, social cognition, and creativity, theater and games help children understand multiculturalism, cultivate civic awareness, enhance cooperation and communication skills, and spark their creativity and imagination.

Conclusion

The stage and games are not only arenas for testing emotions and thoughts but also forges shaping future citizens. In the future, theater and games will continue to play a crucial role in shaping human emotion, cognition, and creativity, helping us co-write a more diverse, innovative, and inclusive future.
