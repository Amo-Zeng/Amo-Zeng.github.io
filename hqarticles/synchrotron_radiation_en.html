Synchrotron Radiation: From Probes of the Micro World to Transformers of Daily Life

Synchrotron radiation, a mysterious phenomenon originating from high-energy physics laboratories, is rapidly transitioning from the pinnacle of science to every aspect of daily life, becoming a bridge connecting the micro and macro worlds. It is not just a brilliant beam of light but also a "microscope" capable of penetrating the essence of matter, helping humanity uncover the deepest secrets of nature, driving technological progress, and quietly transforming our lives.

Synchrotron Radiation: The "Blade of Light" in the Micro World

The discovery of synchrotron radiation dates back to the 1940s when scientists studying the movement of high-energy electrons in magnetic fields accidentally found that these electrons emit a special type of light radiation tangent to their path. This light has extremely high brightness, a narrow spectral range, and strong polarization, earning it the title "noble light." The emergence of synchrotron radiation has provided scientists with a previously unprecedented tool, allowing them to strip away the surface disguises of matter and reach its essence.

On the scientific exploration journey, synchrotron radiation is like a sharp "blade of light," widely applied in materials science, life sciences, chemistry, earth, and environmental sciences. In materials science, synchrotron radiation helps scientists analyze the microscopic structure of polymer materials, providing a theoretical foundation for the design of new materials; in the field of life sciences, synchrotron radiation's X-ray structural analysis technology allows us to glimpse the three-dimensional structure of protein molecules, opening new doors for drug development; in environmental science research, synchrotron radiation's fine spectral analysis capability helps scientists track the migration and transformation processes of pollutants in the environment, providing scientific evidence for environmental protection.

Synchrotron Radiation Source: The "Super Microscope" for Exploring the Micro World

Synchrotron radiation sources are not artificially manufactured but are a natural phenomenon. When high-energy electrons move in curved paths at speeds close to the speed of light in a magnetic field, they emit electromagnetic waves tangent to their path, known as synchrotron radiation. This radiation has continuous wavelength, high brightness, and good collimation, making it an ideal tool for exploring the micro world.

With its wide wavelength coverage and high brightness, synchrotron radiation sources can enhance resolution to the atomic level. For example, using X-ray beams, scientists can clearly observe the arrangement of atoms in crystals, bringing breakthroughs to materials science, biology, and other fields. In materials science, synchrotron radiation sources help develop new high-performance materials, such as lightweight and high-strength materials for aerospace and flexible display materials for electronic devices; in life sciences, synchrotron radiation sources reveal the three-dimensional structures of biological macromolecules like proteins and nucleic acids, providing important clues for drug development; in chemistry, synchrotron radiation sources are used to study molecular reaction processes on catalyst surfaces, which is of great significance for energy and environmental protection.

From Follower to Leader: The Leapfrog Journey of China's Synchrotron Radiation Sources

The development history of China's synchrotron radiation sources is an epic journey from "follower" to "leader." In the 1980s, China's synchrotron radiation source construction started late, with a significant gap from the international advanced level. In 1989, China's first synchrotron radiation device, the Beijing Electron-Positron Collider (BEPC), was completed, marking the first step in China's synchrotron radiation source construction. However, BEPC's synchrotron radiation was mainly used for high-energy physics experiments, and its performance indicators were still far from the international advanced level.

Entering the new century, the construction of China's synchrotron radiation sources entered a phase of rapid development. In 2009, the Shanghai Synchrotron Radiation Facility (SSRF) was completed and put into operation, which was China's first third-generation synchrotron radiation source, with its performance indicators reaching the international advanced level, marking the leap from "following" to "running alongside." In recent years, China's synchrotron radiation source construction has continuously achieved breakthrough progress. In 2020, the National Synchrotron Radiation Laboratory of the University of Science and Technology of China successfully developed the "Hefei Light Source," the world's first "fourth-generation" synchrotron radiation source, with brightness two orders of magnitude higher than that of the third-generation source, marking the leap from "running alongside" to "leading."

Synchrotron Radiation: The Bridge from Laboratories to Daily Life

The value of synchrotron radiation is not limited to scientific research; it is quietly transforming our daily lives. In the medical field, synchrotron-based imaging technologies, such as X-ray computed tomography (CT), provide more precise diagnostic imaging information; in industrial production, synchrotron advanced non-destructive testing technology offers reliable quality control, reducing production costs; in the field of art archaeology, synchrotron non-destructive analysis technology provides important support for cultural heritage protection and historical research, allowing us to communicate across time and history.

Synchrotron radiation, from a faint beam of light in high-energy physics laboratories to the "light of wisdom" illuminating the boundaries of human cognition, reflects humanity's unremitting spirit of exploration of the unknown world. With the continuous progress of science and technology, the bridge of synchrotron radiation connecting the micro world and the real world will become even more solid and broad, leading us towards a more brilliant future.
