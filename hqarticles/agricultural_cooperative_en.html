Insights on Agricultural Modernization

From "Small-Scale Farming Mentality" to "Modern Agricultural Thinking"

Agricultural Cooperatives Reshape the Role of Farmers, Leading Agricultural Modernization

In the process of China's agricultural modernization, the role of farmers is undergoing profound transformation. The traditional "small-scale farming mentality"—characterized by dependency, conservatism, and information isolation—has been a significant barrier to agricultural modernization. However, with the rise and development of agricultural cooperatives, farmers' mindset and behavior patterns are gradually shifting from the "small-scale farming mentality" to the "modern agricultural thinking." Through organized, large-scale, standardized production, as well as the introduction of advanced technologies and management practices, agricultural cooperatives help farmers develop market awareness, competition awareness, and cooperative awareness, reshaping their roles and providing strong support for the structural reform of agricultural supply-side.

Limitations of Traditional Small-Scale Farming and the Urgency of Modern Agricultural Transformation

Small-scale farming, as a traditional model of Chinese agriculture, is based on family units and self-sufficient production methods, which have long formed a situation of dependency, conservatism, and information isolation. Farmers are accustomed to relying on government subsidies and policy support, lacking the motivation for proactive innovation and market exploration; in production decisions, they tend to follow traditional practices, fear risks, and are unwilling to try new technologies and varieties; information isolation limits farmers' connection with external markets, making it difficult to obtain timely market information and technical support, leading to a disconnect between production and market demand, and low product added value.

These issues collectively hinder the adaptability of small-scale farming to the needs of modern agriculture, impeding the process of agricultural modernization. Farmers' dependency and conservatism prevent them from engaging in market competition, while information isolation limits their competitiveness in the market. To achieve agricultural modernization, it is essential to fundamentally change farmers' mindset and behavior patterns, driving them to shift from "small-scale farming mentality" to "modern agricultural thinking."

The Role and Impact of Agricultural Cooperatives: From "Going It Alone" to "Standing Together"

The emergence of agricultural cooperatives provides an important platform for farmers to change their mindset. Through organized, large-scale, standardized production, cooperatives help farmers overcome the limitations of decentralized operations, improving production efficiency and market competitiveness. By centralizing the procurement of production materials and unified product sales, cooperatives reduce production costs and enhance the market negotiation power of products. Additionally, cooperatives introduce advanced agricultural technologies and management practices, helping farmers improve production levels and product quality.

In terms of mindset transformation, agricultural cooperatives organize training and exchange activities to help farmers develop market awareness, competition awareness, and cooperative awareness. By directly connecting with the market, farmers gradually recognize the importance of market demand, begin to actively monitor market dynamics, and adjust their production structure accordingly. The enhancement of competition awareness makes farmers more focused on product quality and brand building, striving to increase product added value. The cultivation of cooperative awareness helps farmers recognize the importance of collective strength, willing to cooperate for mutual prosperity.

Successful Practices of Agricultural Cooperatives: Solving Product Sales Challenges and Optimizing Supply-Side Structure

Agricultural cooperatives play a significant role in solving product sales challenges and optimizing the supply-side structure. Below are several typical successful case studies:

  1. Yangcheng Lake Crab Cooperative: Branding and Market Connection

    Farmers in the Yangcheng Lake area of Suzhou, Jiangsu Province, established a cooperative to unify cultivation standards, packaging design, and sales channels, successfully creating the well-known brand "Yangcheng Lake Crab." The cooperative not only enhanced the market competitiveness of products but also maximized farmers' benefits through brand premium. This case demonstrates how cooperatives can transform scattered agricultural products into high-added-value brand products through branding strategies.

  2. Shouguang Vegetable Cooperative: Order Agriculture and Market Demand Connection

    The Shouguang Vegetable Cooperative in Shandong Province established an "Market + Cooperative + Farmer" order agriculture model to accurately connect with market demand, developing off-season vegetables, organic vegetables, and other specialty products, achieving increased farmer income and agricultural efficiency. This model effectively addresses the issue of overproduction caused by traditional agricultural practices of blindly following market trends.

  3. Jiangning Smart Agriculture Cooperative: Digital Transformation to Improve Production Efficiency

    The Jiangning Agricultural Cooperative in Nanjing, Jiangsu Province, created a smart agriculture platform, using IoT technology to monitor soil and climate data in real-time, accurately controlling agricultural production, and improving product yield and quality. This case demonstrates how cooperatives can drive the transformation and upgrading of agricultural production methods through digital technology.

These cases show that agricultural cooperatives not only help farmers solve sales problems but also drive agricultural supply-side structural reform by optimizing planting structures, enhancing product quality, and brand value.

Future Outlook: From "Production-Oriented" to "Demand-Oriented," Promoting High-Quality Agricultural Development

Looking ahead, agricultural cooperatives will continue to play a crucial role in promoting farmers' transformation into modern professional farmers. First, cooperatives will further promote farmers' skills development through systematic training and practical experience, helping them master modern agricultural technologies and management knowledge, becoming professional farmers with professional素养 (professionalism). Second, cooperatives will continue to strengthen farmers' market awareness and competition awareness, helping them better adapt to market changes and enhance product market competitiveness through deep market integration.

Moreover, cooperatives can guide farmers to participate in the extension of the agricultural value chain, helping them expand from mere agricultural product producers to various links of the agricultural value chain, such as product processing, logistics, and brand building, further increasing farmers' income levels. With the help of digital and intelligent technologies, cooperatives can optimize production and sales processes, enhancing the precision and efficiency of agricultural production.

At the same time, agricultural cooperatives should continue to deepen supply-side structural reform, shifting from "production-oriented" to "demand-oriented." Through market research and data analysis, cooperatives can accurately connect with market demand, guide farmers to adjust planting structures, and develop marketable specialty agricultural products. The promotion of green organic agriculture, smart agriculture, and circular agriculture will provide new momentum for the sustainable development of agriculture.

Conclusion

Agricultural cooperatives, through organized, large-scale, standardized production, and the introduction of advanced technologies and management practices, help farmers gradually shift from "small-scale farming mentality" to "modern agricultural thinking," reshaping their roles. By solving product sales challenges and optimizing the supply-side structure, cooperatives drive the modernization and high-quality development of agriculture. In the future, agricultural cooperatives will continue to play a leading role, supporting the structural reform of agricultural supply-side, promoting the advancement of Chinese agriculture to a higher level, and providing solid talent and industrial foundations for rural revitalization and agricultural modernization.
