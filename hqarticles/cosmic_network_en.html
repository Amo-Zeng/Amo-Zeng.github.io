II. The Quantum Entanglement Network: The Ghostly Dance Across Spacetime

Quantum entanglement is one of the most counterintuitive phenomena in quantum mechanics, describing the special correlations between two or more particles. Even separated by light-years, these particles remain mysteriously connected, with the state of one particle instantly affecting the state of another. Within the cosmic web, this quantum entanglement exhibits unique distribution characteristics. For instance, intergalactic quantum entanglement has been discovered between galaxies and even superclusters, possibly originating from the early universe or related to extreme astronomical events such as supernova explosions or black hole mergers.

The potential applications of the quantum entanglement network are fascinating. The feasibility of faster-than-light communication, the application of quantum teleportation on a cosmic scale, and the view of the entire universe as a massive quantum computer all challenge our traditional understanding of information transmission. Especially quantum teleportation technology, which can transfer quantum states from one location to another without physical carriers. Theoretically, this technology can be extended to a cosmic scale, enabling intergalactic quantum communication. This communication method not only breaks the speed limit of light but also provides the technical foundation for cross-species and cross-civilization consciousness sharing.
