Digital Twin, Collective Memory, and Globalization: The Modern Dilemma and Transcendence of Multidimensional Identity

In the intersection of contemporary society's digital wave, globalization, and collective memory, identity is no longer a single, fixed mirror image, but a complex multidimensional landscape. The virtual world of social media shapes our "Digital Twin," the fractures in collective memory provide space for rewriting ourselves, and the mobility of globalization shatters the static mirror image of traditional identities. These three elements intertwine in modern society, jointly constituting the contemporary dilemma and the possibility of transcendence in our identity.

1. Tension between Digital Twin and Real Self: The Game of Virtual and Real Identity

Within the virtual field of social media, each of us possesses a "Digital Twin" â€“ a meticulously designed, idealized self-image. This twin may be an amplification of a specific personality in life or a completely detached "perfect self." This virtual identity undoubtedly provides a way to escape reality, but it also brings profound identity dilemmas.

  • Crisis of Identity Splitting: When the Digital Twin and the real self experience significant tension, individuals may fall into a sense of unreality, leading to social anxiety and self-doubt.
  • Social Media's Positive Feedback Mechanism: The "like economy" and instant feedback mechanism of social media continuously strengthen the Digital Twin.
  • Key to Balance: To resolve this identity splitting dilemma, we need to find a balance between the virtual and the real.

2. Fractures in Collective Memory: The Dialectic of Rewriting the Self and Identity

Collective memory is a dynamically constructed narrative system by society, shaping individual identity through historical events, cultural traditions, and social norms. However, it is within the fractures of collective memory that the true self emerges.

  1. Standardization and Oppression of Collective Memory: Collective memory is a selectively reshaped narrative, limiting independent thinking about self-identity.
  2. Courage to Rewrite Memory: Finding the self within the fractures of collective memory requires a courage to rewrite.
  3. Creative Expression of Individual Memory: In the era of social media, individual memory gains unprecedented expression space.

3. Identity Mobility in the Globalized Era: Self-Reconstruction in a Multidimensional Landscape

The process of globalization has shattered the static mirror image of traditional identity, providing a fluid, multidimensional landscape. In this landscape, identity is no longer a fixed mirror but a series of flowing prisms.

  • Mobility of Identity: In the era of globalization, a person can be a conglomerate of multiple identities.
  • Profound Meaning of Mobility: The mobility of identity offers a richer space for self-cognition.
  • Cultural Integration in the Globalized Era: Identity in the globalization era is not only diverse but also deeply integrated.

4. Conclusion: Multidimensional Balance and Creative Reconstruction of Identity

In the intersection of digitization, globalization, and collective memory, our identity is undergoing unprecedented reconstruction. Facing these challenges, we need to find balance between the virtual and the real, rewrite ourselves within the framework of collective memory, and shape multidimensional self-identity in the mobility of the globalization era. Ultimately, the true self is neither a virtual Digital Twin nor an individual entirely defined by collective memory, but a unique existence constantly creating and reconstructing in a multidimensional landscape.
