Neurotopology of Visual Power and Technical Texture: The Politics of Perception in the Post-Media Era

Reconstructing the Theoretical Framework

This paper reveals the cognitive-political mechanisms of digital visual ecology through tridimensional lenses of neuroplasticity, generative algorithms, and power topology. Integrating Malabou's cerebral plasticity theory, Hayles' posthumanist framework, and Deleuzian philosophy, we construct an interdisciplinary analytical model.

I. Neural Coupling: Biotechnical Embeddedness of Visual Spectacle

Under the drive of Rewiring mechanisms (Malabou,2008), the plasticity of human visual cortex has evolved into a cognitive interface for technological intervention. Zeki's neuroaesthetic research reveals that the lateral geniculate nucleus not only transmits visual signals but shapes aesthetic differential geometry like a tensor network (Zeki,1999). This discovery resonates with Hayles' recursive embodiment theory, forming a bidirectional dynamical model of visual processing: the torque effect between feedforward perception flow and feedback cognitive networks (Hayles,2017).

II. Algorithmic Memes: The Dialectics of Machine Vision Generation

Digital imaging technologies have transcended media materialism. Kittler's assertion that "technology precedes existence" finds uncanny resonance in networked visual ecosystems. Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) materialize Lacan's mirror stage - machine vision reconstructs the symbolic order through adversarial training (Manovich,2021). The deterritorializing recombination of visual elements in deep learning models resembles Deleuzian "Body without Organs" unfolding fold movements at pixel dimensions.

IV. Visual Alchemy: Optical Apparatus of Power Topology

The gaze economy of surveillance capitalism (Zuboff,2019) colludes with short-video platform addiction mechanisms, transforming Debord's spectacle society into hyper-real interfaces with neuromodulatory functions. Foucault's Panopticon reconstitutes as light-cone matrices in the cloud, where every frame becomes a topological scalpel of power codes. Here we invoke the concept of "virtual skin" (Hansen,2006) to disclose the production of technological unconsciousness in touchscreen interactions.

V. Conclusion: Remapping Posthuman Visual Geography

On the dark continent of visual hegemony, Haraway's Cyborg Manifesto and Glissant's poetics of relation illuminate paths for cognitive decolonization. Democratizing perception requires developing slow-vision protocols that resist light-speed cognition, inspired by Gabriel's theory of residual senses and holographic ethics. By tracing Deleuze's tactile-visual (Tactile-Optique) legacy, we may construct resistant topologies against algorithmic capture, transforming the retina into a new frontier for embodied revival.
