Artificial Intelligence and Public Engagement: A Dual Path Driving Sustainable Development in the Nuclear Industry

With the growing global demand for low-carbon energy, nuclear energy, as an efficient and stable energy form, still plays a significant role in the global energy structure. However, the safety of nuclear energy remains a focal point of public concern and a key factor for the sustainable development of the nuclear industry. As a core component in ensuring nuclear safety, nuclear safety review not only faces challenges from technological innovations but also must strike a balance between public trust and transparency. This article explores how technological innovation and public trust can drive the sustainable development of the nuclear industry through three dimensions: the application of artificial intelligence (AI) in nuclear safety review, the optimization of public engagement mechanisms, and the multiple impacts of nuclear safety review on the sustainable development of the nuclear industry.

I. Opportunities and Challenges of Artificial Intelligence in Nuclear Safety Review

1.1 The Potential of AI Technology in Nuclear Safety Review

With the rapid development of artificial intelligence technology, its application potential in various fields is continuously being explored. As a high-risk and high-tech energy form, the safety review process of nuclear energy is particularly critical. The introduction of AI brings unprecedented opportunities for nuclear safety review, especially in improving review efficiency, processing complex data, and dynamically predicting risks.

AI's automated processing capabilities can significantly shorten the time required for nuclear safety review. Traditional review methods rely on expert experience and manual analysis, which are time-consuming and prone to human error. AI can automate the processing of large amounts of data, quickly identify potential risks, and reduce the review cycle by 30% to 50%. This efficiency improvement not only reduces review costs but also makes the review process more flexible, allowing for timely responses to emergencies.

1.2 Key Challenges in AI Application

Although AI shows great potential in nuclear safety review, its application also faces numerous challenges. First, the safety and reliability of AI algorithms are the primary concerns in nuclear safety review. The complex and ever-changing nature of nuclear safety data means that even minor errors can lead to severe consequences. Therefore, AI algorithms must undergo rigorous validation and testing to ensure they operate stably under extreme conditions.

Secondly, the handling of complex nuclear data, such as radioactive materials and reactor design, is another significant challenge. The high complexity of nuclear data requires AI to have powerful multimodal data integration, high-dimensional data processing, and dynamic prediction and adjustment capabilities. By enhancing these capabilities, AI can effectively identify potential safety hazards and adjust analysis models in real-time to ensure timely risk identification.

1.3 Ethical and Regulatory Issues

The application of AI in nuclear safety review involves not only technical issues but also ethical and legal considerations. Firstly, the use of AI must comply with ethical standards to ensure it does not threaten human safety. A strict ethical review mechanism must be established to ensure that AI development and usage follow transparent, fair, and responsible principles. Additionally, preventing the potential misuse of AI is another critical issue. Nuclear safety review involves national security and public interests, and any improper use of AI could lead to serious consequences. Therefore, a multi-level regulatory mechanism must be put in place to ensure AI technology is not used for malicious purposes.

II. Public Engagement and Transparency in Nuclear Safety Review

2.1 Balancing Transparency and Confidentiality

In nuclear safety review, information transparency is the foundation of public trust but must also balance national security and technical confidentiality needs. Therefore, it is crucial to determine which information can be made public and which should remain confidential. For example, design standards for nuclear facilities, emergency plans, and environmental impact assessment reports should be prioritized for disclosure, while internal design drawings and technical details of nuclear facilities should remain confidential.

By establishing a graded information disclosure mechanism, a balance can be found between transparency and confidentiality. For instance, information can be categorized based on sensitivity, with low-sensitivity information prioritized for disclosure, while medium to high-sensitivity information requires strict review and authorization before release. This approach not only meets public知情 needs but also ensures national security and technical secrets are not compromised.

2.2 Enhancing Public Understanding and Engagement with Modern Technology

The public often struggles to understand abstract nuclear safety information, especially when it involves complex technical jargon and safety metrics. To improve public acceptance of nuclear safety information, modern technologies such as virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) can be powerful tools. Through VR technology, the public can "enter" nuclear power plants and gain an intuitive understanding of how nuclear facilities operate and the safety measures in place, thereby reducing their unfamiliarity and fear of nuclear energy. Additionally, AR technology can simulate real emergency scenarios during drills, helping the public learn correct response measures and boosting their confidence in nuclear safety measures.

2.3 Optimizing Public Engagement Mechanisms

The effectiveness of public engagement mechanisms directly impacts the legitimacy and credibility of nuclear safety review. By introducing diverse forms of public engagement, such as community roundtables, online public forums, and expert advisory committees, public participation can be increased. Furthermore, through education and outreach, incentive mechanisms, and transparent feedback mechanisms, the influence of public participation can be enhanced, ensuring that public opinions are fully considered in nuclear safety review.

III. Multiple Impacts of Nuclear Safety Review on the Sustainable Development of the Nuclear Industry

3.1 Driving Technological Innovation and Competitiveness

The rigor of nuclear safety review is both a prerequisite for ensuring nuclear safety and a challenge for technological innovation and market competition. However, stringent review standards are not purely obstacles; they also drive technological progress. Through strict reviews, developers must thoroughly consider potential safety risks and address them during the design phase. This "safety-driven" innovation model, while increasing development costs and time, ensures the safety and reliability of the final technology.

New-generation nuclear reactor technologies, such as small modular reactors and accelerator-driven subcritical systems, have fully considered the balance between safety and economics during the design phase. These technologies not only offer higher safety levels but also significantly reduce construction and operational costs through modular design and intelligent control, thereby enhancing the competitiveness of nuclear power.

3.2 Advancing Nuclear Waste Management and Facility Decommissioning

Nuclear waste management and facility decommissioning are long-term challenges for the sustainable development of the nuclear industry. Nuclear safety review plays a crucial role in driving progress in these areas. For example, the design and application of waste management technologies must fully consider potential environmental risks and long-term safety, driving continuous innovation in nuclear waste management. Additionally, standardized management of facility decommissioning helps reduce costs and improve efficiency, providing valuable experience and technical support for future decommissioning efforts.

3.3 Enhancing Synergy Between Nuclear and Renewable Energy

In the context of energy transition, nuclear energy and renewable energy sources like wind and solar have both competitive and complementary relationships. As a stable baseload power source, nuclear energy can compensate for the intermittency of renewable energy. Nuclear safety review enhances the stability and flexibility of nuclear power plants, enabling them to better adapt to changes in the power system and achieve synergy with renewable energy sources like wind and solar.

IV. Conclusion

As a core component in ensuring nuclear safety, nuclear safety review is both a challenge for technological innovation and market competition and a significant driver of the sustainable development of the nuclear industry. With the introduction of artificial intelligence technology, the efficiency and accuracy of nuclear safety review have been significantly improved, while the optimization of public engagement and transparency has enhanced public trust in nuclear energy. Looking forward, as technology continues to advance and public trust gradually builds, nuclear safety review will play a crucial role in driving the sustainable development of the nuclear industry and contributing to the global transition to green energy.
