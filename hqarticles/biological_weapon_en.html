Multidimensional Threats of Biological Weapons: From Ecological Imbalance to Global Public Health Crisis

Biological weapons, as a means of attack using pathogens, toxins, or genetically modified organisms, have a history that dates back to ancient times. However, with the rapid advancement of modern biotechnology, their forms and threats have become more complex and dangerous. Although international law explicitly prohibits the development, production, and use of biological weapons, their potential threats have far exceeded the traditional military scope, posing profound and multidimensional impacts on ecosystems, ethics, and global public health security.
