In the evolution of media ecology, algorithm recommendation technology plays a crucial role. Algorithms analyze users' behavioral data to predict their interests and preferences, thereby pushing the most "relevant" content. While providing convenience for users, this technology has quietly changed the way we access information. The "sweet trap" of personalized recommendations lies in the false sense of satisfaction it creates. Within this chamber, we only encounter information similar to our own views, hear voices from "our own people," and see "facts we agree with." This homogenization of information not only solidifies our thinking but also makes us lose tolerance and understanding for different perspectives.

Algorithm recommendations not only construct echo chambers but also deepen their walls through "reinforcement effects."
