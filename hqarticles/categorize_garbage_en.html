The Trash Map: Digital Redemption of Urban Memories

Cities are containers of human civilization, bearing the traces of historical changes. However, the rapid process of urbanization is like a giant shredder, mercilessly crushing precious urban memories. In this crisis of memory loss, the emergence of the "Trash Map" is like a lightning bolt piercing the darkness, pointing us toward a bright path to save urban memories.

The "Trash Map" is not a traditional map showing the distribution of trash but a special map recording urban life trajectories and behavioral habits. From discarded electronic products, we can glimpse the pace of technological development; from broken pottery shards, we can piece together the living scenes of urban residents; from piles of construction waste, we can feel the speed and power of urban expansion. These seemingly useless trash are in fact witnesses of history, silently telling the past of the city, waiting to be rediscovered and interpreted.

The Urban Memory Museum: Interweaving of Virtual and Reality

The birth of the "Trash Map" provides new ideas for preserving urban memories, but relying on it alone is far from enough. We need a more systematic and comprehensive platform to collect, organize, and display these fragments of urban memory. The "Urban Memory Museum" thus emerges, leveraging digital technology to reintegrate memory fragments scattered across the city, building a virtual and real intertwined sanctuary of urban memory.

In the "Urban Memory Museum," we can experience ancient urban life through virtual reality technology; understand the development path of the city through interactive games; and visually track the trajectory of urban changes through data visualization. Digital technology has given wings to the preservation and dissemination of urban memories, making history more vivid, lively, and within reach.
