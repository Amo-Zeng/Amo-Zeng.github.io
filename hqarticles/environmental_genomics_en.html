1. Cognitive Reconstruction of Theoretical Paradigm Shifts

From Watson-Crick's revelation of the DNA double helix (1953) to the completion of the Human Genome Project (2003), genetic decoding has focused on understanding life's essence. Environmental Genomics 2.0 now extends this paradigm across geological timescales[1], systematically coupling pollen deposition layers and isotope data (Fig.1) to identify key genotype-phenotype correlation nodes in Late Holocene (≈11,700 BP-present) ecological succession trajectories.

★ Anthropocene Theoretical Dialogue

This technology-driven "rewriting of geological life archives" resonates with the philosophical core of Crutzen's Anthropocene concept—species intelligence deeply intervening in Earth system operations[2]. However, vigilance is required against technocratic tendencies that may obscure the safe operating space defined by the Planetary Boundaries framework[3]
