Human Vulnerabilities: The Deep-Seated Concerns of Cybersecurity

In the digital age, technological advancements have not fully curbed the threat of cyberattacks, instead revealing a deeper vulnerability: "human weaknesses." Psychological studies show that human cognitive biases, emotional manipulation, and social engineering loopholes often become the key to attackers breaking through defenses.

  • Cognitive Biases: Humans often exhibit cognitive biases such as confirmation bias and availability heuristic in information processing. Attackers use meticulously designed phishing emails or fake websites to trick users into revealing sensitive information.
  • Emotional Manipulation: Emotions are a significant driver of human decision-making. Attackers manipulate user behavior by triggering fear, greed, sympathy, and other emotions.
  • Social Engineering: Attackers exploit human social psychology to deceive. By masquerading as trustworthy individuals or institutions, attackers gain users' trust and access sensitive information.

The Metaverse: The Interplay of Virtual and Real Security

The rise of the metaverse marks a further blurring of the boundaries between virtual and real. In this digital parallel world, cybersecurity issues have become more complex and three-dimensional. The dimensions, scale, and value of personal data have reached unprecedented levels.
