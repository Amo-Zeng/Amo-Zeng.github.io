Hydrogen-Driven "Microbial Electrochemical Plants": The Convergence Revolution of Synthetic Biology and Photocatalysis

Background: Challenges in Traditional Hydrogen Production and the Urgent Need for Green Alternatives

Under global carbon neutrality goals, hydrogen energy is hailed as the "ultimate energy" due to its zero-carbon emission characteristics. However, current mainstream hydrogen production technologies face dual challenges:

  • Grey Hydrogen Challenge: 76% of production relies on fossil fuels, emitting 9-12 tons CO₂ per ton of hydrogen
  • Green Hydrogen Bottleneck: Water electrolysis requires 55 kWh/kg energy consumption with rare metal catalysts

2022 data shows green hydrogen costs remain high at $3-8/kg, representing only 0.1% of production. Microbial Electrochemical Factories (MEFs) integrate synthetic biology with nanophotocatalysis, achieving 13.7% solar-to-hydrogen conversion efficiency while enabling carbon-negative hydrogen production.
