Data-Driven and Culture-Empowered: Building a Multi-Stakeholder Health China Ecosystem

In the grand blueprint of the "Health China" strategy, how to construct a multi-stakeholder, co-created, and shared health ecosystem by integrating modern technology with traditional wisdom has become a critical issue in promoting national health. Achieving this goal not only requires the deep application of big data technology but also the modern redefinition of traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) culture and the collaborative efforts of multiple stakeholders, forming a comprehensive approach of "data-driven, culture-empowered, and multi-stakeholder involvement."

I. Data-Driven: Big Data Technology Empowering "Preventive Medicine"

The concept of "preventive medicine" is a crystallization of wisdom in traditional Chinese medicine, emphasizing "prevention is better than cure." Driven by modern technology, big data technology provides strong technical support for "preventive medicine," helping it regain new vitality.

  • Precision Portrait and Risk Prediction: Big data technology integrates multi-dimensional massive data such as personal health records, lifestyle, and environmental factors to build precise health portraits for individuals and identify potential high-risk groups. Using advanced machine learning algorithms, big data can dynamically predict disease trends and issue early warning messages, truly achieving "prevention before it happens."
  • Personalized Intervention and Health Management: Personalized health management solutions based on big data analysis can tailor intervention measures covering diet, exercise, and rest according to individual circumstances, significantly improving the efficiency and effectiveness of health management. For example, Zhejiang Province's "Health Brain" platform monitors residents' health conditions in real time and provides precise health management services, greatly enhancing residents' health levels.
  • Building a Smart "Preventive Medicine" System: To fully leverage the role of big data in "preventive medicine," we need to build a systematic and intelligent "preventive medicine" system. This system includes a comprehensive data collection network, an efficient data analysis platform, intelligent intervention tools, and strict data security mechanisms. Only through the construction of this system can big data be fully empowered in "preventive medicine."

II. Culture-Empowered: Modern Value Reconstruction of Traditional Health Wisdom

Traditional Chinese health wisdom, accumulated over five thousand years of Chinese culture, is deeply integrated with modern medical concepts and regaining new life globally.

  • Integration of Traditional Health Wisdom and Modern Preventive Medicine: The concept of "preventive medicine" aligns highly with modern medical precision prevention, both emphasizing early intervention and prevention. For example, the theory of "yin-yang balance" in traditional Chinese medicine resonates with modern medical fields such as immune regulation and endocrine balance, providing new perspectives for modern medical research.
  • Promotion and Innovation of TCM Culture: The promotion of TCM culture is diversifying through integration with modern technology and new media. For instance, during the COVID-19 pandemic, the unique advantages of TCM were widely recognized and provided valuable Chinese experience for global pandemic prevention and control. Such practices also strongly support the global promotion and application of TCM.
  • TCM's Practice in Health China Construction: TCM demonstrates broad application prospects in chronic disease management and health promotion. Personalized TCM adjustment plans can effectively manage and prevent chronic diseases; meanwhile, integrating TCM health concepts into the national fitness program can open new pathways for national health.

III. Multi-Stakeholder Participation: Co-Constructing and Sharing Health China Ecosystem

The realization of the Health China strategy cannot rely solely on the unilateral efforts of the government; instead, it requires building a multi-stakeholder, co-constructed, and shared ecosystem, with the government, market, social organizations, and citizens collectively contributing and forming synergy.

  • Stakeholders of the Health China Ecosystem: The Health China ecosystem comprises four main stakeholders: the government, market, social organizations, and citizens. The government plays the role of leader and promoter; the market satisfies diverse health needs through offering health products and services; social organizations serve as bridges and connectors, facilitating efficient resource allocation; and citizens are both practitioners of healthy lifestyles and ultimate beneficiaries of health outcomes.
  • Co-Constructing and Sharing Mechanisms: Building the Health China ecosystem requires establishing comprehensive policy guidance mechanisms, benefit coordination mechanisms, information sharing mechanisms, and social supervision mechanisms to ensure that all stakeholders balance their own interests with social public interests, achieving win-win outcomes.
  • Exploration of Practical Pathways: By promoting the integrated development of the health industry, strengthening health technology innovation, deepening medical and health system reforms, and enhancing health education and promotion, we can effectively build a multi-stakeholder Health China ecosystem. For example, Beijing's "Smart Family Doctor" project integrates residents' health data to provide community doctors with intelligent auxiliary diagnostic and health management tools, significantly improving the efficiency and quality of family doctor services.

IV. Challenges and Outlook

Although big data technology and traditional health wisdom bring great opportunities to Health China construction, challenges such as data silos, data quality, and talent shortages still exist. In the future, with continuous technological advancements and policy improvements, big data will play an even more significant role in the "preventive medicine" field, and traditional TCM culture will serve national health in more modernized ways.

Conclusion

The realization of the Health China strategy requires the organic integration of "data-driven, culture-empowered, and multi-stakeholder participation." Big data technology provides precise tools for "preventive medicine," traditional health wisdom offers a deep cultural foundation for health management, and the multi-stakeholder health ecosystem ensures the co-construction and sharing of national health. By building a "data-driven, culture-empowered, multi-stakeholder" Health China ecosystem, we can shift from "reactive medical care" to "proactive health," providing strong support for achieving the Health China strategy goals and contributing Chinese wisdom to global health.
