Introduction

The invariance of norms, as one of the core concepts of modern physics, ensures the form-invariance of physical laws under different reference frames or norms. This principle not only plays a central role in quantum mechanics and quantum field theory but also serves as a fundamental aspect in electrodynamics, particle physics, and other fields. However, can the concept of invariance of norms transcend the boundaries of physics and extend into neuroscience and social sciences? This article delves into the applications of invariance of norms in quantum entanglement, brain neural networks, and social norms, revealing the profound connections among symmetry and nonlocality, robustness and generalization abilities, as well as the stability and transformation of social systems through this interdisciplinary perspective.
