Comprehensive Perspective on Market Power

Interweaving Psychology, Social Networks, and Environmental Sustainability

Multidimensional Impact of Market Power

Market power extends beyond economic indicators, involving multiple dimensions such as psychology, social networks, and environmental sustainability. This article will explore the complex interplay within these areas and their collective impact.

Psychological Perspective: Consumer and Corporate Psychological Tactics

The psychological perspective of market power reveals the significant influence of consumer mental states, cognitive biases, and emotional responses on market control. Brand loyalty is not only based on a product's functionality but also on consumers' deep identification with the brand's culture and design philosophy.

Social Network Analysis Perspective: Dynamic Impact on Market Structure

The social network analysis perspective of market power emphasizes the complex connections among enterprises, suppliers, distributors, and consumers and their impact on market power. Key nodes within social networks exert profound influence on market power by controlling the flow of information, resource allocation, and market dynamics.

Environmental Sustainability Perspective: The Double-Edged Impact of Market Power

With growing global concern for environmental sustainability, the environmental sustainability perspective of market power has become particularly important. The environmental policies and practices of industry leaders can shape sustainability standards across the industry, yet they may also become barriers to progress.

Collective Impact and Future Prospects

The comprehensive perspective on market power offers a new lens to understand market dynamics and brand competition. As research deepens in psychology, social network analysis, and environmental sustainability, we will gain a more holistic understanding of the multidimensional impact of market power.
