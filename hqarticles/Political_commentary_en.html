I. The Mirror of Data: Political Portraits and the Demystification of Power

The concept of "political portraits," akin to a mirror of data, reveals the digital contours of political figures' positions, values, and policy inclinations through real-time capture and analysis of their public data. By using natural language processing to analyze speech texts, algorithms can identify the inclinations of different political figures on issues such as economic policies, social welfare, and foreign relations. Through clustering analysis of massive data, we can even outline an ideological spectrum across political parties, constructing a fine-grained political map.

This data-driven analysis does not stop at the individual level but can extend to group analysis. By tracking the interaction patterns among political figures and the correlations of policy positions, the mirror of data can outline complex power networks. II. The Political Forest: The Metaphor of the International Landscape and Survival Strategies

In the "political forest" of international politics, countries act like different "forest animals," following the "laws of the forest," and competing for resources and influence in a survival-of-the-fittest environment. The lion kingdom, with its powerful military force and hegemonic ambitions, attempts to expand its territory through force; the fox alliance, with its cunning intelligence, navigates between powers through flexible alliances and diplomatic means to protect its interests; the bear tribe adopts a conservative strategy, focusing on protecting its territory and resources; and the rabbit tribe, known for its agility and vigilance, survives in the harsh forest through cooperation and migration.

However, the situation in the forest is not static. Over time, the hegemony of the lion kingdom is challenged, the wisdom of the fox alliance faces new tests, the conservative strategy of the bear tribe becomes outdated, and the survival strategies of the rabbit tribe are constantly adjusting. Each animal adapts to the changes and challenges in the forest, searching for its own position and survival strategies.

Archaeological studies show that the "passive resistance" of the Z generation did not lead to the collapse of society but instead gave rise to a new collective consciousness. These seemingly scattered actions eventually triggered the "Decentralized Political Revolution" of 2035, proclaiming that politics is no longer the monopoly of elites, and every ordinary person has the right to participate in social governance.
