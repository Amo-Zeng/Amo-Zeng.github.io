From “New Normal” to “Heart Normal”: Emotional Marketing in the Food Market, Insights into Female Consumers, and Metaverse Innovation

The COVID-19 pandemic was not just a massive geopolitical and economic shock, but also an unprecedented social and psychological revolution. In the post-pandemic era, consumer psychology and behavior have undergone profound changes, and the food industry is facing unprecedented challenges and opportunities. The traditional “New Normal” is no longer sufficient to address the changes of this era; food brands need to shift to a new “Heart Normal,” by employing emotional marketing, gaining insights into the diverse needs of female consumers, and leveraging metaverse technology to establish deeper emotional connections with consumers, thereby winning the market.

Consumer Psychological Changes in the Post-Pandemic Era

The pandemic has changed people's lifestyles and profoundly influenced our psychological state. Consumers' focus has shifted from mere material needs to deeper emotional and psychological needs:

  • Enhanced Sense of Security and Health Awareness: During the pandemic, people's concern for food safety and health surged. Consumers are increasingly inclined to choose brands that provide a sense of security and health assurance.
  • Comfort and Emotional Comfort: Prolonged isolation and social distancing made people feel lonely and anxious. Consumers seek emotional comfort in food, expecting products that bring comfort and psychological solace.
  • Sense of Belonging and Community Awareness: The pandemic made people keenly aware of the importance of community and family. Consumers place greater emphasis on emotional connections with brands, expecting those that bring a sense of belonging and community identity.
  • Sustainability and Social Responsibility: The pandemic exposed the fragility of global supply chains and made consumers more concerned about the origin, production methods, and social responsibilities of food brands. Enterprises with sustainability and social responsibility are more favored.

Emotional Marketing: Connecting with Consumers' Hearts

Facing these psychological changes, food brands need to employ emotional marketing strategies to establish deeper emotional connections with consumers. Here are some effective emotional marketing strategies:

  1. Story Marketing: Creating Emotional Resonance: Consumers are not just buying food, but an emotional experience. Brands can tell compelling stories to evoke emotional resonance. Whether it's the brand's founding story, product development story, or interaction story with consumers, they can all become effective tools for emotional marketing.
  2. Community Building: Establishing Emotional Bonds: In the post-pandemic era, community awareness and belonging are particularly important. Food brands can create online and offline community platforms to establish emotional bonds with consumers. For example, they can organize online cooking classes, community group purchasing activities, or offline tasting events to make consumers feel the warmth and support of the brand.
  3. Social Responsibility: Delivering Emotional Value: In the post-pandemic era, consumers pay more attention to the social responsibility of brands. Food brands can participate in public welfare activities, support environmental projects, etc., to deliver emotional value and win the trust and support of consumers.
  4. Experience Marketing: Creating Emotional Memories: Food is not just a material enjoyment, but also an emotional experience. Brands can create unique consumption experiences to leave consumers with deep emotional memories. For example, they can launch limited-edition packaging designs, personalized customization services, or collaborate with artists to launch co-branded products.

“She Economy”: Insights into the Diverse Needs of Female Consumers

In today's economic society, the rise of the “She Economy” cannot be ignored. With the continuous improvement of women's status in various fields such as society, economy, and technology, their consumption power has also increased, gradually becoming the main force in the food market. However, the needs of female consumers are not single; they have more diverse and personalized expectations for food. If food brands want to seize the opportunity in the era of the “She Economy,” they must deeply understand the diverse needs of female consumers and unlock the market growth code.

  • Focus on Health and Nutrition: Female consumers pay more attention to the nutritional value and health attributes of food, preferring to choose foods that are low in sugar and fat, high in fiber, and rich in vitamins and minerals to meet their health needs.
  • Pursuit of Convenience and Efficiency: Modern women face multiple pressures in life and work, and time has become a scarce resource. Therefore, they are more inclined to choose convenient and fast foods, such as ready-to-eat foods, frozen foods, functional beverages, etc., to save time and improve life efficiency.
  • Emphasis on Emotions and Experiences: Female consumers not only focus on the material attributes of food but also pay attention to the emotional experience brought by food. They hope to meet their emotional needs through food, such as joy, relaxation, comfort, etc.
  • Pursuit of Personalization and Differentiation: With the awakening of self-awareness, women pay more attention to expressing their individuality and meeting differentiated needs. The needs of women in different age groups, professions, and lifestyles are different.
  • Attention to Brand and Social Responsibility: Female consumers also consider the brand's values and social responsibility when choosing food. They are more inclined to support brands that have sustainable development concepts, pay attention to environmental protection, and public welfare.

How food brands can meet the diverse needs of female consumers:

  • Health and Nutrition: Creating “Her Exclusive” Health Solutions: Brands can develop targeted health products based on women's physiological characteristics and health needs. For example, for young women's anti-aging needs, launch foods rich in collagen and antioxidants; for the nutritional needs of pregnant women, launch foods rich in folic acid, iron, and calcium; for the bone health needs of middle-aged women, launch foods rich in calcium and vitamin D.
  • Convenience and Efficiency: Providing “Her Needed” Convenient Solutions: Brands can innovate product forms and packaging designs to provide convenient and efficient food solutions for women. For example, they can launch small-packaged, portable ready-to-eat foods to provide energy for women anytime and anywhere in busy work and life; they can launch pre-made dishes, frozen foods, etc., to reduce women's cooking time and improve life efficiency.
  • Emotions and Experiences: Creating “Her Aspiration” Emotional Value: Brands can employ emotional marketing strategies to create unique emotional experiences and win women's hearts. For example, they can tell stories to combine the product's development background, brand philosophy with women's emotional needs, evoking their resonance; they can create immersive consumption scenes, such as cafes, dessert shops, etc., to provide women with relaxation and joyful emotional experiences.
  • Personalization and Differentiation: Meeting “Her Unique” Needs: Brands need to conduct precise market segmentation to provide differentiated products and services for different female groups. For example, they can launch foods suitable for different age groups based on the needs of women in different age groups; they can launch foods suitable for professional women, housewives, sports enthusiasts based on the needs of women with different lifestyles; they can launch foods for special dietary needs such as vegetarian, ketogenic, gluten-free, etc.
  • Brand and Social Responsibility: Delivering “Her Identification” Value Concepts: Brands can win women's recognition and support by delivering sustainable development concepts, paying attention to environmental protection, and public welfare. For example, they can adopt renewable resources, reduce carbon emissions, support women's entrepreneurship, etc., to demonstrate the brand's social responsibility and values.

Food Metaverse: The Collision of Virtual and Reality, Creating Immersive Brand Experiences

With the rise of the metaverse concept, all industries are exploring how to open up new possibilities in this virtual world. The food industry is no exception. The metaverse provides unprecedented opportunities for food brands to create immersive brand experiences in the virtual world and establish closer connections with consumers.

  • Virtual Tasting: A Trans-Regional Taste Experience: Metaverse technology can allow consumers to “taste” products in the virtual world through virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) technology. Brands can establish virtual tasting experience zones in the metaverse, where consumers can use VR devices or AR applications to taste virtual food, feel the product's taste, aroma, and texture.
  • Immersive Experience: Creating Multi-Sensory Food Journeys: Metaverse technology can not only simulate taste experiences but also create multi-sensory immersive experiences through virtual environments. Brands can build virtual food factories, farms, or cooking classrooms in the metaverse, where consumers can use VR devices to visit the food production process, understand the source of raw materials, production technology, and food safety measures. In addition, brands can also hold cooking classes, wine tasting events, etc., in the virtual environment, allowing consumers to experience the process of making food and tasting fun in the virtual world.
  • Digital Collectibles: Giving Food Unique Digital Identities: Metaverse technology can also give food unique digital identities, creating digital collectibles (NFTs) through blockchain technology. Brands can convert limited-edition food packaging designs, brand stories, or food processing technology into digital collectibles, and consumers can obtain exclusive digital identities and ownership by purchasing these digital collectibles. This not only provides consumers with unique collection value but also creates new marketing channels and revenue sources for brands.

How food brands can use metaverse technology to create immersive brand experiences:

  • Integrating Virtual and Reality: Building Seamless Brand Experiences: Brands can establish virtual experience zones in the metaverse and provide corresponding physical products and services in the real world. For example, brands can provide virtual tasting experiences in the metaverse and offer offline tasting sessions or delivery services in the real world. By integrating virtual and reality, brands can provide consumers with consistent brand experiences, enhancing their participation and loyalty.
  • Creating Interaction and Socialization: Establishing Virtual Communities: Brands can create virtual social platforms in the metaverse, allowing consumers to interact and communicate in the virtual world. Brands can organize virtual cooking competitions, food sharing events, etc., to encourage consumers to share their food experiences and creativity in the virtual community. Through interaction and socialization, brands can enhance consumers' participation and sense of belonging, establishing closer brand connections.
  • Delivering Brand Values: Creating Virtual Brand Ambassadors: Brands can create virtual brand ambassadors, using virtual characters or avatars to deliver the brand's philosophy and stories to consumers. Virtual brand ambassadors can interact with consumers in the metaverse, share the brand's history, product knowledge, and health advice, helping consumers better understand the brand and products. Through virtual brand ambassadors, brands can establish a more intimate brand image in the virtual world, enhancing consumers' trust and recognition.

In today's food market marketing, the traditional “New Normal” is no longer sufficient to address the profound changes in consumer psychology and behavior. Food brands need to shift to a new “Heart Normal,” by employing emotional marketing, gaining insights into the diverse needs of female consumers, and leveraging metaverse technology to establish deeper emotional connections with consumers, thereby winning the market.

Through emotional marketing, food brands can build lasting emotional bonds in consumers' hearts, becoming an indispensable part of their lives. Whether through story marketing, community building, social responsibility, or experience marketing, brands need to deeply explore consumers' emotional needs and establish deeper emotional connections.

As women's status in various fields such as society, economy, and technology continues to improve, their consumption power also increases, gradually becoming the main force in the food market. However, the needs of female consumers are not single; they have more diverse and personalized expectations for food. Food brands need to deeply understand the diverse needs of female consumers and employ diversified strategies to meet their expectations in the era of the “She Economy.”

Metaverse technology brings unprecedented innovation opportunities to the food industry. Food brands can create immersive brand experiences in the virtual world through virtual tasting, immersive experiences, and digital collectibles, establishing closer connections with consumers. Facing the challenges and opportunities of the metaverse, food brands need to actively explore and innovate, using metaverse technology to enhance brand experiences and win consumers' favor.
