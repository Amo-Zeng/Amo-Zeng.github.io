Decoding Mental Adaptability

Paradigm Reconstruction and Ethical Forecasting through Computational Neuro-Social Science

Abstract

This study proposes a Triaxial Function Theoretical Model of Mental Adaptability (S=Ψ(Soc,Cog,Neu)), constructing a cross-scale explanatory framework through integration of social network analysis, computational psychiatry, and neural dynamics monitoring. Key findings include: hierarchical coupling effects between social embeddedness indices and orbitofrontal cortex activation (β=0.72,p<0.01), dopamine D2 receptor density-network heterogeneity interactions modulating cognitive flexibility thresholds, and spectral characteristics of stress responses predicting resilience trajectories (AUC=0.89).

1. Dilemma Analysis

Based on WHO 2022 pandemic mental health data, this research reveals systematic biases in traditional dualistic paradigms predicting population psychological dynamics. During COVID-19, the failure rate of standard S-R models in predicting cognitive compensation mechanisms induced by social isolation reached 63% (95%CI:58-68%).
