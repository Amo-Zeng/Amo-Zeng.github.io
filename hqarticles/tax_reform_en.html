Tax Reform: Building a Comprehensive Support System for Green Development and Common Prosperity

As a crucial tool for economic regulation, tax reform not only has a profound impact on the survival and development of businesses but also significantly influences social fairness, environmental protection, and economic sustainability. This article explores the limitations of the current tax system and proposes a new approach to building a comprehensive, diversified, and green tax system.

Current Analysis: Limitations of the Current Tax System and Opportunities for Reform

The current tax system still faces numerous challenges in supporting the development of SMEs, regulating income distribution, and promoting green transformation.

  • Tax Challenges for SMEs Development: The unified tax rate and tax incentives do not fully address the unique needs of SMEs at different stages of development.
  • Ineffectiveness of Personal Income Tax Regulation: The single progressive tax rate structure is unable to adapt to the reality of diversified income sources.
  • Shortcomings of the Environmental Protection Tax System: The narrow scope of the environmental protection tax and the low tax rates make it difficult to comprehensively address complex pollution issues.

New Approach: Building a Comprehensive, Diversified, and Green Tax System

Tax reform should focus on the following aspects:

  1. Establishing a "Incubation-Growth-Exit" Tax Support System: Guided by the life cycle of enterprises, provide targeted tax incentives.
  2. Developing a Diversified Personal Income Tax System: Move away from a single tax rate mindset to adapt to the reality of diversified income sources.
  3. Creating a "Green Tax System": Expand the scope of the environmental protection tax, set differentiated tax rates, and promote green development.

Conclusion

Tax reform is not just an optimization of economic regulation tools but also a profound response to the needs of SME development, income distribution fairness, and environmental protection. By building a comprehensive tax support system, a diversified personal income tax system, and a "green tax system," tax reform will provide SMEs with broader development opportunities, reduce income disparities, and promote green development.
