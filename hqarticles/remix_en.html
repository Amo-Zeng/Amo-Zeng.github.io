Mixing: From Blind Test Revolution to Sound Architecture in the Metaverse

In recent years, the music production field has undergone profound changes, from the rise of blind test mixing, the transformation of mix engineers into emotion conveyors, to the emergence of music as an 'architectural' element in the Metaverse. Mixing, a core component of music production, is experiencing unprecedented innovation.

Blind Test Revolution: A Return to Art or a Triumph of Technology?

Mixing has long been considered the perfect combination of science and art. It requires precise control over technical parameters like audio signals, sound fields, and dynamics, as well as a keen grasp of musicality and emotional expression. However, with the rapid advancements in digital audio technology and post-production software, mixing techniques have become increasingly complex, creating countless awe-inspiring sound effects. At the same time, these sophisticated technical methods can also be a double-edged sword, enhancing sound quality while potentially causing people to lose their way in the maze of technology, overlooking the essence of music—emotional transmission and resonance.

Against this backdrop, "blind test mixing" has emerged as a new trend that shakes the traditional mixing art hall. By stripping away visual distractions, mix engineers can focus on the music itself, returning to pure auditory experiences. This is seen as an attempt to return to the original intention, helping mix engineers to break free from the "halo effect" and "bias effect," allowing the final work to more purely reflect the creator's intent. However, blind test mixing has also sparked controversy. Some argue that this method overemphasizes objectivity, neglecting the artistic nature of mixing, potentially stifling the mix engineer's individuality and creativity, stripping the work of its soul and vitality.

In fact, blind test mixing is not a panacea; it cannot evaluate the overall atmosphere, spatial feel, and dynamic range of a mix, which are key elements of mixing art. It also cannot completely eliminate subjective auditory preferences. Nevertheless, blind test mixing still provides mix engineers with a new way of thinking and working methods, prompting them to break out of established thinking patterns, focus more on the music itself, and enhance their professional skills and artistic cultivation.

Emotional Resonance: Transformation from Sound Design to 'Superpowers'

Music is a carrier of emotions, and mixing is the 'alchemy' that gives music its soul. Traditional mix engineers might be seen as silent 'sound tuners' in the background, mechanically adjusting volume and equalization. However, truly excellent mix engineers are sound directors with 'superpowers,' using sound as a brush and mixing as a medium to construct scenes, shape characters, and tell stories in the world of music, ultimately transmitting emotional resonance to every listener.

By analyzing classic song mixing cases, we can more intuitively understand how mix engineers evoke emotional resonance through sound design. For example, in Adele's "Someone Like You," the mix engineer uses exquisite reverb processing to create a ethereal and sacred atmosphere, drawing listeners into the emotional vortex of the song. Michael Jackson's "Billie Jean" uses precise dynamic processing to enhance the rhythm and auditory impact.

In an interview, mix engineer Mike Peterson emphasized the core value of mix engineers is "emotional transmission." He believes that mix engineers are not just adjusting sounds but shaping the emotional expression of music. Every song has a story behind it, and the mix engineer's job is to make this story "speak" more beautifully and movingly with sound.

Sound Architecture in the Metaverse: Shaping the Future Soundscape

The Metaverse, as a digital space that integrates virtual reality, augmented reality, and internet technology, is gradually becoming part of human society. In this world built by code and data, music is no longer just background melody but an 'architectural' element that shapes the environment, emotions, and experiences. Mixing technology, as a core component of music creation and production, is facing unprecedented challenges and opportunities.

In the Metaverse, music transmission is no longer limited by the three-dimensional space of the physical world but can flow freely in countless virtual dimensions. This is both a challenge and an opportunity for mix engineers. They need to create realistic sound environments in virtual space, allowing listeners to feel the depth, breadth, and directionality of sound. At the same time, music creation in the Metaverse also needs to consider multi-user interaction in virtual environments, ensuring consistency and coherence of sound between different users while maintaining unique auditory experiences for each user.
