Artificial Intelligence and Data Sovereignty

Data Sovereignty: Geopolitical and Technological Contest in the AI Era

As generative AI technologies transcend language and national borders, data sovereignty has become a strategic concern that governments must directly address. The EU’s AI Act and China’s Measures for Security Assessment of Data出境印证 (note: this should probably be the "Data Export Security Assessment Measures") jointly show that AI development must operate within the sovereign framework of data governance.

Three Dimensions of Sovereignty Challenges

Global deployment of AI technology is reshaping the boundaries of data sovereignty:

  • Data Jurisdiction Conflicts: Cross-border AI training data may simultaneously trigger compliance obligations under GDPR and China’s Personal Information Protection Law
  • Battle for Algorithmic Decision-Making: Military AI applications blur the boundaries between traditional state authority and private algorithms
  • Risk of Technological Dependence: Outsourcing AI in critical infrastructure leads to loss of core decision-making capabilities

Four-Layer Architecture for Sovereignty Protection

Building a data sovereignty defense system requires multi-dimensional coordination:

  1. Technological Sovereignty: Develop localized foundational large models such as Qwen and ERNIE Bot
  2. Data Classification and Grading: Establish sensitive data catalogs in accordance with the Data Security Law
  3. Computing Infrastructure Regulation: Implement security assessments for cloud computing services
  4. International Rule-Making: Participate in the formulation of global AI ethics standards

Innovative Regulatory Practices

Countries are exploring new regulatory models adapted to AI development:

  • Regulatory sandboxes: Experimental oversight of AI medical diagnostics by the UK Information Commissioner's Office
  • Reverse long-arm jurisdiction: China’s Anti-Foreign Sanctions Law establishes countermeasures for cross-border data requests
  • Traceability of responsibility: The EU AI Act requires full life-cycle logging

Dynamic Balance Between Sovereignty and Innovation

Data sovereignty should not be an isolationist barrier, but a mechanism to build new forms of trust:

• Establish a blockchain-based federated learning system for cross-border data usage without data exposure

• Develop sovereign digital identity systems ensuring citizen data autonomy

• Promote governance of AI open-source communities to build a controllable algorithm ecosystem
