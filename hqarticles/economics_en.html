New Paradigms in Global Economic Governance: Challenges and Strategies

The current global economic governance system faces multiple challenges, including the rise of protectionism, the vulnerability of global supply chains, and the emergence of new economies. To address these challenges, it is necessary to explore the establishment of a more inclusive and responsive new paradigm for global economic governance.

Challenges in Current Global Economic Governance

  1. Rise of Protectionism: Increases in tariffs and non-tariff measures between nations threaten global economic order, potentially leading to instability in the global trade system.
  2. Vulnerability of Global Supply Chains: The COVID-19 pandemic exposed risks of supply chain disruptions, impacting production, logistics, and market equilibrium, challenging the stability and resilience of the global economy.
  3. Emergence of New Economies: The rapid economic growth and international influence of emerging economies necessitate enhancing their representation and participation in existing governance systems.

Establishing a More Inclusive and Responsive Structure for Global Economic Governance

  1. Strengthening International Cooperation: Enhancing collaboration in multilateral institutions to promote trade liberalization, investment facilitation, and financial stabilization.
  2. Optimizing Resource Distribution: Focusing on fair resource distribution to foster green, sustainable, and inclusive growth, requiring collective efforts from the international community.
  3. Designing Mechanisms to Address Transnational Economic Risks and Crises: Establishing flexible, efficient crisis management mechanisms, including early warning systems, emergency relief mechanisms, and international financial safety nets.

Specific Recommendations and Action Plans

  1. Promoting Reform of Multilateral Trade Systems: Reforming the World Trade Organization, strengthening its core role, and promoting the openness and inclusiveness of regional trade agreements.
  2. Strengthening Supply Chain Management: Enhancing supply chain transparency and resilience through diversified layouts and risk management to reduce disruption risks.
  3. Promoting Inclusion of New Economies: Supporting their active participation in global economic governance, enhancing their representation and voice in international organizations.

Conclusion

Building a new paradigm for global economic governance requires joint collaboration. By strengthening international cooperation, optimizing resource allocation, and designing effective mechanisms, we can modernize and make the governance system more inclusive, achieving a more stable, fair, and sustainable global economic development.
