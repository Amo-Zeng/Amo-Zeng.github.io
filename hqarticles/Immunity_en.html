The Coevolutionary Two-Way Adaptation Between Host and Symbiotic Microbiome

Introduction: Quantum Game Framework for Host-Microbe Interactions

Core Darwinian paradigms face systemic challenges. Traditional linear "survival of the fittest" logic fails to explain precise regulation between host immunity and symbiotic microbiota. A quantum game theory framework offers new insights: microbiome diversity mirrors quantum superposition states, while immune regulation acts as "measurement operators".

2023 Science research employing single-cell multi-omics revealed that abundance fluctuations of Faecalacterium and Bacteroides are driven by host IgA secretion-microbiome metabolite feedback loops. This dynamic equilibrium positions the microbiome as the "driver" of host phenotypic plasticity.
