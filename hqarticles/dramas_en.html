Television Series: Resonators of Social Sentiment and Exporters of Cultural Values

Television series, as a cultural product deeply ingrained in people's lives, have long transcended mere entertainment, evolving into a powerful medium that reflects social realities, sparks public discourse, and shapes cultural values. Like resonators of social sentiment, they keenly capture the pulse of the times and evoke resonance among audiences. They also serve as exporters of cultural values, subtly conveying specific cultural symbols and values, shaping people's spiritual world.

1. Emotional Resonance and Value Guidance: The Two-Way Interaction between Television Series and Social Sentiment

Excellent television series are always rooted in reality, depicting the complexities of society with a keen perspective and profound strokes, evoking emotional resonance among viewers. Whether it's "All Is Well" and "A Little Reunion" with their delicate portrayal of family ethics, "Ordinary Glory" and "Minning Town" with their realistic depiction of the workplace, or "The Long Night" and "In the Name of the People" with their courageous exposure of social issues, these works are like barometers of social sentiment, authentically reflecting the joys and sorrows of people amidst the changes and development of the times. While watching, viewers can find reflections of themselves in the experiences of the characters, feel the temperature and pulse of social sentiment, and thus trigger reflections on themselves, society, and life.

Television series not only reflect social sentiment but also guide it, becoming disseminators of values. In recent years, a number of mainstream television series have emerged, such as "Minning Town," "The Age of Awakening," and "Medal of the Republic," which vividly present the grand journey of the Communist Party of China leading the people in revolution, construction, and reform, with their exquisite production and touching stories. They have shaped a group of respectable, lovable, and amiable role models of the times, igniting viewers' patriotic enthusiasm and national pride. The success of these works lies not only in their guaranteed ratings but also in their positive impact on society, guiding people to establish correct values and outlook on life, and凝聚ing the spiritual force to strive for a better future.

However, as a mass cultural product, television series also need to be wary of potential risks in guiding social sentiment. Some works excessively pursuing commercial interests, in order to attract attention, resort to exaggerated and distorted methods, amplifying negative emotions, spreading negative values, and even triggering social anxiety. In this regard, we should strengthen the guidance and management of television series creation, encourage the creation of more outstanding works that promote mainstream values and convey positive energy, and give full play to the positive role of television series in reflecting social sentiment and guiding social thought, contributing to the construction of a harmonious and beautiful society.

2. Algorithm Recommendation: A New Creative Ecology with Both Opportunities and Challenges

The popularization of the internet and mobile terminals has brought about earth-shattering changes to the film and television industry. It has also given rise to the era of "algorithm recommendation," presenting new challenges and opportunities for the creation of television series.

On the one hand, algorithm recommendation has brought about challenges such as "traffic is king," limited content innovation, data-driven creation, undervaluation of artistic value, audience segmentation, and hindered cultural transmission. Platforms, to cater to algorithms, overly rely on formulaic creation, resulting in serious content homogenization. Excessive reliance on data indicators has led some creators to excessively pursue sensory stimulation, neglecting the refinement of character development, plot logic, and emotional expression, resulting in a decline in the overall quality of the work. Algorithm recommendation can easily lead audiences to be confined to their own interest circles, exposed to a single type of work, which is not conducive to the dissemination and exchange of diverse cultures.

On the other hand, algorithm recommendation has also brought about opportunities for the creation of television series, such as precise user reach, improved creation efficiency, reduced distribution costs, expanded influence of works, promoted content innovation, and expanded thematic boundaries. Creators can utilize data analysis provided by algorithm recommendation to better understand market demands and user preferences, using them as references for creation, improving creation efficiency and the market acceptance of their works. Algorithm recommendation can accurately push works to potential audiences, reducing the cost of traditional publicity and distribution, and providing more opportunities for small and medium-cost works with novel themes to stand out.

Faced with the opportunities and challenges brought by algorithm recommendation, the creation of television series needs to actively seek coping strategies. Creators should always prioritize content quality, delve deeply into real life, shape vivid characters, construct logically sound and engaging stories, and win audience recognition with high-quality content. Platforms also need to continuously optimize algorithm recommendation mechanisms, paying attention not only to data indicators but also to the artistic value and social benefits of works, providing more exposure opportunities for outstanding works and guiding the healthy development of the industry.

3. Cultural Symbols and Values: Television Series as Envoys of Cultural Transmission

Television series are not only resonators of social sentiment but also exporters of cultural values. By shaping vivid characters, constructing storylines, and presenting social landscapes, they subtly convey specific cultural symbols and values to audiences, playing an important role in cross-cultural communication and national image building.

The integration of cultural symbols makes television series a window for cultural transmission. Series often incorporate cultural symbols with national and regional characteristics into their plots and scenes, such as tea ceremony, calligraphy, and traditional Chinese medicine in costume dramas, local cuisines, traditional festivals, and folk customs in modern dramas. These symbols are like cultural codes, showcasing the profoundness of Chinese culture to the audience. For example, "Nirvana in Fire" showcases traditional Chinese etiquette, clothing culture, and strategic wisdom; "A Bite of China" meticulously depicts various local cuisines; and "All Is Well" reflects Chinese-style family relationships, all of which have resonated with audiences and sparked their interest in and identification with Chinese culture.

The transmission of values endows television series with ideological depth and social responsibility. An excellent television series can often reflect the spirit of the times, respond to social concerns, and convey positive values. For example, "The Age of Awakening" promotes patriotism and revolutionary heroism by portraying the struggles of revolutionary pioneers; "Minning Town" showcases China's remarkable achievements in poverty alleviation, conveying the positive energy of striving to change one's destiny; and "A Lifelong Journey" reflects the development and progress of Chinese society through the vicissitudes of several generations, praising the fighting spirit and inherent kindness of ordinary people. These television series have not only achieved high ratings but also evoked strong resonance and profound reflection among viewers, subtly guiding the direction of social values.

However, television series also face challenges in cultural export and value transmission, such as the risks of cultural discount and stereotypes, the tendency towards entertainment over values, and the lack of cultural innovation in homogenized competition. To better leverage the role of television series in cultural export and value transmission, creators need to uphold cultural confidence, tell good Chinese stories, adhere to value guidance, convey positive social energy, and persist in upholding integrity and innovation to enhance the cultural connotation of their works.

Conclusion

Television series are both resonators of social sentiment and exporters of cultural values. They reflect the changes of the times, the progress of society, and the spiritual outlook of the people, while also bearing the responsibility of spreading culture and shaping values. It is believed that in the future, television series will continue to play their unique social function, resonate more deeply with social sentiment, and contribute to building a harmonious and beautiful society and promoting outstanding Chinese culture.
