I. Deconstructing Bureaucracy: Institutional Genes of "Risk-Transfer Mechanisms"

The inherent paradox of modern bureaucracy, as Weber observed, manifests when top-down command chains encounter multi-level information decay. The resulting ambiguous zones of responsibility form "structural voids" in institutional design. Within this context, grassroots governance develops unique risk-aversion strategies...

  • Concretization of Luhmann's "Risk Society" theory in bureaucratic organizations
