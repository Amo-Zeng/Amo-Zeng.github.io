The Future of the Data Value Chain: From Democratization to Servitization, and Beyond to Virtual-Physical Convergence

In the era of the digital economy, data has become a core productive factor driving social progress, often referred to as the "new oil." However, for a long time, data resources have been monopolized by a few platform giants, creating information silos, resulting in the loss of individual data sovereignty, imbalanced value distribution, and hindering social innovation vitality. The future of the data value chain will evolve through three stages: data democratization, data as a service, and virtual-physical convergence, collectively building a more equitable, open, and co-creative digital future.

I. Data Democratization: Breaking Monopolies, Reshaping the Value Chain

Data monopolies have brought about three key issues: the loss of individual data sovereignty, imbalanced value distribution, and hindered innovation vitality. Data democratization aims to break data monopolies, returning data to its true creators—individuals—and through the establishment of fair, open data-sharing mechanisms, unleashing the value of data.

  • Empowering Individuals: By implementing data ownership and authorization management, ensuring individuals' rights to know, control, and benefit from their personal data.
  • Open Sharing: Building decentralized data-sharing platforms to break information silos and promote secure, trustworthy data circulation.
  • Value Co-creation: Driving deep integration of data with technology and scenarios, forming a value creation system with multiple stakeholders participating.

II. Data as a Service: Paradigm Shift from Product Thinking to Service Thinking

Data as a Service (DaaS) is a customer-centric approach that emphasizes creating data-driven value through dynamic, personalized, and continuous services.

  • Core Elements: Continuity, interactivity, and dynamism.
  • Implementation Path: Enterprises need to establish agile data service platforms, breaking down data barriers to achieve real-time data flow and intelligent matching.

III. Virtual-Physical Convergence: Data-Driven Digital Twins and Metaverse

Digital twins, through the collection, analysis, and simulation of massive data, construct virtual replicas of the real world, reflecting real-time conditions and providing data support for decision-making. The metaverse is a virtual space parallel to the real world where users can engage in social, entertainment, learning, and other activities.

  • Application Scenarios: Smart manufacturing, smart cities, healthcare, and other fields.
  • Technical Support: Virtual Reality (VR), Augmented Reality (AR), Artificial Intelligence (AI), and other technologies.

Conclusion: A New Era of Data-Driven Digital Civilization

The future of the data value chain is an evolutionary process from democratization to servitization, and then to virtual-physical convergence. Data democratization will break monopolies and promote social equity; data as a service will reshape business models and drive value creation; virtual-physical convergence will open a new chapter in digital civilization. Let us embrace this historical opportunity together and move forward into a new era of data-driven digital civilization!
