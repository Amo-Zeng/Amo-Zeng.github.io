The Essence of the Universe Lies in the Participation of Cognition

Traditional physics observes the world from an external perspective, treating natural laws as objective entities independent of consciousness. However, in the growing convergence between contemporary physics and cognitive science, a new insight is emerging: the universe is not a "separate external existence" disconnected from the observer, but rather an outcome that “emerges” through information interactions and cognitive processes.

The Universe as a Dynamic Expression of Information

Quantum mechanics reveals how the act of measurement affects the state of a system. The superposition and collapse mechanisms of quantum states suggest that the "reality" of the universe is not pre-defined, but is constructed during the process of information processing. In other words, information is not merely a tool for describing existence—it is the foundation of existence itself.

Cognition as the Subject of Information Processing

Observers are not only detectors of physical systems but also integral parts of the universe's structure. Human consciousness, animal cognitive systems, and artificial intelligence’s capacities for perception and reasoning, all represent the universe’s information structure "self-perceiving" and "self-evolving" at different levels.

The Emergence of the Universe: A Phenomenal World Growing from Quantum States

Quantum information theory further suggests that macroscopic reality is not fundamental—it emerges from higher-dimensional quantum entanglement and computation. This means the world we perceive is only a localized manifestation layer of a deeper cosmic structure.

  • Observation participates in the construction
  • Cognition is the mechanism of emergence

Looking Forward: Understanding How We Understand the Universe

With the development of artificial cognitive systems, we can imagine using intelligent agents and quantum computing to simulate how the universe constructs the space, time, matter, and consciousness we experience based on information. This is not merely a deepening of science, but also a transformation of philosophy.
