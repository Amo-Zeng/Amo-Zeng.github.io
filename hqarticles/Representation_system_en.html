Fluid Functional Representation: Paradigm Shift and Institutional Restructuring

Introduction: Legitimacy Crisis and Evolutionary Context of Representation in the Digital Age

Human political civilization is undergoing radical transformation in the digital revolution, where the fundamental proposition of "being represented" faces existential challenges. This paper integrates theories of dynamic representation rights, resilient representation systems, and tripartite interaction models to illuminate the inevitable transition from "subject-centric" to "functionally fluid" representation systems under technological disruption, reconstructing the discourse and institutional frameworks of representational legitimacy.

I. Paradigm Deconstruction: Triple Failure Mechanisms of Static Authorization Models

1.1 Linear Authorization and Chronic Issues in High-Velocity Societies

Estonia's digital citizenship case reveals that public opinion cycles on cryptocurrency regulation (Median=17 days) far exceed parliamentary deliberation cycles (120 days). Data from China's City Brain shows administrative response times averaging T+82 hours, with dynamic response gaps reaching 78%.

1.2 Territorial Binding Principles vs. Cyberspace Countereffects

Among 3,556 decision nodes in the Global Carbon Account (GCAP) trading system, only 17% belong to nation-states, exposing a three-tiered legal governance gap.

1.3 Monopolized Representative Capacity and Professionalization Divides

In MakerDAO governance committee's 35 stability fee adjustments, only 22% complied with Gini coefficient optimization criteria. Gitcoin DAO's quadratic voting introduced 8-15% contributor evaluation biases.

Vitalik Buterin's "time-sliced delegation" theory enables split and recombined representation rights. PrimeDAO experiments show dynamic delegation improves governance efficacy (GEE) by 28%.

2.2 Resilient Representation Systems: Cross-Sovereign Network Topology

The "Honduras Post-Disaster Protocol Chain" case demonstrates a 3D network decision resilience index (DRI) of 0.73, surpassing traditional unilateral government response (0.51).

2.3 Tripartite Interaction Model: Human-Machine Hybrid Authority
