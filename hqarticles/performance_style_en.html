Beyond Anthropocentrism: Multidimensional Mirrors and Trans-Species Aesthetics in Performing Arts

In Greek mythology, Narcissus was so captivated by his reflection in the water that he turned into a narcissus flower. This "water mirror" reflecting the inner self has continuously evolved throughout human civilization, eventually crystallizing into an eternal magical mirror in the temple of art—performing arts. As a delicate "cultural prism," performing arts reflect the soul of an era and mirror the dialogue between artists and their times. From Shakespeare's Venetian merchants to Brecht's narrative theatre, from Mei Lanfang's Peking Opera reforms to Pina Bausch's dance theatre, every transformation of art tells the social psychology of different historical stages. This magical mirror is not just a manifestation of artistic forms but also a reflection of the spirit of the times. The humanist spirit of the Renaissance was manifested in theatrical expressions, while the existentialist thought of the 20th century found resonance in the theatre of the absurd. Every cry and every gesture of the artist is reflected in this mirror, showing the expression of the era.

I. The Carnival of the Other: Alternative Aesthetics of Animal Performance

Under human observational perspectives, animal behavior is often simplified as instinctual responses to environmental adaptation. However, when examined from a performance perspective, a fascinating fact emerges: the animal world has already established its unique "performance system." The territorial displays of African lion prides are not just manifestations of strength but also meticulously choreographed "ritual dramas." In this play, the lion's mane, posture, and intimidating roars are all evolved performance symbols over millions of years. The threat displays in baboon communities are perfect "improvised dramas," using complex body language and vocal codes to build social hierarchy recognition mechanisms.

The fundamental difference between these animal performances and human theatrical arts lies in: it exists not for an audience but as a survival strategy. Every movement has its ecological significance, and every performance system is a result of natural selection. This primitive yet pure performance aesthetics prompts us to rethink the origins and ultimate purposes of human performance. By acknowledging the existence of animal performance, the definition of performing arts no longer confines itself to humans but expands into a universal mechanism across species.

II. The Poetics of Algorithms: The AI-Driven Performance Revolution

When human performing arts meet artificial intelligence, an even deeper revolution is happening. Virtual characters created by algorithms, with their precise emotional calculations and expressions beyond human limitations, are reshaping the boundaries of performance aesthetics. In this digital theatre constructed by code, algorithmic performances break through the physical limitations of human performance: they can complete emotional transitions in 0.1 seconds and create choreographies unimaginable by humans in the pixel world.

But most thought-provoking is the aesthetic traits displayed by algorithmic performances, forming a curious complementarity with human performances. Human performances are full of randomness and life texture, while algorithms bring precision and infinite replicability. This two-way performance dialogue makes us realize: in the digital age, performing arts are no longer a human-exclusive territory but an open, trans-species aesthetic space. From virtual idols in the metaverse to experimental AI-created theatre, performing arts are undergoing unprecedented reconstruction and rebirth.

III. The Stage on the Tongue: Metaphorical Dialogue between Performance Styles and Sensory Experiences

In the vast realm of the senses, vision and hearing intertwine into the brilliance on the stage, while taste, often overlooked, can carve out a unique path in the interpretation of performance styles. When we savor a performance, our taste buds seem to transform into acute art critics, concretizing those indescribable artistic sensations into multi-layered experiences on the tongue.

  • Spicy Performance Style: Like a sharp dagger, it pierces the audience's heart. It disdains lukewarm emotional expressions and tears through the surface of life with intense tension. This style is akin to the spicy hot pot in Sichuan cuisine, initially overwhelming yet irresistibly addictive. Actors use exaggerated肢体 language and sharp dialogues to expose the inner turmoil and contradictions of characters. The audience experiences a kind of exhilarating pain under the spicy impact.
  • Sweet Performance: Like a meticulously brewed sugar water, it soothes the audience's soul with gentle gestures. This style leans towards restraint and delicacy, like a macaron in French pastry, layered yet harmonious. Every glance and microexpression of the actor is finely polished, narrating the warmth and beauty of the characters. This performance does not require dramatic emotional releases but flows subtly, allowing the audience to gradually feel the beauty of art.
  • Sour and Bitter Performance: Like tasting an aged red wine, it requires time's sedimentation and the patience of savoring. This style often reflects life's complexity and helplessness. Actors use restrained and patient performances to fully display those unspeakable pains and regrets. The sour and bitter performance is not just a direct presentation of sour emotions but a dialogue with time, allowing the audience to feel the depth and thickness of life through the act of tasting.

IV. The Future of Performance: The Convergence Point of Trans-Species Aesthetics

As we stand on the ruins of anthropocentrism and re-examine performance, a unique way of human cognition of the world, a grander picture is emerging: performance is no longer a human privilege but a universal mechanism for self-expression among different forms of existence in the universe. From animal instinctual displays to AI's precise calculations, from human emotional projections to quantum system's indeterminate fluctuations, although the forms of performance vary, their core spirit—self-validation through presentation—is the same.

This trans-species performance aesthetics not only breaks the anthropocentric understanding of "performativity" but also inspires us: human performing arts should embrace dialogue with other species and forms of existence in a more open and inclusive manner. In this sense, future performing arts will be a symphony composed jointly by humans, animals, and artificial intelligence: with the primitive rhythms of instinctual drives, precise algorithmic constructions, and the emotional warmth of life experiences.

Conclusion

The magical mirror of performing arts never fades; it reflects the past and foresees the future. In this mirror, we not only see the exquisiteness of art but also decode the era's secrets. From ancient Greek theatres to digital virtual stages, from animal survival displays to algorithmic performances, performing arts have always been a mirror of human spirit, reflecting our pursuit of beauty, exploration of truth, and self-awareness. This magical mirror, spanning time and species, will continue to witness every leap of human civilization, recording the eternal chapters where art and era intertwine.
