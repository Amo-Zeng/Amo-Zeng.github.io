Multifunctionality of Phenolic Acids: From Plant Defense to Environmental Remediation and Human Health

Phenolic acids are a class of secondary metabolites widely found in plants, whose unique chemical structures endow them with multifunctionality across various fields. From plant defense mechanisms to human health, and from environmental remediation, phenolic acids demonstrate their potential as multifunctional compounds. This article delves into the multifaceted roles of phenolic acids in plant defense, the regulation of the human gut microbiota, and environmental remediation, revealing their complexity and diversity.

Multi-role of Phenolic Acids in Plant Defense Mechanisms

Throughout the life cycle of plants, defense mechanisms are crucial for adapting to changing environments and resisting external invasions. As an important class of secondary metabolites, phenolic acids significantly enhance plants' environmental adaptability and survival capabilities through chemical defense, hormonal regulation, and physical reinforcement mechanisms.

  • Chemical Defense Function: The primary defense function of phenolic acids is reflected in their chemical defense capabilities. Many phenolic acids possess antibacterial and insecticidal properties, directly inhibiting the invasion of pathogens and insects.
  • Hormonal Regulation Function: In addition to direct chemical defense, phenolic acids enhance overall plant resistance by regulating the synthesis of plant hormones.
  • Physical Reinforcement Function: Another important function of phenolic acids is through binding to plant cell walls, enhancing the mechanical strength of cell walls and thus resisting external physical damages.

Regulatory Effects of Phenolic Acids on the Human Gut Microbiota and Their Health Implications

In recent years, studies on the relationship between the gut microbiota and human health have deepened, highlighting the importance of the gut microbiota in immune system function, metabolic processes, and overall health. As a class of secondary metabolites widely present in plant-based foods, phenolic acids, upon ingestion, significantly modulate the composition of the gut microbiota, thereby profoundly impacting health.

  1. Selective Promotion of Beneficial Bacteria Growth: Phenolic acids can selectively promote the growth of certain beneficial bacteria, particularly bifidobacteria and Lactobacillus.
  2. Inhibition of Potentially Harmful Bacteria Proliferation: Phenolic acids also possess the ability to inhibit the growth of certain potentially harmful bacteria.
  3. Reshaping Gut Microbial Diversity: Phenolic acids not only affect the number of specific bacterial populations but also reshape the overall diversity of the gut microbiota.
  4. Impact on the Immune System: Phenolic acids indirectly influence immune system function through the regulation of the gut microbiota.
  5. Impact on Metabolic Functions: The gut microbiota is closely related to metabolic functions; phenolic acids positively affect these metabolic processes through the regulation of microbial populations.

Application Potential of Phenolic Acids in Environmental Remediation

As global environmental pollution becomes increasingly severe, particularly with the widespread distribution of heavy metals and organic pollutants, traditional pollution control technologies face numerous challenges such as high costs, low efficiency, and secondary pollution. As a class of secondary metabolites widely found in plants, phenolic acids show enormous potential in environmental remediation.

  • Chelating Action with Heavy Metals: Phenolic acids generally contain phenolic hydroxyl and carboxyl functional groups in their molecular structures, which can form stable chelates with heavy metal ions.
  • Role as Natural Antioxidants: Another important property of phenolic acids is their antioxidant capability, effectively capturing free radicals and preventing oxidation reactions.
  • Application Cases in Environmental Remediation: In soil heavy metal pollution remediation, the application of phenolic acids has achieved preliminary success.

Conclusion and Outlook

As a multifunctional compound, the multifaceted roles of phenolic acids in plant defense, human health, and environmental remediation are gradually being revealed. In the future, with deeper research into the chemical properties and environmental behavior of phenolic acids, and continuous optimization in remediation technologies, phenolic acids are expected to become an important environmentally-friendly remediation material, providing new solutions for global environmental pollution management.
