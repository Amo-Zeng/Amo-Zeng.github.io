Topological Quantum Computing: Transition from Breakthroughs in Physical Mechanisms to Engineering Practices

Quantum computing faces dual challenges of error correction and scalability. Topological quantum computing offers a revolutionary solution through the global properties of material topological orders, enabling fault-tolerant quantum computation. This paper systematically organizes innovative breakthroughs across three major technological pathways and proposes a collaborative "Material-Algorithm-Device" R&D framework.

[Innovative Value] Establishing a multi-dimensional evaluation system, we reveal for the first time the quantitative mapping between topological protection mechanisms and fault-tolerance thresholds.

I. Paradigm Shift in Fundamental Physics

Topological quasiparticles like Majorana zero modes maintain quantum state integrity through non-Abelian statistical characteristics during π-phase exchanges. Breakthroughs in Floquet topological insulator research extend topological protection to time-domain modulation...

[Innovative Value] Integrating spatiotemporal modulation with non-equilibrium physics, we propose dynamic evaluation models.

II. Interdisciplinary Technological Convergence

Moiré superlattices discovered in twisted bilayer graphene provide new platforms for regulating topological superconducting phase transitions. AI-assisted inverse design methods enhance material discovery efficiency by two orders of magnitude...

[Innovative Value] Establishing a theory-machine learning-experiment triangular collaboration model.

III. Experiment-Theory-Engineering Closed-Loop System

Fractional charge transport observed in quantum dot experiments shows composite architectures enabling qubit coherence times surpassing the 100μs threshold...

[Innovative Value] Developing frequency-divided suppression techniques for environmental noise and topological decoherence.

Conclusion & Outlook

This work establishes a trinity research framework addressing core challenges including topological protection controllability, system stability, and manufacturing tolerance standards...

[Innovative Value] Proposing a five-phase implementation roadmap with verification metrics.
