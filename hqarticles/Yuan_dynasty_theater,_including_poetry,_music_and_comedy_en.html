Zaju Drama: Multidimensional Urban Cultural Writings and Modern Implications

Article Content

With the rapid development of artificial intelligence, Zaju Drama, as part of traditional culture, is being rediscovered and reinterpreted. This article explores its modern significance from three dimensions: intercultural writing, aesthetics of the body, and street culture, and compares it with cultural understanding through the lens of AI.

I. Intercultural Writing

Zaju Drama showcases a high degree of intercultural integration by blending the Chinese language with ethnic minority cultures. In the context of today's AI, this intercultural writing can be compared to multimodal, multilingual AI interfaces, offering inspiration for cultural understanding and generation.

II. Aesthetics of the Body

The performance of Zaju Drama is not only focused on language but also emphasizes gestures, expressions, and rhythm. This "aesthetics of the body" can today engage in a cross-temporal dialogue with AI-driven virtual performances, dance generation, and emotional computing technologies.

III. Street Culture

Rooted in urban life and close to the everyday experiences of common people, Zaju Drama reflects the aesthetics and values of the urban middle class. In the AI era, this characteristic of Zaju Drama reminds us to pay attention to how technology can return to a "human-centric" approach and serve the cultural needs of ordinary people.
