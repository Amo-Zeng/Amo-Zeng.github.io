I. The Magnetic Code of Life's Origin

In the chaotic early stages of life's emergence, Earth's magnetic field resembled a dynamically woven energy network. Recent experiments indicate that plasma plasmon oscillations triggered by geomagnetic storms can create nanoscale electric field gradients in molecular clouds, which may precisely and selectively catalyze the directional polymerization of nucleotide chains. II. The Fragile Balance of Modern Civilization

The probability of the global power grid system encountering a once-in-a-century super geomagnetic storm has risen from 0.1% during the 1859 Carrington Event to 12%, reflecting a delicate balance with the solar activity cycle and the thinning of Earth's magnetosphere. III. The Magnetic Rule of Galactic Ecology

The Hubble Telescope's revelation of galactic-scale "magnetic cocoons" has altered traditional star formation models. More astonishingly, the Chandra X-ray Observatory captured magnetic filamentary structures in the jet of the central black hole of the NGC 1275 galaxy, with energy density showing fractal correlations with dark matter distribution, providing a new dimension to the hypothesis of magnetic field-driven cosmic structure formation.

IV. The Cognitive Revolution of Civilization's Evolution

Humanity's understanding of magnetic storms itself is a history of cognitive boundary breakthroughs. From the 18th-century geomagnetic inclination meter to modern LHC magnetic storm simulators, technological advancements have allowed us to reconstruct the early Earth's plasma turbulence environment.
