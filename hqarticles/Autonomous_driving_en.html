Part One: Technological Revolution — The Paradigm Leap of "Perception"

The technological core of autonomous driving is a paradigm leap from "mechanical vision" to "cognitive understanding." The traditional reliance on stacking sensors (LiDAR + cameras + millimeter-wave radar) is giving way to a perception revolution driven by "world models." As pointed out in "The Paradigm Revolution of 'Perception'": early systems were like "blind men touching an elephant"—LiDAR outlined shapes, cameras recognized colors, radar detected distances, but they lacked an essential understanding of the physical world.

World Model: AI's "Common Sense Engine"

Waymo's Pathways model and Tesla's Occupancy Networks mark a turning point: they no longer simply identify objects but construct dynamic 4D spatiotemporal representations. Part Two: Social Revolution — The Game and Restructuring of the Traffic Commons

The solution isn't more complex rules but embedding "negotiation algorithms": Mercedes-Benz's DRIVE PILOT uses external displays to show "Please go first" or "I will proceed," mimicking human drivers' intent communication.
