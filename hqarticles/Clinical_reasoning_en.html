Post-Human Clinical Thinking: When Cyborg Patients Redefine Medical Boundaries

Introduction: Technological Evolution and Medical Cognition Subversion

In 2024, a Neuralink brain-computer interface recipient described a novel sensory experience: "I perceive Wi-Fi signals like seeing air currents." This liminal state between biological neurology and digital signals forced clinicians to confront a fundamental question: When the boundary between human and technology dissolves, by what logic should medicine define health and disease?

From Galen's humoral theory to evidence-based medicine, clinical thinking has always relied on the "natural human body" paradigm. The emergence of cyborg patients is shifting medicine from "defect repair" to "possibility negotiation" - demanding not just technological upgrades, but a radical cognitive revolution.
