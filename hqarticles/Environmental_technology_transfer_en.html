The Triple Paradox of Environmental Technology Transfer

Abstract

This paper constructs a Technology-Institution-Culture tridimensional framework to systematically analyze three contradictions in environmental technology transfer: technological generation gap, value chain lock-in effects, and cognitive friction barriers. Through case studies including China's photovoltaic poverty alleviation (2024 data update), African green hydrogen industry dynamics, and knowledge integration in Andean water temples, we propose governance pathways requiring simultaneous achievement of technological incremental adaptation, intellectual property model innovation, and cognitive system dialogue.

Keywords: Environmental technology transfer, Adaptive generation gap, Knowledge stickiness, Cognitive translation, Technology democratization, South-South cooperation
