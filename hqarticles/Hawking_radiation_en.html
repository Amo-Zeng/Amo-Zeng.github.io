I. The Physical Essence of Hawking Radiation and the Information Revolution

1. Gravitational Hijacking of Quantum Vacuum

The essence of Hawking radiation is the filtering of quantum fluctuations by curved spacetime: among the virtual particle-antiparticle pairs generated near the event horizon, negative-energy particles fall into the black hole causing mass loss (classical explanation). However, recent research suggests this process is more likely the distortion and re-radiation of quantum field modes by spacetime curvature (indirectly verified by 2018 Unruh effect experiments). Notably, the coexistence of Planck's constant ħ and gravitational constant G in the black hole temperature formula T=ħc³/(8πGMk) implies this is the first observable evidence of quantum gravity effects.

The conflict between information conservation law and black hole thermodynamics' second law was resolved within the AdS/CFT duality framework. In 2019, a Stanford team demonstrated through quantum simulation that black hole information might be encoded in the quantum entanglement phase of Hawking radiation via the "butterfly effect" (PRL 123, 141601). This discovery supports the core proposition of the holographic principle: three-dimensional spacetime events can be completely mapped onto a two-dimensional surface, with Hawking radiation serving as the dynamic output interface of this mapping.
