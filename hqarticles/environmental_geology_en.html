Comprehensive Strategies for Protecting Cultural Heritage, Urbanization, and Climate Change

Cultural heritage, urbanization processes, and climate change are three major challenges faced by society today. They not only influence each other but also shape our understanding of the future and the formulation of protection strategies. This article aims to explore how modern technology combined with traditional methods can develop strategies that protect cultural heritage, rationally develop urban underground spaces, and address geological hazards brought about by climate change.

I. The Interaction between Cultural Heritage and Geological Hazards

Cultural heritage is not just a testament to human history; it is also crucial for cultural identity and historical continuity. However, they often face threats from the natural environment, like earthquakes, landslides, and mudflows. These geological hazards can destroy centuries-old monuments in an instant and cause irreversible damage to sites and murals. Modern technologies such as satellite remote sensing, drone monitoring, and ground-penetrating radar provide new tools for heritage conservation, helping us to predict and assess the risks of geological hazards. At the same time, traditional preservation methods like using original materials for restoration, focusing on cultural transmission through craftsmanship, and environmental management ensure that restored buildings maintain consistency in appearance and structure, continuing the cultural legacy.

Response Strategies:

  • Comprehensive Risk Assessment: Combine modern monitoring technologies with traditional knowledge to identify the most vulnerable cultural heritage and formulate priority protection plans.
  • Early Warning Systems: Use modern technology to establish early warning systems, complemented by traditional warning signs like unusual animal behavior, to increase accuracy.
  • Restoration and Reinforcement: Employ modern materials and technologies for structural reinforcement during restoration, ensuring long-term stability while preserving traditional craftsmanship.
  • Public Education and Engagement: Through education and public engagement, raise awareness about cultural heritage conservation, encourage community participation, and enhance public responsibility and sense of belonging.
