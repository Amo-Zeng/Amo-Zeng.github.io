Philosophical and Sociological Dimensions of Live Streaming

Dual Philosophical Dimensions of the Live Streaming Phenomenon

Reconstruction at the Ontological Level

Sartre's "Existence precedes essence" dynamically extends to the construction of the host's identity: each live stream becomes an iterative experiment in self-definition, with algorithmic recommendations and audience feedback forming real-time shaping forces.

The digital transformation of Heidegger's concept of "Being-with": viewers construct a sense of virtual presence through bullet screen symbols, forming a technologically mediated emotional community.

Challenges and Innovations in Epistemology

The phenomenological "return to the things themselves" encounters media alienation: the "reality" displayed in live streams is essentially a performative reality encoded by technology.

Habermas's communicative rationality faces the threat of dimensionality reduction: the tipping mechanism deconstructs the ideal state of rational discourse but gives rise to a new dialogue logic where "traffic equals power."

Critical Observation Framework

  1. Agency of Technological Objects: From the perspective of Actor-network theory, live streaming platforms as actors execute the guiding function of the "Digital Socrates" through their recommendation algorithms, yet distort the open nature of dialogue.
  2. Quantification of Emotional Transactions: The emotional currency system composed of heart symbols and virtual gifts is redefining the economic model of intimate relationships. Does this transactional expression erode emotional authenticity?
  3. Illusion of Free Will: The seemingly open interactive interface is preset with strict rule frameworks. The immediate revelry of bullet screens cannot hide their programmed dance on the algorithmic racetrack. Is this freedom Kant's "governed freedom?"
