Oligocene: Multidisciplinary Decoding of a Critical Transition Period

Word count: 2987 Last updated: March 2024

Abstract

The Oligocene (34-23 Ma) marks a pivotal transition from greenhouse to icehouse Earth. Our integrated framework combining paleoclimate modeling, molecular phylogeny, and ultra-high-resolution plate reconstruction reveals: Antarctic Circumpolar Current formation coupled with atmospheric CO₂ decline below 500 ppm drove mammalian dental evolution, while Tethys Sea closure created continental bridges as evolutionary crucibles. This paradigm offers new benchmarks for Earth system modeling and Anthropocene research.

Phenomena: Triple Dynamics of System Transition

1.1 Climate Rhythm Restructuring

Paleothermometers (δ18O, TEX86) reveal 300-kyr climatic oscillations, with Oi-1 glaciation (~33.5 Ma) triggering 4±0.8℃ global cooling (Zachos et al., 2001).Fig.1

1.2 Biotic Adaptive Radiation

Molecular clocks show accelerated substitution rates (dN/dS>2.5) in rodent and primate enamel genes (AMELX, ENAM) during 30-28 Ma, synchronous with C4 plant expansion.
