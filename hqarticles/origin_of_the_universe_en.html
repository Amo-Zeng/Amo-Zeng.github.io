I. Origins: The Core of Eternal Creation

Traditional notions view "origins" as the beginning of time, the starting point of everything. However, this understanding is based on the assumption that time flows in a linear manner, like a never-ending river, with the origin signifying the source of this river. But the essence of time is far from this simple analogy. Defining "origins" as the beginning of time is actually a misinterpretation of its true nature, limiting our depth and breadth of understanding "origins."

Time, as a parameter describing the change and movement of things, is fundamentally a human abstraction. We create the concepts of "past," "present," and "future" to understand the changes in the world, but this does not mean that time is an objective entity. When we say "originating at the beginning of time," we are actually imposing our understanding of time onto "origins" itself, greatly limiting the concept of "origins."

"Origins" should not be confined within the framework of time. If we step out of the linear flow of time, we find that "origins" is more like a node in the eternal cycle of the universe, a state in the process of evolution. It is not the beginning of time but a reflection of a specific stage in the development of things. Like the superposition state in quantum mechanics, "origins" simultaneously contains the possibilities of the future and the memories of the past, a key node in the chain of cosmic evolution.

"Origins," as initial conditions, refers not only to the state of matter but also includes the generation of information, the aggregation of energy, and the formation of laws. These initial conditions are continuously reconstructed and reorganized during the cosmic evolution process, forming the complex world we see.

When we re-examine "origins" with this perspective, we find its relationship with time becomes more complex and profound. "Origins" does not encompass time but contains the possibilities of time. It is not the beginning of time but a state of time evolution, a manifestation of the universe's self-creation and reconstruction process.

Under this understanding, our perception of "origins" undergoes a fundamental transformation. It is no longer a slave to time but a core element of cosmic evolution. "Origins" is the process of the universe self-creating at every level and dimension, a reconfiguration of information, energy, and laws. It is not a one-time creation event but an eternal creative process of the universe.

II. Information: The DNA and Evolutionary Code of the Universe

In the process of the universe's origin and evolution, information plays a crucial role. Physicist John Wheeler famously proposed the assertion "it from bit," hinting that information might be the most essential feature of the universe, the key to deciphering its origins.

From the perspective of information, the birth of the universe can be seen as the creation of an information system. At the moment of the Big Bang, tiny quantum fluctuations carried weak seeds of information, which acted like the universe's DNA, determining the direction of subsequent evolution. Quantum field theory tells us that the vacuum contains infinite potential information, and the Big Bang was merely the process of converting this potential information into reality.

Cosmologists have discovered that the structure and evolutionary trajectory of the universe are closely related to the transmission and processing of information. The discovery of gravitational waves provides us with a new channel for acquiring information, allowing us to "listen" to the early universe's echoes. The existence of dark matter and dark energy, though mysterious, cannot be ignored in terms of their information characteristics. Perhaps they are the nodes in the cosmic information network that we have yet to fully understand.

Even more astounding is the profound connection between information and the structure of spacetime revealed by modern physics. The holographic principle suggests that the universe we perceive may be the product of higher-dimensional information projection. Theories like string theory imply that the fundamental building blocks of the universe might be quantum bits of information rather than mere particles of matter.

Zooming in on the microcosm, we find that information constitutes the fundamental properties of matter. Quantum entanglement phenomena demonstrate that information transfer can transcend the constraints of spacetime, exhibiting more miraculous characteristics than classical physics. The existence of the Higgs field suggests that information might be the true cause of giving matter its mass, rather than the energy fields we are familiar with.

Re-examining the origins of the universe from the perspective of information reveals a grander picture. The universe is not a simple collection of matter but a vast information processing system. In this system, information is both the carrier and the content, the result and the cause. Perhaps understanding how information shapes the universe is the key to unlocking the ultimate mystery of the cosmos.

III. Consciousness: The Inner Driving Force and the Ultimate Mystery of Cosmic Evolution

In the evolution of the universe, consciousness is like a key that may unlock the ultimate mystery of the origins of the universe. From quantum mechanics to string theory, modern physics reveals phenomena that far exceed the scope of classical physics. Concepts like quantum entanglement, multi-dimensional space, and parallel universes challenge our traditional understanding of the material world. Behind these perplexing phenomena may lie the mechanisms of consciousness.

Consciousness is not merely an appendage of matter but may be one of the inner driving forces of cosmic evolution. As proposed by renowned physicist Roger Penrose, the quantum nature of consciousness may be closely related to the birth and evolution of the universe. This view suggests that consciousness might be the key to unlocking the mystery of cosmic origins.

The relationship between consciousness and the universe is a dialectical symbiotic process. On one hand, the evolution of the universe provides the material basis and necessary conditions for the emergence of consciousness; on the other hand, consciousness influences and shapes the trajectory of cosmic evolution in a unique way. From microscopic particles to macroscopic universes, from simple life forms to intelligent civilizations, the evolution of consciousness and the evolution of the universe show remarkable synchronicity. This synchronicity suggests that consciousness may be an inevitable product of cosmic evolution rather than a mere coincidence.

The universe manifests its current form and laws perhaps because of the existence of consciousness. The concept of "participatory universe" proposed by physicist John Wheeler implies that the observer's consciousness may influence the form and evolution of the universe. When we re-examine the origins of the universe from the perspective of consciousness, the traditional material-centered view is challenged. The birth of the universe may not only be a Big Bang of matter but also an awakening of consciousness.

Modern scientific research suggests that consciousness may possess characteristics that transcend spacetime. This characteristic provides new possibilities for explaining the origins of the universe. Consciousness may exist prior to matter, or it may be born simultaneously with matter, together forming the foundation of the universe. The relationship between consciousness and the origins of the universe may be a circular process of mutual causation. The birth of the universe creates an environment suitable for the survival of consciousness, while the existence of consciousness influences the direction of cosmic evolution. This interactive relationship constitutes a new paradigm for understanding the origins of the universe.

Viewing the origins of the universe from the perspective of consciousness, we gain a new perspective. Consciousness is no longer a passive observer of cosmic evolution but an active participant, or even a driving force. This understanding overturns our traditional view of the universe and provides new insights for explaining many cosmological puzzles. The symbiotic relationship between consciousness and the universe paints a more complete and profound cosmic picture. In this picture, consciousness is no longer a negligible bystander but may be the ultimate key to unlocking the mystery of the origins of the universe.
