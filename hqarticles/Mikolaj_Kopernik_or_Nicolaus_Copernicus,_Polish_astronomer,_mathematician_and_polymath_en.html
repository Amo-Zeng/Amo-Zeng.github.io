Copernicus: A Dual Revolution in Science and Humanities

Copernicus and Humanism in the Renaissance Period

During the Renaissance, humanism emerged, emphasizing individual dignity, rational thinking, and critical analysis. Copernicus's "De Revolutionibus Orbium Coelestium" was not only a revolution in astronomy but also a concrete practice of humanism in the scientific field.

The Development of Copernicus's Theory and Scientific Methodology

Copernicus's heliocentrism was not an accidental inspiration but the result of long-term observation, critical thinking, and systematic research. His scientific methodology embodies rigorous logical reasoning, a focus on empirical data, and a challenge to authority.

Conflict between Science and Religion and Copernicus's Impact

Copernicus's theory stirred great upheaval in Europe at the time, laying the foundation for later astronomical development and profoundly expanding the boundaries of human cognition.

Conclusion

The connection between Copernicus and humanism during the Renaissance period is a paradigm of the intersection of science and humanities. His scientific methodology, like a revolutionary seed, was sown in the soil of science, ultimately nurturing the towering tree of modern science.
