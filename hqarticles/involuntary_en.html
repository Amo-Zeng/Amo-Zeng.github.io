The Power of the Unconscious: Hidden Manipulation from Machine Learning to Data Capitalism

We are living in an era dominated by unconscious forces. These forces do not originate from direct human intentions but rather through complex algorithms, data analysis, and quantum phenomena, silently shaping our future, thoughts, and behavior. From the "ghosts" in machine learning to the "invisible hand" of data capitalism, to the interdisciplinary dialogue between the unconscious and the quantum world, these seemingly independent fields are actually intertwined, collectively revealing the multidimensional aspects of unconscious power.

The "Ghost" in Machine Learning: Subtle Control Over the Future

Machine learning, as the core technology of artificial intelligence, is rapidly changing the face of the world. By training on massive amounts of data, it empowers machines with pattern recognition and predictive abilities, solving complex problems ranging from climate change to autonomous driving. However, this rapid development also brings an unsettling "ghost": the over-reliance on machine learning and excessive trust in AI systems may lead us to overlook the limitations of our own cognition and even relinquish control over our own destiny.

  • The "Black Box" of Decision-Making: The decision-making process of machine learning is often a "black box," where we cannot understand the specific mechanisms behind its conclusions.
  • Expansion of Autonomy: Machine learning systems may be granted increasing autonomy, participating in various aspects of social decision-making.
  • Bias Continuation: If the training data and algorithms contain bias, machine learning systems will inevitably perpetuate and even amplify these biases.

The "Invisible Hand" of Data Capitalism: Unconscious Power Manipulation

In the era of data capitalism, Adam Smith's "invisible hand" is operating in a new, unconscious way, silently transforming social power structures and individual behavior patterns. Traditional notions of power are usually associated with visible, conscious control, but in the age of data capitalism, a new form of power is emerging: unconscious power.

  1. Data Collection: Each of us is actively or passively contributing our own data, forming digital profiles of our behavioral patterns, interests, social relationships, and more.
  2. Data Analysis: Businesses use advanced algorithms to analyze data, uncovering hidden patterns and trends behind the data.
  3. Data Utilization: Using the results of data analysis, businesses can subtly manipulate our behaviors and decisions.

Echoes of the Unconscious: An Interdisciplinary Dialogue from Dreams to the Quantum World

At the intersection of science and spirituality, the power of the unconscious is also revealed through the wondrous resonance between dreams and the quantum world. Dreams have long been a window for humans to explore the unconscious world. In dreams, we free ourselves from the constraints of the physical laws of the real world, experiencing rich imagination beyond time and space. Interestingly, dream experiences have surprising similarities to superposition and entanglement states in quantum mechanics.

  • Superposition and the Complexity of the Dream World: In quantum mechanics, a particle can exist in multiple states simultaneously, collapsing into a definite state only when observed.
  • Entanglement and the Collective Unconscious: Quantum entanglement phenomena show that two or more particles can form a mysterious connection, with changes in one particle instantly affecting the other, no matter how far apart they are.
  • Consciousness and Quantum Observation: Some scientists and philosophers believe that there is a profound connection between consciousness and the quantum world, proposing the "quantum consciousness" hypothesis.

Conclusion: Redefining the Relationship Between Humans and Technology

Facing the multidimensional aspects of unconscious power, we need to maintain a clear mind, neither blindly optimistic nor overly cautious. First, we should strengthen the supervision and management of machine learning systems to ensure their transparency, fairness, and interpretability, avoiding misuse or negative impacts. Second, we need to build a more inclusive and diverse AI ethics system to ensure that the development of machine learning aligns with the values of human society. Most importantly, we must always maintain confidence in our own cognitive abilities, actively face challenges, continuously explore unknown fields, rather than leaving all problems to machines to solve.
