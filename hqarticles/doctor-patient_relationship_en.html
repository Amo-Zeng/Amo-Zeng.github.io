Reconstructing Doctor-Patient Relationships: From Theater Mode to Destiny Community

In contemporary medical scenarios, the doctor-patient relationship is often likened to a meticulously scripted drama: doctors as leads, strictly adhering to the "script" of professional authority; patients as supporting characters, passively accepting treatment plans. This "theater mode" ensures medical efficiency but also deepens the tension and estrangement between doctors and patients. With the rapid development of information technology, the digital wave sweeping the globe is profoundly transforming the healthcare industry. However, the existence of the digital divide makes it significantly different for various patient groups to access and utilize digital healthcare resources, further exacerbating doctor-patient conflicts. How to break the predicament of "theater mode," bridge the digital divide, and construct a new model of doctor-patient relationships has become an urgent issue to address.

The Dilemma of Doctor-Patient Relationships in Theater Mode

The theater theory offers a unique perspective: in medical interactions, both doctors and patients are "actors" participating in a pre-set drama. This pre-setting mainly stems from doctors' professional mindset—the authoritative role formed after specialized training, and patients' compliance expectations towards medical authority. Within this framework, fixed role-playing patterns form: doctors play the role of authority, while patients play the role of followers.

This role pre-setting brings two significant drawbacks:

  1. Mechanization of Doctors' Thinking: Doctors often simplify complex interpersonal interactions into technical operations, overlooking emotional needs and humanistic care in the medical process.
  2. Dissolution of Patient Subjectivity: Patients are forced to fully cede health decision-making authority to doctors, losing active participation in their own health.

These two tendencies not only weaken the essential value of healthcare—healthcare is not just a technical operation but also an organic combination of life care and self-management—but also intensify the estrangement between doctors and patients.

The Digital Divide Exacerbates Doctor-Patient Conflicts

Under the push of the digital wave, emerging technologies such as telemedicine, electronic medical records, and health management apps bring convenience to both doctors and patients. However, significant differences exist among patient groups of different ages, educational backgrounds, and economic levels in their ability to access and use digital healthcare resources. This "digital divide" further exacerbates doctor-patient conflicts, turning technology into a "barrier" rather than a "bridge."

  1. Imbalance in Information Access: Young patients with digital skills can easily access a wealth of healthcare information, while elderly patients may be trapped in "information silos," leading to information asymmetry between doctors and patients. Doctors may feel challenged by "information overload" from patients, while patients may suffer anxiety from excessive information.
  2. Varied Service Experiences: Convenient functions such as online booking, electronic payments, and intelligent guidance improve the medical experience for some patients, but those lacking digital skills may face difficulties in booking and long waiting times, further widening the gap in service experiences. Moreover, digital communication methods weaken emotional interactions between doctors and patients, reducing trust.

From Theater Mode to Destiny Community

To break the predicament of "theater mode" and bridge the digital divide, it is essential to reconstruct doctor-patient relationships, transforming them from a closed "actor performance system" to an open "co-creation stage." The core of this reconstruction lies in:

  1. Establishing Equal Dialogue Mechanisms: Allowing patients to fully express their needs, doctors are no longer unilateral authorities but listeners and collaborators.
  2. Cultivating Empathy in Doctors: Helping doctors understand individual differences among patients and respect their emotional needs.
  3. Building a Participatory Treatment Model: Integrating patients into the decision-making process of treatment, making them participants in shared decision-making.

In this new doctor-patient relationship model, doctors transition from authoritative "actors" to professional "directors," and patients transform from passive "actors" to active "participants." There are no fixed "scripts," only constantly evolving "plots"; no unilateral "performances," only co-created "art."

The concept of the "Doctor-Patient Community" offers new perspectives for this reconstruction. The "Doctor-Patient Community" refers to a new cooperative relationship established between doctors and patients based on shared values, interests, and goals. In this relationship, doctors are no longer lofty authorities, and patients are no longer passive recipients; both are equal partners striving for health maintenance together.

Creating a New Model of "Shared Destiny" in Doctor-Patient Relationships

To build a new model of "shared destiny" in doctor-patient relationships, the following aspects need to be addressed:

  1. Reshaping Value Concepts, Building Trust Foundations
    • Respecting Life, People-Centered: Doctors should always prioritize patients' life safety and physical health, while patients should understand the professional pressures and limitations of doctors, granting them full trust and respect.
    • Transparent Communication, Enhancing Understanding: Hospitals should establish sound information disclosure systems, and doctors should patiently listen to patients' concerns, explaining conditions in plain language, respecting patients' right to know and choose.
    • Integrity as the Foundation, Building Harmony: Doctors should uphold professional ethics, avoiding over-treatment and false advertising; patients should also comply with hospital regulations, jointly maintaining a good medical order.
  2. Optimizing Service Processes, Enhancing Patient Experiences
    • Streamlining Processes, Facilitating Medical Visits: Hospitals should optimize the medical process, implementing services such as appointment registration, electronic medical records, and self-service payments, reducing patient waiting times.
    • Paying Attention to Details, Demonstrating Humanistic Care: Hospitals should focus on details, providing comfortable medical environments, convenient services, and thoughtful guidance, making patients feel cared for and warm.
    • Strengthening Communication, Resolving Conflicts Promptly: Hospitals should establish sound doctor-patient communication mechanisms, promptly understanding patients' needs, properly handling disputes, and avoiding escalation of conflicts.
  3. Strengthening Doctor-Patient Cooperation, Jointly Combating Diseases
    • Promoting Health Knowledge, Enhancing Patients' Self-Management Abilities: Doctors should actively disseminate knowledge on disease prevention and healthy lifestyles, helping patients establish correct health concepts.
    • Encouraging Patient Participation, Jointly Formulating Treatment Plans: Doctors should respect patients' wishes, encouraging their involvement in the formulation of treatment plans, jointly selecting the most suitable treatment paths.
    • Strengthening Follow-Up Management, Consolidating Treatment Effects: Doctors should enhance follow-up management for patients, promptly understanding recovery conditions, guiding rehabilitation training, and consolidating treatment effects.
  4. Improving Institutional Safeguards, Promoting the Construction of Doctor-Patient Community
    • Strengthening Legal Regulations, Protecting the Rights of Both Sides: Governments should accelerate the improvement of healthcare legal regulations, clarifying the rights and obligations of both doctors and patients.
    • Improving Medical Dispute Resolution Mechanisms, Safeguarding Legal Rights: Governments should establish sound third-party mediation mechanisms, guiding both sides to resolve disputes through legal channels.
    • Enhancing Medical Ethics and Conduct, Improving Medical Staff's Professionalism: Governments and hospitals should strengthen medical ethics and conduct, improving the professional quality and service levels of medical staff.

Conclusion

The reconstruction of doctor-patient relationships is not only a technological innovation but also a return to the essence of humanistic healthcare. By breaking the shackles of "theater mode," bridging the digital divide, and constructing the "Doctor-Patient Community," we can hopefully realize a new model of doctor-patient relationships: doctors and patients are no longer in a relationship of authority and obedience but equal partners striving for health maintenance together. This new "shared destiny" model is not only an innovation in medical models but also a redefinition of medical values.

When we are no longer limited by the "script," both doctors and patients can truly become co-guardians of life and health, working together in closer cooperation to safeguard the health of the people.
